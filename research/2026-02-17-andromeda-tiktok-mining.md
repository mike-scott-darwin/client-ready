# Andromeda Update — TikTok Practitioner Mining

## One-Sentence Summary

Post-Andromeda, creative IS targeting — the algorithm uses your ad copy/visuals to find buyers, account-level learning means all campaigns feed one brain, and simplified structures (fewer campaigns, broader audiences) are winning.

## Key Findings

### 1. Account-Level Learning Is Real

**@thejeffjin (40.9K views, 2,394 likes, 360 shares):**
> "With Andromeda, your whole ad account learns from the performance of each different campaign. Andromeda has multiple brains — some focused on delivery priority, others on budget. Putting all ads in one campaign may seem beneficial, but it can hurt if your ads aren't good. Set up a separate testing campaign, but spend less than you normally would before Andromeda."

**Implication:** Your testing campaign at $50/ad set still matters, but account-level learning means the data from testing feeds your scaling campaigns faster. This is new — pre-Andromeda, campaigns were siloed.

### 2. Creative = Targeting (The Biggest Shift)

**@raisperhar (27.2K views, 1,612 likes, 187 shares):**
> "If you want Meta to show your ads to the exact customer & don't waste money on irrelevant impressions, you need to start mentioning your ideal customer or the exact problem in your primary text & creatives to give strong signal to Meta."

**@ecommerce.daily (32.1K views, 1,062 likes, 446 shares):**
> "AI now decides which ads get shown — not you. Creative variety beats targeting now."

**@ecommerce.daily (5K views):**
> "The new CBO + broad ad structure that wins in 2025. How to feed Facebook's AI the right data for cheaper CPMs."

**Implication:** This validates your angle-based ad strategy (Pain, Mechanism, Social Proof). Each angle naturally speaks to a different buyer — Andromeda uses that specificity to find the right person. No interest targeting needed. Be explicit: "If you're a coach who can't explain what you sell..." — the algorithm reads that.

### 3. Simplified Structures Winning

**@rickyhayesreal (1.1K views):**
> "One campaign, one ad set, one flexible ad — let Meta's AI do the rest. Run Advantage+ Sales, mix UGC/carousel/static, $25/day for 7 days."

**@carloskicksads (58K views):**
Multiple videos on "Andromeda friendly structures" and "2 high performing structures." Most prolific creator on this topic with 5 videos in the data set.

**Implication:** The trend is toward fewer, broader campaigns. But the ultra-simplified one-campaign approach is probably too aggressive for a new funnel without pixel data. The hybrid approach (ABO testing + CBO scaling) still applies — just simpler than before.

### 4. Creative Testing Changed

**@carloskicksads (24.6K views):**
> "Creative testing in Facebook ads post Andromeda update"

**@michal_digital_marketer (3.5K views):**
> "How to test Facebook ads creatives in 2026 with Meta Andromeda update"

**Implication:** Testing methodology needs updating for Andromeda. Account-level learning means you can run testing campaigns at lower budgets (the whole account benefits). Creative specificity matters more — vague ads get shown to vague audiences.

### 5. Specificity Over Broad Messaging

**@carloskicksads (4.9K views):**
> "Facebook ads require specificity post Andromeda for more sales"

**Implication:** Generic "make money online" ads will perform worse. Ads that say exactly who they're for and what problem they solve perform better because Andromeda uses that signal to match. This aligns with Client Ready's positioning — "coaches who can't explain what they sell."

# Personal Mastery Framework

**Source:** Personal Mastery-20260205181155.md (coaching program curriculum)

## Overview

A complete personal development + business design framework. Covers identity work, emotional mastery, ideal day design, and business vision creation. Useful as both coaching methodology and personal practice.

**Core premise:**
> "Success is 80% psychology and 20% mechanics" — Tony Robbins

---

## The Mastery Paradigm

### The Problem: Chasing Tactics
Most people look for external tactics, strategies, and funnels to get where they want to be. But:

> "If you do not have the business that you want you have not become the person who can run it yet" — Alex Charfen

### The Solution: Inside Out
Instead of changing from outside in, start from the core and go inside out by committing to mastery.

**The journey of "becoming":**
- Becoming a new version of yourself
- Breaking free of limitations, beliefs, emotions, paradigms
- See it as "becoming" more of who you truly are
- Leave "the old" behind, embrace "the new"

### The Divided Self
> "The tension between the two personas within us — one of our Authentic, private identity, and the other the false, 'sane' self that we present to the world"

## Part 1: Describing The Old Character

### Questions to surface current reality:

1. How would you describe yourself right now in three sentences or less?
2. Where do you currently live? How much money do you currently make and have?
3. How do you currently present yourself? What do you look like?
4. What are your good and bad habits? What things do you do repeatedly?
5. What are your strengths? (List 5)
6. What are your weaknesses? (List 5)
7. What negative things pop into your mind on a daily basis?
8. What was your childhood like?
9. What past events do you worry about or replay in your mind?
10. What things do you worry about in the future?
11. What things block you from doing what you want to do each day?
12. Who are you currently angry at? What do you blame?
13. Who do you currently judge and what do you judge them for?
14. What things do you love doing and could happily do everyday?
15. What things do you hate doing and never want to do ever?
16. What does the average day look like for you?
17. What are your character traits?
18. How do you imagine others gossip and talk about you?

## Part 2: Creating The New Character

### Questions to design desired identity:

1. How would you describe the new version of you necessary to effortlessly achieve all of your dreams and goals? (3 sentences)
2. What skills and strengths does this new character need?
3. What strengths does this new character have that your current one does not?
4. What weaknesses is this new character free of?
5. What good habits does this new character have?
6. What bad habits is this new character free of?
7. What fears is this new character free of?
8. How does this new character present him/herself?
9. What judgements is this new character free of?
10. What stories about the past is this new character free of?
11. What traits does this new character have?
12. Describe what the average day looks like for this new character
13. Describe what this new character loves doing that the current character hates
14. How would other people gossip and talk about this new character?

## Part 3: The Identity Gap

**Definition:** The journey from your current self to your desired self.

> "Before you can do something you must first be something." — Johann Wolfgang von Goethe

### Key insight:
- Depression/sadness = living in the past, replaying old stories
- Anxiety = living in the future, predicting outcomes before they happen

> "Our primal brains are wired to watch out for the 'negative' for protection so we tend to have negative visualizations of future outcomes."

### Collapsing Time
- When you experience the future — you're in the present
- When you experience the past — you were in the present
- There is only the present moment

> "You have a choice at every second who you want to be in this present moment"

## Part 5: Mastering Emotional State

### The principle:
> "What's wrong is always available, so is what's right" — Tony Robbins

We're hardwired to find what's wrong on autopilot → keeps creating same outcomes.

**The truth:**
> "We've been trained to FEEL a certain way, and we've done it so long we THINK it's how we ARE."

### How to shift emotional state:
1. **Your Emotions** — how you feel (happy/sad)
2. **Your Physiology** — physical body, posture, smiling/frowning, what you put into your body
3. **The meaning you associate** — remove the meaning to the problem = **Detachment**

## Part 11: Cultivating Flow State

### Steps to Flow:
1. **Sense of Purpose** — connect activity to larger meaning
2. **Clear Intention** — define specific intention for task
3. **Create Concentration** — water nearby, slightly hungry (fasted), avoid heavy carbs

### Remove Distractions:
- Wake up early
- Don't check phone first
- Close open loops in mind
- Use News Feed Eradicator (Chrome extension)
- Let friends/family know
- Go to new location
- Phone on airplane mode, in another room
- 2-3 hours+ blocked off

4. **Challenge-Skill Balance** — task should stretch skills just beyond their edge
5. **Focus** — fully concentrate, remove interruptions
6. **Embrace Loss of Self-Consciousness** — lose yourself, experience timelessness
7. **Embrace the Experience** — find intrinsic enjoyment, not just external rewards

## Part 12: The Art of Detachment

**Definition:**
> "Setting your intentions clearly and then releasing any attachment to the outcome (i.e., needing it to happen)"

### Three approaches to detachment:

**1. Focus on Becoming**
Don't need to achieve something to feel satisfied — focus on who you're becoming.

**2. Understand the Principle of Polarity**
Everything has equal and opposite. Each goal brings its own positives and negatives. Embody a state of neutrality.

**3. Go Inwards**
When feeling "needing" or "craving," ask:
- Why do I feel like I NEED this to go a certain way?
- Managing emotional state — why do I feel need or lack?
- Connecting to vision, why, and purpose
- Understanding your timeline and the universe's timeline may differ
- What lessons might I need to learn to level up?

## Part 13: Mission and Vision

### Developing Your Purpose:
1. Why do you do what you do?
2. How does your current state of being affect other people's lives?
3. How does what you do in business improve people's lives? (beyond the obvious)
4. What change would you like to cause in your world?
5. Why is this important to you? (at a heart and human level)
6. If everything happens for a purpose, what is the purpose for your life?

### Creating a Vision for Your LIFE:
1. What is your dream future reality?
2. How would this benefit others if accomplished?
3. Why is this important to you personally?
4. How would you capture this in a succinct way that inspires you?

### Creating a Vision for Your BUSINESS:
1. What big audacious goal would you love your business to achieve? (should excite AND scare you)
2. What would be the benefit to the world if you accomplished this?
3. Why should you and those around you dedicate their life's work to this?

### Creating a Moonshot Mission:
1. What did you want to be as a child?
2. Top 25 curiosities, interests, and passions
3. Current strengths, skills, and innate talents
4. Problems that piss you off in life and the world
5. If money were no object, what would you spend your days on?

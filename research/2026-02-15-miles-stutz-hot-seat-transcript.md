## Ad Performance Benchmarks

**Unique outbound CTR (the metric that matters):**
- Below 1% = ad needs improvement or targeting is wrong
- 1%+ = ad is working
- Exception: if you're making sales profitably, a lower CTR can be acceptable
- Worldwide targeting naturally comes with lower CTR than high-income country targeting

**Checkout page CVR benchmark:** 30% (from previous session, reinforced here)

**Where to find it in Ads Manager:** Customize Columns, toggle on "Unique Outbound Clicks" and "Unique Outbound CTR"

## Headline Formula (The 5-Second Test)

Miles shared a framework for evaluating any sales page before launch. Five questions you must be able to answer immediately:

- **Who is this for?** — Clear target audience visible instantly
- **Why am I here?** — The payoff/result they'll get
- **Why should I care?** — Unique differentiator (unique mechanism + without statements)
- **Why should I believe?** — Trust signals, testimonials, proof
- **What do I do next?** — Clear CTA

**Headline structure:** Action verb + unique mechanism + results + payoff + without X + guarantee

Example Miles gave: "Launch [unique mechanism] and turn 17-dollar buyers into 5-10K clients within seven days"

Sub-headline pattern: "The only system that [does X] in under [timeframe], [delivers Y] and automatically [does Z] without [pain point 1], [pain point 2] or [pain point 3]"

**Key language principle:** Sell shortcuts. The world is burned out, too much information, too much work. Use words like "AI-powered," "under an hour," "automatically." Give people the relief of spending less time.

## Funnel Structure (What Miles Recommends for Starting Out)

**Start simple. Do NOT build OTOs first:**
- Short-form sales page (above-the-fold messaging only to start)
- 3 order bumps
- Straight to thank you page
- That's it. Test this before adding OTOs.

Miles is spending 1,200/day on his snack with just above-the-fold copy. No long-form page needed to start.

**Order bump positioning:** Order bumps are SHORTCUTS to accomplishing the goal. Not extra content — speed.

Examples from the call:
- ADHD course bump: "We both know you have ADHD and you'll hate sitting through a 90-minute workshop. Here's the summary for 17 bucks."
- Dating confidence bump: "Listen to this audio before a date and be the most confident man she's ever seen."

**OTO structure (when ready):**
- OTO1 after purchase (instead of thank you page)
- If they decline OTO1, show downsell of OTO1
- If they decline/buy, show OTO2
- If they decline OTO2, show downsell of OTO2
- Max 2 OTOs with downsells
- Self-sorting options: like Mercedes vs AMG — same car, more features at higher price (47 vs 97)

## Digital Snack Pricing Strategy

**Miles' numbers:**
- 17-dollar snack: CPA is about 95 dollars
- 9-dollar no-phone offer: CPA is about 45 dollars

**The pricing logic:**
- Lower price = lower CPA = faster feedback loops = less ad spend to know if it works
- Higher price = higher AOV but higher CPA = need bigger war chest to test
- Sweet spot Miles found: below 10, preferably 9
- Nothing wrong with going higher — math balances out (higher AOV offsets higher CPA)
- Start low, increase over time: 9 to 15 to 27 to 47

**If an OTO converts at 20%, test raising the price.** One member had a 7-dollar OTO converting at 20%. Miles said test it at 47. If it still converts, try 67, then 97. Stop when diminishing returns hit.

**Multiple snacks strategy:** Each digital snack addresses ONE angle and ONE payoff. To scale, create more snacks targeting different pain points in the same market. This lets you saturate more of your market vs. spending more on one angle.

## Ad Testing Structure (Miles' Exact Setup)

**Testing campaigns (ABO — Ad Set Budget Optimization):**
- One campaign with different ad sets inside
- Each ad set has 8-10 ads
- Creative mix: 2-3 B-roll videos with overlay text + 2-3 static images
- Each creative gets one short-form copy and one long-form copy (= 8-10 ads total)
- Budget: 50/day per ad set
- Launch about 3 new ad sets per week
- Scale winners by 20% every 48 hours

**Scale campaigns (CBO/ASC — Advantage Sales Campaign):**
- Pick highest converting ads monthly
- Move into a CBO with one ad set, 5-6 winning ads inside
- Budget: 100/day to start
- Scale by 20% every 48 hours when profitable
- ASC requires one ad set to work

**Targeting:**
- Always have one ad set worldwide
- Always have one ad set with high-income countries only: US, UK, Canada, Germany
- Australia and New Zealand optional (Miles doesn't love them for targeting)

**Creative guidance:**
- B-roll with overlay text is performing very well
- Static images with direct headlines
- Headlines should be "grabby" — look at YouTube thumbnails for inspiration (they clickbait)
- One short-form and one long-form copy per creative

## Mid-Funnel Strategy (Branding Campaigns)

This was the biggest tactical insight from the call. Miles says this is the single best thing he's done for mid-funnel.

**Setup:**
- Engagement campaign on Meta
- Conversion location: video views (through-play)
- 5-10 videos, 90 seconds to 3 minutes each
- Head-talking videos from Instagram (existing content is fine)
- Budget: 5/day per ad (can go as low as 3/day)
- Take post ID from Instagram — no special creative needed

**Who to target:** Start with retargeting all existing audiences. Then expand to cold.

**Miles' results:**
- Retargeting audience: 40,000 people
- Spending 250/day on retargeting PROFITABLY (just selling the low-ticket snack)
- Frequency of 6 — people see his face constantly
- Gets DMs from strangers saying "I see you everywhere"
- Started getting install (high-ticket) sales from DMs — people he'd never interacted with
- No phone calls needed — just branding ads driving awareness into existing funnel

**Why it works:** Forces your buyers and prospects to watch your content without you needing to go viral on Instagram. Creates familiarity and trust at scale. Replaces the "post and pray" content strategy.

**The formula:** Low-ticket ads (acquisition) + branding ads (mid-funnel) + email (nurture) + Instagram profile with sales post (conversion)

## Miscellaneous Tactical Notes

**On AI (OpenAI Claw/Claude):** Miles opened the call talking about AI agents. He has a dedicated Mac Mini for AI with its own Gmail account, no access to personal credentials. Security-conscious setup. Says this is bigger than ChatGPT and is geeking out on it.

**On GHL vs other platforms:** Strongly recommends GHL over Kajabi for funnels. Kajabi is for hosting products, not for building funnels. Offered a member a free GHL sub-account because their Kajabi page was hurting conversions despite 500 sales.

**On funnel design:** Clean, professional design matters. One member had 500 sales but a messy page. Miles showed them his template (BSLP — Beautiful Simple Landing Page) and said design alone was leaving money on the table.

**On language specificity:** Don't sell the deep transformation in the snack. Sell the surface-level problem they're aware of. Example: "Don't sell nervous system regulation — sell confidence on a date." The deep work happens in the high-ticket offer.

**Common mistake:** Running ads to the wrong URL. One member spent 250 dollars driving traffic to an old page because the Meta ad had the wrong website URL. Always verify.

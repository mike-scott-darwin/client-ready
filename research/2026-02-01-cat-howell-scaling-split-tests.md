# Cat Howell: Scaling Advice + Split Test Findings

**Source:** Multiple Facebook posts from Catherine Howell (Admin, All-star contributor)

---

## 1. Reducing CPA at Scale

**Context:** Omon doing $80k-$100k/mo, $600/day ad spend, $100 CPA

### Cat's Advice

**Hyper-segment your ad copy and sales page:**
> "The best way to reduce your CPA at scale is to hyper-segment your ad copy and sales page (ex: instead of selling a course on ads to low ticket offers, position it as 'ads to sell $17 ebooks')."

**Target more unaware audiences:**
> "Build out your existing sales page and creatives to they speak to more unaware segment of the audience - as CPA creep is usually because Facebook has depleted the aware segment of the audience."

**$100 CPA is normal:**
> "A $100 CPA is quite normal. If your funnel converts at 2% and you are getting $2 link clicks then that's a $100 CPA."

**The math:**
- $2 CPC ÷ 2% conversion = $100 CPA
- Fix: Increase conversion rate OR decrease CPC
- "If you have a healthy AOV then this won't be an issue"

## 2. Split Test: More Order Bumps = Higher Everything

### Finding
> "The order form with the most order bumps has a higher conversion rate + higher earnings per click ($3 difference is huge!)."

### The Data

| Metric | Control (fewer bumps) | Variation (3 bumps) | Change |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------|--------|
| Page views | 445 | 519 | +17% |
| Opt-ins | 23 | 52 | +126% |
| **Opt-in rate** | 5.17% | **10.02%** | **+94%** |
| Sales | 22 | 49 | +123% |
| **Sales rate** | 4.94% | **9.44%** | **+91%** |
| **Earnings/page view** | $3.39 | **$6.10** | **+80%** |

### Key Insight
More order bumps nearly **DOUBLED** conversion rates and earnings per page view.

### Cat's Caveat
> "If you are brand new with your offer - don't get overwhelmed and think you need 3 order bumps to launch. Go live with your minimum viable funnel and then add on as you go."

---

## 3. Split Test: Video vs No Video

### Finding
> "I ran a split test on my main sales page - one with video, one without. The one without the video has worked best. I for sure thought the video would do better."

### Why It Might Have Lost
> "People watching the video may be closing the pop up sooner"

### Takeaway
Test everything. Assumptions are often wrong.

## 4. Testing vs Scaling Campaign Performance

**Problem:** Great results in testing campaign (ROAS 3-7), terrible in scaling campaign (ROAS 0.4-0.6)

### Cat's Advice
> "No not doing anything wrong - not all ads will perform in the scaling campaign as they did in the testing one as these are separate campaigns with different learning optimisations."

**Rule of thumb:**
> "Give it a minute - if it persists at $150 CPA after a couple sales then ax it (keep it running in your ABO testing campaign though)."

### Key Insight
- ABO testing campaigns and CBO scaling campaigns have different learning
- Ads won't always perform the same when moved
- Keep winners running in testing while you test in scaling

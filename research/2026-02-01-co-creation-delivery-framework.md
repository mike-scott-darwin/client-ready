# Co-Creation Offer - Delivery Framework Analysis

**Source:** Co-Creation Offer PDF (Tom Noske / Scale Academy)

---

## What This Document Contains

A comprehensive coaching program delivery framework with 4 pillars:
1. Leadership (your presence as the product)
2. Retention Psychology (emotional vs results-based)
3. Client Roadmap (clarity over content volume)
4. Integration & Implementation (tools that drive action)

Plus bonus sections:
- AI-Powered Course Creation System (prompts for offer extraction)
- OfferSharp methodology (niche-down + outcome focus)

---

## The 4 Pillars Summary

### 1. Leadership

**Core insight:** Clients buy how it *feels* to be around you. Your energy sets the tone more than any framework.

Key principles:
- **Presence is the product** — rushed/distracted energy leaks into everything
- **Protect capacity like profit** — your nervous system = your business
- **Clarity over control** — you don't need to fix everything, just lead clearly
- **Be what you want to see** — complexity is contagious, so is calm
- **Fixing isn't leading** — hold space without managing every emotion

### 2. Retention Psychology

**Core insight:** Clients don't stay for results alone. They stay for how they *feel* in your space.

Why clients leave:
- Disconnected from you
- Lost in the group
- Forgotten or invisible
- Like they're not keeping up
- Like their presence doesn't matter

**The goal:** "I feel like I belong here." "This program sees me."

Tactical systems:
- **Strong onboarding** (welcome video, form, clear first steps, 7-10 day flow)
- **Engagement tracking** (Green/Orange/Red status by activity)
- **Client engagement calendar** (weekly prompts, monthly focus questions)
- **Client database** (name, stage, goal, recent win/stuck point, engagement status)
- **Crew pods** (5-10 clients grouped by stage)

### 3. Client Roadmap

**Core insight:** Clients don't need more content. They need clarity.

Programs fall apart because the path is missing, not because content is bad.

Clients need to know:
- Where they are in the journey
- What comes next
- What they can safely ignore right now

**Implementation:**
1. Map 5-8 core milestones (outcome-based phases, not topics)
2. Create targeted info for each milestone (Looms, checklists, 1-pagers — not bloated courses)
3. Help clients self-identify their stage (intake form, quiz, or GPT)
4. Build visual roadmap as the anchor for everything

### 4. Integration & Implementation

**Core insight:** Clarity and connection matter, but without implementation, clients stall.

Clients should always know:
- What to do next
- When to do it
- Where to find what they need

Tactical systems:
- **Baseline support structure** (1:1 vs group vs hybrid, DM access, boundaries)
- **Tools aligned to roadmap** (each asset maps to a milestone)
- **Co-creation moments** (live workshops, hot seats at specific milestones)
- **Client implementation dashboard** (track where they are, see what's next, find resources)
- **Automated accountability** (email/GPT check-ins between calls)
- **Experience-level adjustments** (beginners get more hand-holding, advanced get tool access)

## Offer Creation System (AI-Powered)

A 3-stage process:

1. **Niche Research & Offer Analysis** — Find successful offers in your space, analyze their components
2. **Knowledge Extraction** — Deep-dive into your expertise, ideal customer, results, credibility
3. **Offer Synthesis** — Combine market-proven structure with your unique knowledge (80% proven, 20% you)

---

## OfferSharp Methodology

A niche-down process to find ONE clear, specific, measurable outcome:

1. Confirm general niche (1-3 words)
2. Provide 10-20 subcategories, pick one
3. Drill down until outcome is numerical + time-bound
4. Optional avatar lock (specific audience filter)
5. Continuum check (smaller/faster vs bigger/longer variations)
6. Suggest packaging (Challenge, Mastermind, Course, High-Ticket, etc.)

**Key principle:** Start with OUTCOME first, not the product.

The Co-Creation framework is built for *group coaching programs* with retention as a KPI. Your current ladder has two distinct delivery challenges:

### For Skool Community

With Co-Creation thinking:

- **Crew pods** — 5-10 people grouped by stage or offer type

## Decisions to Make

1. **Is delivery infrastructure a priority right now?** You have no testimonials yet. Getting wins might matter more than optimizing retention.

2. **If yes, where first?** Sprint ($297) is the most leveraged place — group format, ongoing, creates case studies.

3. **What's the minimum viable delivery upgrade?**
   - Milestone-based visual roadmap
   - Onboarding sequence (Day 1-7)
   - Weekly engagement rhythm in Skool

4. **What requires more infrastructure?**
   - Engagement tracking (Notion/Airtable)
   - Crew pods (manual grouping)
   - Automated check-ins (email or GPT)

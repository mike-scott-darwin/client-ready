# Front-End Pricing Research: $17 vs $27

## Research Question

Should the Client Ready front-end be priced at $17 or $27 to maximize conversions and overall funnel health?

## Current State

| Element | Current | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Front-end | $27 | Client Ready Offer System |
| Bump 1 | $47 | 24-Hour Launch Kit |
| Bump 2 | $37 | Plug & Play Funnel Pack |
| Max checkout cart | $111 | Before OTOs |
| Target AOV | $100-180 | Including OTOs |

## Key Findings

### 1. Both $17 and $27 Are Proven Sweet Spots

Industry data shows $7, $17, $27, and $47 are all "sweet spot" prices for low-ticket digital offers. Neither is universally superior—it depends on backend structure and traffic source.

### 2. The $10 Difference Changes Buyer Quality

**$17 buyers:**
- More impulse purchases
- Higher volume
- Better for building large buyer lists fast
- May be less qualified for high-ticket backend
- "A $7 buyers list is about as effective as a freebie list" for $300+ backend products

**$27 buyers:**
- More considered purchase (still impulse, but higher commitment)
- Lower volume, higher quality signal
- Better suited for $5K+ backend (your goal)
- Filters out tire-kickers earlier

### 3. AOV Math Changes Everything

Current at $27:
- If 30% bump rate: $27 + $25.20 (avg bump) = ~$52 before OTO
- If 15% OTO1 rate at $297: adds ~$44.50 avg
- Estimated AOV: $96-110

At $17:
- Higher conversion, but lower starting point
- Same bump rates: $17 + $25.20 = ~$42 before OTO
- Need ~60% more front-end volume to match revenue
- May attract buyers who never scale to OTO/backend

### 4. The Real Lever: Offer Stack Perception

The research suggests the $10 difference matters less than:
- **Stack perception**: Does it feel like a steal?
- **Bundle psychology**: "Stack it. Add bonuses that complement the core offer to make your buyer feel like they're getting VIP access for a latte's worth of cash."
- **Price anchoring**: Striking out ~~$497~~ → $47 matters more than $17 vs $27

### 5. Affiliate Consideration

If affiliates are part of the plan:
- $17 front-end = lower commissions, need higher volume
- $27 front-end = better for affiliate incentives
- Some marketers price at $17 for own list, $27+ for affiliates

## Three Options to Consider

### Option A: Stay at $27 (Recommended for Current Stage)

**Rationale:** You're targeting coaches who can pay $5K+ high-ticket. A $27 buyer is more qualified than a $17 buyer. Focus on improving conversion rate through copy/offer, not dropping price.

**Actions:**
- Improve sales page conversion (target >2.5%)
- Strengthen order bump copy (target 30%+ take rate)
- Track qualification rate to high-ticket calls

### Option B: Drop to $17, Increase Bumps

**Rationale:** Maximize front-end volume, make money on bumps.

**New structure:**
- Front-end: $17
- Bump 1: $57 (increase from $47)
- Bump 2: $47 (increase from $37)
- Max checkout: $121 (higher than current $111)

**Risk:** More buyers, but may attract less qualified leads for $5K backend.

### Option C: Test Both with Split

**Rationale:** Let data decide.

**Setup:**
- Traffic source A → $17 landing page
- Traffic source B → $27 landing page
- Track: CVR, AOV, OTO rate, and qualification-to-call rate
- Run for 100+ sales each before deciding

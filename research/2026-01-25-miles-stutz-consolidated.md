## Key Findings (Priority Order)

### 1. Frame Recurring as "Sprint"
**Source:** Jan 20 call

Instead of "$37/mo (cancel anytime)" → "4-Week Sprint for $297"

- Backend: Still a subscription
- Email: Notify before next charge
- Psychology: One-time commitment feels safer

**Two-Option OTO Structure:**
| Option | Price | Frame |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Quick Win | $97 | "1 week access" |
| Full Sprint | $297 | "4 weeks to achieve X result" |

### 2. $97 Order Bump = 47% Pickup
**Source:** Dec 2 call

A "done-for-you snack idea service" at $97 converted 47% of buyers.

**Rule:** If bump conversion >40%, test higher prices.

### 3. OTO Pricing Framework
**Source:** Dec 2, Dec 23 calls

| OTO | Price Range | Positioning |
|-----|-------------|-------------|
| OTO1 | $0–$200 | Done-with-you (tools, templates) |
| OTO2 | $200–$400 | Done-for-you (services, blueprints) |

**Sequencing:** Each OTO solves the NEXT problem after purchase.

### 4. $300 Validation Test
**Source:** Jan 13 call

Before building a full funnel:
1. Create minimal snack offer
2. Build simple landing page
3. Run $300 in ads over 3-7 days
4. Target: 2-3 sales
5. If validated → scale to $300-400/day at 0.8-1.2 ROAS
6. If no sales → kill it, test next

### 5. Order Bumps = Energy Reducers
**Source:** Jan 13 call

Bumps should REDUCE effort to get results, NOT add more work.

| Good Bumps | Bad Bumps |
|------------|-----------|
| Templates | More courses |
| AI tools | More reading |
| Done-for-you audits | More modules |
| Swipe files | More complexity |

### 6. Catalog Model > Single Offer
**Source:** Dec 2, Dec 23 calls

Single flagship = 1-3% of market
Digital Snacks = capture the other 97%

| DS Type | Purpose | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Clarity | Understand the problem | Assessments, roadmaps |
| Plan | Create a plan | Frameworks, blueprints |
| Execution | Tools for action | Templates, scripts |

### 7. No Phone Offer (NPO) Structure
**Source:** Dec 23 call

Sell high-ticket without calls:

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Specific Goal + Timeline | "Launch in 30 days" not "feel confident" |
| Milestone Roadmap | Week 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 |
| Deliverables | Done-with-you > Done-for-you |

**Pricing Options:**
- PIF: Unscalable bonus (extra 1-on-1)
- Payment Plan: Justify with specific expense
- Weekly/Monthly: Low entry, upsell on first call

### 8. Don't Gate Low-Ticket Snacks
**Source:** Dec 2 call

Consumption > Protection
- More touchpoints = more trust
- Simpler tech stack
- Leads to higher-ticket sales

### 9. Email Sequence: Lead to Limitations
**Source:** Jan 20 call

**Problem:** Automated emails not booking calls.

**Fix:** Lead customers to the LIMITATIONS of their purchase.

| Email | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| 1-3 | Deliver value, help them use the product |
| 4-5 | Surface the limitation — "Now you need X, here's where most get stuck..." |
| 6-7 | Position the call as the solution |

**Target:** 3-5% call booking rate from openers.

### 10. Your CPA is Good
**Source:** Jan 20 call

Miles's benchmark: $97 CPA on $17 product
Your CPA: €45-50 on €29 product

You're outperforming.

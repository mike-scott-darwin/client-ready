## Miles' Feedback

### 1. Use Outbound Clicks as Denominator
> "Your opt-in rate is actually 3.26%. Always take outbound clicks from FB."

**Why:** Page views include refreshes, bots, etc. Outbound clicks = real humans who clicked your ad.

### 2. 3.26% Opt-in is "Okay"
> "This opt-in rate is okay considering it's a short form funnel."

**Benchmark:** ~3% opt-in rate is acceptable for short-form funnels.

### 3. 10% Sales Page Conversion is Low
> "Only a 10% conversion rate on sales page is low."

**How to fix:**
- Option A: Test the headline
- Option B: Add more context — "who it is for, pay off & PROOF it works"

### 4. Consider Unchecking Order Bumps
> "Consider unchecking order bumps if selected & add more proof to check out page"

**Why:** Pre-checked bumps can hurt conversion if people feel tricked. Adding proof to checkout page can offset this.

## Application to Client Ready

### Your Funnel Stages
1. **Ad → Landing page click** (CTR)
2. **Landing page → Checkout** (opt-in/conversion rate)
3. **Checkout → Purchase** (sales page conversion)
4. **Purchase → Bump take** (bump conversion)

### What to Track
- **Outbound clicks** from Meta (not page views)
- **Opt-in rate** = opt-ins / outbound clicks
- **Sales conversion** = purchases / opt-ins
- **Bump take rate** = bump purchases / orders
- **AOV** = total revenue / orders

### If Conversion is Low, Test:
1. **Headline** — biggest impact
2. **Context** — who it's for, payoff, proof
3. **Pre-checked bumps** — may help or hurt depending on audience sophistication
4. **Proof on checkout page** — testimonials, guarantees

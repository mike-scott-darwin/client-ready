# Cat Howell — Hot Seat Call (Feb 9, 2026)

**Source:** Live Profit Lab hot seat call transcript. Cat + members (Sherry, Jacob, Jen/Flor, Carolyn/Sherilyn).

## Key Findings

### 1. Four Scaling Strategies (Cat's Full Framework)

Cat laid out 4 distinct scaling strategies — this is the first time the complete framework appeared in one place:

| Strategy | What | When | Who Should Use |
|----------|------|------|----------------|
| **Multiple Entry Points** | Multiple front-end offers in same CBO campaign | First — easiest, highest converting | Anyone with 2+ offers or upsells that can be flipped to front-end |
| **Unaware Messaging** | Long-form sales pages + copy for unaware audience segment | Second — when entry points aren't enough | Everyone eventually, but especially saturated niches |
| **Hyper-Nicheing** | Same offer, messaging tailored to micro-segments of your audience | Advanced | Broad audiences (e.g., "coaches" → "agency owners who want low-ticket") |
| **Avatar Expansion** | Same offer, marketed to adjacent audiences | Advanced | When your core audience is tapped (e.g., lash artists → hairdressers) |

**Fifth strategy (enterprise only):** Proctor & Gamble model — same product under different brands. Not relevant for solo operators.

### 2. Multiple Entry Points — The Core Scaling Insight

**This is Cat's #1 recommended scaling strategy.** More important than unaware messaging.

**The grocery store analogy:**
> "Imagine you're a grocery store and you start selling bananas. That's all you sell. You attract people who want bananas. But not everyone likes bananas. So you start to offer apples and peaches and soy milk. With each SKU, you get to attract more people."

**Why it works:**
- Each new front-end offer lets Facebook hit the AWARE segment of a new audience slice
- Aware audience converts cheap ($20-50 CPA) — you keep hitting the easy buyers
- Delays the need to crack unaware audiences
- Facebook retargets across offers within the same campaign

**Cat's current setup (4 entry points):**
1. Ads training ($47)
2. 10-Day Sales Copy Challenge ($27)
3. Low Ticket Codex ($88)
4. Profit Lab membership ($111/month)

**Result:** Adding entries 3 and 4 brought her CPA back down after it spiked to $100/sale at scale.

**Critical tactical detail — all in ONE campaign:**
> "You should only have one ad set. If you're doing CBO. All those four different offers that I'm sending out to, they're sitting in the same ad set."

**Why one ad set (not separate):**
- Multiple ad sets = auction overlap even within CBO
- One ad set = Facebook has full control to retarget across offers
- Someone who doesn't want the $88 product sees the $27 product in the same campaign

### 3. Campaign Architecture (Cat's Actual Setup, Feb 2026)

| Campaign | Type | Purpose | Budget |
|----------|------|---------|--------|
| CBO Main | CBO, single ad set | All validated creatives + all 4 offers | Bulk of spend ($1000/day) |
| ABO Tester | ABO, $15/ad set | Test new creatives before graduating to CBO | $15/ad set |
| Engagement | Separate | Omnipresence (see previous research) | $3-25/day |
| Followers | Separate | Brand awareness | Small |

**Graduation protocol:**
1. Test creative in ABO at $15/day
2. Wait for 3-5 sales
3. If positive ROAS → move to CBO (use Post ID to keep social proof)
4. Keep the ABO ad running too ("if it's working, don't fuck with it")

**Why graduate before adding to CBO:**
> "When you start spending $1000 on those CBOs and you put an ad in there that you don't know if it works... Facebook will typically feed a lot of budget to new ads. It will send a lot of your spend towards it."

### 4. Pricing & AOV Deep Dive (Cat's Real Numbers)

**Cat's January 2026:**
- Revenue: $80,000
- Ad spend: $30,000
- Revenue from ads directly: $40,000 (ROAS ~1.3)
- Revenue from backend (library, email sequences): $40,000
- Net profit: $52,000

**The backend revelation:**
> "Once you acquire the customer, it's pure profit. You're selling a digital product. If they buy anything else from you, there's no cost of goods sold."

**Cat's AOV currently:** $140
- Was getting $20-30 CPA in first 2 months (brand + aware audience)
- January scaled to $1000/day → CPA jumped to $100
- Still profitable because AOV ($140) > CPA ($100)
- Adding 2 more entry points brought CPA back down

**Price point testing:**
- $17 front-end: "Completely killed my AOV. Went to $70-80." More sales but worse buyers who don't buy bumps/upsells
- $47 front-end: Sweet spot for her audience. "People paying $47 tend to buy everything"
- Different audiences → different optimal price points. Test, don't copy.

**Order bump split test (live, 1 week of data):**
| Version | Bumps | Conversion Rate |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| A | $17, $33, $55 | 2.3% |
| B | $33, $44, $55 | **4.0%** |

Higher-priced bumps are converting better. Still early data but promising.

**Cat's next move:** Raise upsell from $66 to $127. Goal: get AOV to $200.

### 5. The $2,000 Learning Phase Reality

**New insight — quantified for the first time:**

> "If you launch a new campaign with 10 ad creatives at $100/day, Facebook is gonna spread that budget. For the first $2,000 of spend, it won't look profitable."

**The math:**
- 2% conversion, $3 CPC = need 33 clicks = ~$100 per sale
- 10 ads × ~$100 each to get meaningful data = ~$1,000-2,000
- Before $2,000 total spend, most individual ads haven't had enough traffic

**Why people quit scaling:**
> "They start to up their budget and then they're spending twice as much but getting the same amount of sales. Three, four days in, they panic and turn everything off. That's so normal. This is why I say AOV AOV AOV."

**Every budget increase triggers a new learning phase (~7 days).**

### 6. Data & Decision Windows

| Window | Use For | Don't Use For |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| 3-day | Spot budget-eating duds (one ad spending $300, zero sales) | Campaign-level decisions |
| 7-day | Campaign performance trends | Nothing less than this for scaling decisions |
| 14-30 day | True performance baseline | N/A |
| 24-hour | **Never make decisions on this** | Everything |

> "I do not make decisions in my ad account based off a 24 hour window. You will go mentally crazy if you do that to yourself."

### 7. Conversion Rate Benchmarks (Funnel, Not Ads)

| Metric | Minimum | Target | Context |
|--------|---------|--------|---------|
| Funnel conversion (cold) | 2% | 3-5% | Below 2% = messaging problem, not traffic problem |
| Funnel conversion (warm) | N/A | 10-15% | Warm doesn't need sales page, just order form |
| Challenge → core offer (live) | 10% | 17%+ | Jen got 17% on live challenge backend |
| Challenge → core offer (evergreen) | Lower | TBD | Will drop from live rates |
| Cat's overall funnel | — | 5% | Across all offers, all traffic |

### 8. CBO vs ABO Clarification

**Cat runs BOTH simultaneously.** Not either/or.

- **CBO:** All validated creatives. Bulk spend. One ad set. All offers.
- **ABO:** $15/ad set testing ground. Each ad set = one creative being tested.

**ABO is NOT "old way" — it's the testing lane.** Previous research implied ABO was deprecated. Cat explicitly still uses it:
> "When I'm testing ad creatives in my ad set budget campaign, if they work, I keep 'em going. They're working, they're giving you ROAS. I keep 'em in there."

### 9. Kill Criteria for Individual Ads

From Jacob's hot seat:

| Spend vs CPA | Action |
|--------------|--------|
| One sale at expected CPA (e.g., $100 spend, $47 sale with $150 AOV) | Keep running — one data point isn't enough |
| 0.52 ROAS across 2 sales, CPA nearly 2x target | Kill it |
| Facebook feeding all budget to one ad, $300+ spend, zero sales | Kill it (check 3-day window) |

> "One data point is not enough. If it keeps getting low AOV kills, kill it — means it's bringing in cheap people."

### 10. Backend Revenue Is the Real Business

**The most important thing Cat said:**

> "Most of you are sitting on a gold mine because you're not [selling to existing customers]. You're too busy trying to obsess over how to fix your campaigns and get higher ROAS. And you're totally missing the point that you've just brought in 300 new customers and haven't offered them a single damn thing on the back."

**Cat's revenue split:** 50% ads / 50% backend. But backend is **pure profit** (no ad spend, no COGS for digital products).

**Backend options:**
- Email sequences selling other products
- Library of existing courses/trainings
- Affiliate products (Cat made $30K/month from ClickFunnels affiliates alone)
- Cross-selling within existing customer base

**For coaches without products to sell:** Affiliate. Find complementary products, promote them to your buyer list. Pure passive income.

### 11. Separate Sales Pages for Unaware (Cat's Live Split Test)

Cat is currently running a split test:
- **OG sales page:** "Automate the sales of your low ticket offer with ads" — headline with USP, buy button at top, direct response
- **New unaware page:** Same funnel, different landing page. Uses the 5-day Persuasion Challenge copy at the top. No buy button until after the copy. More like an article.

**Results so far:**
> "Facebook absolutely preferred my new sales page. Much cheaper cost per clicks, much greater click through rates."

**Structure of unaware page:**
- Same funnel, same order form
- Just a different landing page URL
- Top section = Persuasion Challenge copy (pattern interrupt → problem → why stuck → solution → offer)
- Rest of page = same as OG
- Less direct response, more editorial/article feel

### 12. "Blue Ocean" Exception

If you're in a space with high demand but few competitors (examples: teaching horse owners to connect without riding), you can stretch ad spend much higher before needing scaling strategies.

**Two conditions that delay the need to scale creatively:**
1. Blue ocean niche (demand > supply of educators)
2. Viral/trending topic (market is building the aware audience for you)

**But trending = cowboys:** Hot topics attract competition fast. May need hyper-nicheing to stand out.

### 13. Retargeting Is Dead (As a Separate Campaign)

> "Because of the way Facebook works nowadays, you don't actually need a separate retargeting campaign."

**Why:** With CBO + Advantage Plus, Facebook retargets automatically within the same campaign. Separate retargeting campaigns create auction overlap.

**Exception:** When testing unvalidated creative, use ABO at $15/day. But this is testing, not retargeting.

### 14. Tracking Sheet Discipline

Cat shared her tracking sheet (Google Sheets):
- Fill in once daily, first thing in morning
- Two tabs: "Total Business" (all revenue) and "Direct from Ads" (ad revenue only)
- Track every funnel edit/test with dates so you can revert if something breaks
- Look at 7-14 day and monthly windows, never single days

## Implications for Client Ready

### Multiple Entry Points — Immediate Opportunity

Client Ready already has multiple offers that could become front-end entry points:

| Current Position | Could Become Front-End? | Notes |
|------------------|------------------------|-------|
| $27 Offer System | Already front-end | Current |
| $17 DM Scripts (bump 1) | Yes — flip to standalone | Low price, clear promise, different audience slice |
| $37 Templates (bump 2) | Maybe | Less standalone value |
| $67 First $5K Client Playbook (bump 3) | Yes — standalone at $47-67 | Different buying reason than offer clarity |

**Cat's insight:** "If you have upsells that work, just create separate order pages for them and drive traffic. They hit the aware segment, cheap conversions."

This means the $17 DM Scripts and $67 First $5K Playbook could each become their own front-end offer → order form, with the other products as THEIR bumps. Same catalog, different entry doors.

### AOV Warning: $17 Front-End Kills AOV

Cat's data: $17 price point "completely killed my AOV." Buyers at $17 don't buy bumps/upsells at the same rate as $47 buyers. If testing DM Scripts as standalone front-end, may need to price higher ($27-37) to attract quality buyers.

### Backend Revenue Gap

Client Ready has NO backend email system yet. No daily emails. No library. No affiliate promotions. Based on Cat's data (50% of revenue from backend), this is a massive gap.

The email ascension system is documented in offer.md but not built. Cat's $40K/month from backend to existing buyers = the model.

### The $2,000 Rule for Launch

When Client Ready launches ads: expect first $2,000 of spend to look unprofitable. Don't panic. Don't kill campaigns before $2,000 total spend across all ads. This is the learning phase.

### ABO Testing Lane Needed

Previous methodology implied single CBO only. Cat runs both:
- ABO at $15/ad set for testing new creatives (3-5 sales to validate)
- CBO for all validated creatives

This should be added to methodology: the two-campaign architecture.

### Price Split Test Opportunity

Cat's live split test: higher-priced bumps ($33/$44/$55) beating lower-priced bumps ($17/$33/$55) in conversion rate. Client Ready's current bumps ($17/$37/$67) are in a similar range. Worth testing bump price increases once traffic is running.

## Summary
This research examines email marketing benchmarks and ascension strategies for coaches selling mid-ticket products ($200-500 range). Key findings reveal that coaches should aim for open rates of 30-50%, click-through rates of 2.5-5%, and conversion rates of 3-5% for well-nurtured lists. The "iron strike window" for upsells occurs 5-9 days after initial purchase, while downsell recovery rates for a $47/month community after declining a $297 offer typically range from 10-20%. Buyer lists require different engagement strategies than prospect lists, with exclusive promotions and value-added content driving retention.

## Key Insights
- Coaches achieve 30-50% open rates by leveraging personal brand trust (vs 36-43% industry average)
- The sweet spot for mid-ticket email conversions is 3-5% - significantly higher than general benchmarks
- Optimal upsell timing occurs either immediately post-purchase (momentum) or 5-9 days later (implementation window)
- Daily email strategies differ radically: prospect lists need education (3-5 posts/week), buyer lists thrive on exclusives
- Downsell recovery rates of 10-20% are achievable when transitioning from $297 to $47 offers with proper positioning
- Email engagement benchmarks for service providers:
  • Open rates: 18-22% (B2B), 30-40% (nurtured lists)
  • Click rates: 2.21% average, 6.21% for automated sequences
  • Reply rates: 6-8% (consulting), 1-5% (cold outreach)

## Data Points
- Media/creator emails achieve 6.17% CTR (highest among industries)
- Automated flows convert 5.58% vs 1.69% for broadcast campaigns
- 40-50% of coaching business revenue comes from email during peak periods
- Buyer list emails generate $42 ROI for every $1 spent (high-ticket)

## Contrarian Findings
- "Just be authentic" underperforms systematic frameworks (Justin Welsh's hub-spoke model)
- More emails ≠ better: Diminishing returns hit after 5 LinkedIn posts/week
- Mobile opens (60%) often get neglected in coaching email design

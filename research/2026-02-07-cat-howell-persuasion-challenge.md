# Cat Howell — Persuasion Challenge (Unaware Audience Edition)

**Summary:** 5-day framework for building sales page copy that speaks to unaware audiences — the biggest segment but hardest to convert. Based on Eugene Schwartz's awareness levels.

## Key Findings

### 1. The Awareness Pyramid (What Happens When You Scale)

When you first launch ads, Facebook targets the aware segment (know about you, ready to buy). You get 7-10% conversion, $20-50 CPAs. Eventually Facebook exhausts this pool and starts showing ads to unaware audiences — conversion drops to 2-4%.

**This is why ads "stop working" for most people.** They're not broken — the audience changed.

| Level | What They Know | Conversion Ease |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|
| Aware | Know you, ready to buy | Easiest — just need the link |
| Solution Aware | Know the problem, comparing options | Medium — show why you're the choice |
| Problem Aware | Feel the pain, haven't committed to solving | Medium — agitate, then present solution |
| Unaware | Don't even know they have a problem | Hardest — need pattern interrupt first |

### 2. Unaware = Two Types

- **Normalized the pain** — "This is just the way it is." They've accepted their situation as permanent.
- **Misattributed the pain** — They think the cause is something else. "I'm gaining weight because of menopause" when it's actually diet.

**For Client Ready:** Coaches have normalized that sales calls are required, or misattributed low sales to bad marketing when it's actually an unclear offer.

### 3. The 5-Day Copy Framework

**Day 1: Pattern Interrupt** — One sentence that calls out their normalized belief WITHOUT naming the problem. No diagnosing, no advice, no blame.

Examples:
- "You've been told it's not possible to be profitable running ads to low ticket offers."
- "You've been told it takes years to overcome grief."
- "You think you have to spend years growing your therapy practice through referrals."

**Day 2: Name the Real Problem** — Gently, without blame. 1-2 sentences calling out symptoms they'll recognize.

- "But losing money on your ads every single day is putting you under emotional strain. And you're not a bank."
- Name specific behaviors: "You're turning campaigns on and off. It's erratic."

**Day 3: Why Most People Get Stuck** — A paragraph explaining why the solutions they've tried don't work. This moves them from unaware → problem aware → solution aware.

- Address what they've tried
- Explain why those methods fail
- Position your method as different

**Day 4-5:** (Not fully covered in transcript — likely: present the solution, CTA)

### 4. Funnel Routing by Awareness

| Audience | Send To | Why |
|----------|---------|-----|
| Warm (IG bio, email list) | Order form directly | Adding a sales page = losing people. Warm audience doesn't need persuasion. |
| Cold/Unaware (ads) | Sales page | Need the full persuasion sequence. Direct response pages don't convert this segment. |

**Critical insight:** The short direct-response sales page (headline, video, buy button) works up to ~$20K/month. Beyond that, you NEED a longer page that speaks to unaware audiences.

### 5. Creative Borrowing (Scale Strategy)

For scaling beyond $50K/month, you need:
- Multiple funnels (not just one sales page, one traffic source)
- Hyper-segmentation of messaging (different angles for different audience segments)
- Complementary products at different price points
- Ideas borrowed from other industries (Cat's popup discount idea came from e-commerce)

### 6. ChatGPT Warning

> "ChatGPT is not the greatest at direct response. Any AI. It doesn't compare."

Cat explicitly says to write the copy yourself first, then use AI to polish. The messaging muscle is the make-or-break skill.

## Implications for Client Ready

### Pattern Interrupt Sentences (Client Ready Unaware Audience)

Applying Day 1 framework — what has our audience normalized?

- "You think sales calls are the only way to sell high-ticket coaching."
- "You've accepted that it takes 12 months of content to get your first paying client."
- "You think the reason your offer isn't selling is because you need better marketing."
- "You've been told you need a bigger audience before you can sell."

### Naming the Real Problem (Day 2)

- "But those sales calls are eating 50-70% of your week. And they're getting harder to book every quarter."
- "The truth is, your offer isn't unclear because your marketing is bad. Your marketing is bad because your offer is unclear."
- "You're not posting enough — you're posting without knowing what to say. And that's an offer problem, not a content problem."

### Why Most People Get Stuck (Day 3)

- "Most coaches try to fix this with better sales scripts, DM strategies, or challenge funnels. But all of those are mechanisms — they work or fail depending on how clear the offer is."
- "You can't customize a flagship offer in a Messenger conversation or a VSL. If someone needs a call to understand what they're buying, the offer is too complex."

### Sales Page Restructure Opportunity

Current sales page is short-form direct response. Cat says this works to ~$20K/month but fails for unaware audiences at scale. The front-end product restructure (case study first) could also inform a longer sales page that follows this Day 1-5 framework.

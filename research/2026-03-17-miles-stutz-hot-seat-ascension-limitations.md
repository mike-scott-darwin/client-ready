## Key Insights

### 1. "Lead People to Their Limitations" — The Core Ascension Framework

Miles' central philosophy for mid-funnel and high-ticket ascension:

- **Every solution creates a new set of problems.** Those problems are limitations. Package the solution to those limitations as your next offer.
- **Don't fake scarcity. Don't manufacture urgency.** Just show people what's required to achieve their goal — most will realize they can't or won't do it alone.
- **You'll know it's working when people beg to buy.** If they're not buying, you're addressing the wrong limitations.

**Workshop example:** Miles runs a free workshop where he helps people craft their low-ticket offer live. Once they're excited about it, he walks through what's needed to launch: sales page, checkout, ad account, pixel, email sequences. This naturally surfaces limitations. The offer (Digital Snacks Funnel Install, $2,500) removes those limitations.

> "Offers are resolving limitations, always. And when you can lead someone to their limitations, there is room for an offer, always."

### 2. Order Bump Benchmarks — 30% Minimum

- **At least 2 of your order bumps must convert at 30% or higher.** If one is at 10%, replace it immediately — that's a funnel leak.
- **OTO1 should convert at 10%+.** Below that, improve the page.
- **Use self-sorting OTOs (Mercedes model):** Option A at $97-107, Option B at $197 with "everything from Option A plus access/DFY/review." Miles reports this converts very well.

### 3. OTO Pricing — Go Higher

Miles recommends higher OTO pricing than most coaches use:

| Position | Miles' Recommended Range |
|----------|------------------------|
| Order Bumps | 2-3 bumps, each converting 30%+ |
| OTO 1 | $100-$200 (self-sorting with two options) |
| OTO 2 | $397-$597 |
| AOV target | $60-70 on front-end |

### 4. Front-End Conversion Rate Benchmarks

- **Short-form funnel:** 3-4% minimum (launch phase), 7%+ target
- **Long-form funnel:** Should convert higher than short-form
- **Key formula:** Unique outbound clicks to sales = your conversion rate

### 5. Middle Funnel — The Missing Revenue Layer

Most coaches go straight from low-ticket to high-ticket and miss the middle:

**Middle funnel channels Miles uses:**
- **Monthly "doors open" email sequence** — 10-day sequence opening on the 1st of each month for the $2,500 install offer. "Energetically, this is when people like to start something new."
- **Instagram pinned posts** with ManyChat automation — say "doc" to get the sales document
- **Weekly workshops** (free or paid) — another conversion channel
- **$67-$97 info products with 1:1 call bundled** — use the call as a sales opportunity

**Result:** $20K/month consistently from install sales alone.

### 6. Ad Strategy — Current Best Practices

- **Campaign:** Optimize for conversions
- **Ad sets:** Ad set budget optimization (ABO), not CBO
- **Targeting:** Broad country-based (US, Canada, UK) > interest-based. Interests "never really worked well" for Miles.
- **Creative:** 5 creatives per ad set, each with short-form AND long-form copy
- **Budget:** Launch at $10-$15/day per ad set, max $50/day on launch
- **Scaling:** Increase 20-50% every 48 hours when winning
- **Bot traffic:** Turn off Audience Network in placements. Broad ads attract bots — check placement breakdown.
- **Winner scaling:** When an ad wins, add budget AND create a new ad set with variations of the winner. Use AI (Manus/Claude) to generate variations from the winning principle.

### 7. Headline Formula Reinforcement

**Pattern:** Tangible method + tangible outcome

- Bad: "The $7 secret that eliminates socials and maximizes your social calendar" (too vague)
- Good: "Install these pre-call videos and eliminate no-shows and speak to the decision maker every single time, guaranteed" (specific method, specific outcome)

### 8. Selling Sales Is Hard

Key insight from Jason Flatley conversation:

> "People don't want to do sales. They want to get more sales, but they don't want to learn sales."

**Implication:** If your front-end is a sales product, reframe it (e.g., "coaching conversation" not "sales call"). Then ascend into marketing/appointments — the exciting stuff — not deeper into sales training.

### 9. ROAS & Break-Even Philosophy

- **0.6 ROAS is acceptable** when back-end ecosystem compensates
- **Break-even on front-end is the early-stage goal** — not the long-term goal
- **Dan Kennedy:** "The one who can spend the most to get a customer wins"
- **If spending $2-4K/day and still breaking even, that's excellent** — the volume feeds mid-funnel and back-end

### 10. Claude > ChatGPT

Miles explicitly recommends Claude over ChatGPT. "Much better. I don't use ChatGPT anymore." Manus also mentioned as a creative generation tool for ad variations.

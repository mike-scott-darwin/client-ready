# Channel Expansion Analysis

**Question:** Beyond X, LinkedIn, and newsletter -- where does the Client Ready audience actually spend time?

## Summary

YouTube is the strongest untapped channel. The audience is already there searching for coaching business advice. Facebook Groups, podcast guesting, and Reddit are secondary opportunities.

## Key Findings

1. **YouTube (highest priority)** -- Audience.md confirms they've "watched hours of YouTube content on how to start a coaching business." YouTube search is intent-based -- people typing "how to validate a coaching offer" are pre-qualified. Content compounds (unlike social). 8-15 minute videos map directly to existing pillars (anti-guru, offer creation, behind the scenes). One weekly video deconstructs into existing X threads + LinkedIn posts + newsletter.

2. **Facebook Groups** -- Coaches in transition congregate in groups like "Online Coaches & Course Creators," "Side Hustle to Full-Time," corporate exit communities. Not brand posting -- engaging as Michael. Answering questions, being the knowledgeable guide. DM strategy from content-strategy.md maps directly.

3. **Podcast guest appearances** -- Audience is still commuting (9-to-5 they're trying to leave). High-trust, low-effort. One guest spot on a coaching/entrepreneur podcast (1K-10K listeners) can drive more newsletter signups than a month of tweets. No hosting overhead.

4. **Reddit (r/Entrepreneur, r/coaching, r/sidehustle)** -- People in the research phase (audience's default mode) live here. Not for promotion -- for being genuinely helpful. Builds authority and drives DMs/newsletter signups organically.

5. **Skip for now: Instagram and TikTok** -- Audience skews older, text-first, skeptical of hype. Format doesn't match the depth the message needs.

## Implications for Reference Files

- The newsletter-first model extends naturally: weekly video → newsletter → social posts

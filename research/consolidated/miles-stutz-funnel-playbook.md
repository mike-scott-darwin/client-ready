# Miles Stutz: Digital Snacks & Funnel Playbook

Consolidated research from Miles Stutz calls, products, and community insights.

---

## Core Philosophy

### Digital Snacks Defined

> "Have your digital snack solve the problem in the **fastest way possible**. The only purpose is to solve a micro problem in one sitting and generate a **visible result**."

Digital snacks are small, focused products that:
- Solve ONE specific problem
- Deliver visible results quickly
- Lead to high-ticket offers automatically

---

## Ad Creative Strategy

### Creative Mix (What's Working Now)

- 5-10 creatives per launch = 10-20 ads per ad set
- Mix: 1-2 B-rolls, 1 carousel, 1-2 static images
- Each image should be **outcome-based** (immediately calls out the result)
- Write **one short form + one long form** ad copy per image

### Creative Tips

- **Women in ads = lower cost per click** (clicks go down a lot)
- **Google Notes theme** always works well
- B-roll footage (no talking, just footage) works well
- Static images with outcome-based copy

### AI Workflow

- Miles uses Manus (not Claude) because it can control browser + create images
- Manus prioritizes quality over speed
- Process: Upload info → analyze → give winning ad example → extract angles → iterate

## Ad Set Structure

### Launch 4 Ad Sets Every Cycle

1. **US, UK, Canada, Germany** — winning audience
2. **US, UK, Canada** — also does well
3. **Worldwide lookalike** — usually sucks, rarely wins
4. **Retargeting** — half budget, almost always wins

### Budget

- Start at **$50/day** (not $100 — too volatile)
- Retargeting at half budget of cold ad sets

---

## When to Turn Off Ads

### The 1.5x / 2x Rule

- **No sale:** Let ad set spend **1.5x your goal CPA** before turning off
- **Got 1 sale:** Let it spend **2x goal CPA** before deciding to keep or kill

**Example (goal CPA = $100):**
- Spend $150 with no sale → turn off
- Got 1 sale at $100 → let it run to $300 before deciding
- Rarely see an ad set become good after failing the 1.5x test

---

## The $300 Rule

- If you spend **$300 without a sale, change the snack**
- Don't optimize — change the offer
- "When you have a winning snack, you KNOW"

### Testing Priority (Order of Impact)

1. **Offer** — biggest impact
2. **Headline**
3. **Price**
4. **Design**

> "The biggest challenge you're going to face is finding an offer that converts on cold traffic. Might need to test 10 to find one. Once you got one, your life changes."

---

## Scaling

- Increase budget of winning ad sets by **20-30% every 48 hours**
- 250 ad limit unless spending $1M/month
- Two ways to scale: launch more ads OR increase budget
- Miles now does both

## Order Bumps Strategy

### Key Insight (from Michael Timinger)

> "I made these smaller order bumps, and that worked so much better. People are buying all three order bumps. At the end, it's the same price."

**Miles' response:**
> "That was the point when I was able to launch short form funnels, three order bumps without OTOs and break even on ads."

### Pre-checking Bumps

- Miles pre-checks his order bumps
- 0.1% complaint rate
- Ethical decision everyone makes for themselves
- Never tested without pre-checking

### Order Bumps Changed Everything (Case Study)

From Digital Snacks community member:

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| AOV | $14 | $23 | **+64%** |
| Sales (3 months) | 210 | 500+ | +138% |

> "Then Miles' team suggested adding more order bumps. That single adjustment shifted everything."

**Same traffic. Same core offer. Just better monetization.**

### Over-Converting Bumps = Headline Signal (Feb 24 Call)

If a bump converts at 40%+, that is a signal. Pull that bump copy UP into the front-end headline.

**Miles' example:** A bump about "use my AI prompt that tells you what you should be selling" converted at 43%. He changed the headline to include "AI-powered system" language. Immediate winner.

**The principle:** Bumps are a low-stakes testing ground. High bump conversion means the language resonates. Use that language in the highest-leverage position (the headline) to increase overall funnel throughput.

**Application:** Once bump data exists, check which bump converts highest. That copy tells you what cold traffic actually wants.

## Landing Pages

### Two Options

1. **Minimal Viable:** Pre-headline, headline, sub-headline, button to buy
2. **Beautiful Simple:** More designed but still simple

**Results:** 50/50 between minimal viable and beautiful simple

> "Long form sales pages convert well when you work towards it. But getting them out as a first one is harder."

### Recommendation

- Start with minimal viable
- If you got a sale, you have something to optimize
- Don't forget funnels that got at least one sale

## Product Creation Strategy

- Launch ads WITHOUT having the product ready
- Create product within 24 hours after first sales
- Use GPT to interview you and extract into PDF (~3 hours)
- Some complaints happen — just deliver within 24 hours

### Three Digital Snacks Approach

**Why Three Offers?**
- Finding an offer that converts on cold traffic is the hardest part
- Might need to test 10 to find one
- Three offers = better odds of finding a winner

**What to Vary:**
- Test three different **outcomes** (not just headlines)
- Outcome can change by target market
- Outcome can change by resource type
- Make them completely different from each other

## Email Follow-up Strategy

### What to Promote

1. **Send people back to the OTO first** (low friction commitment)
2. OTO buyers are most likely to buy high-ticket later
3. Add call booking INSIDE OTO products

> "It's a pain in the ass to get people to book a sales call. Wherever you can squeeze in sales calls in the products itself, like an OTO, it's going to make life a lot easier."

## Targeting Strategy

### Go Broad First

> "Unless you already have a business proven to work on cold traffic, the general assumption is that you have no idea who your ideal client is."

- Go as broad as possible initially
- Let cold market tell you who buys
- Narrow down AFTER you have data

> "The beauty about ads — it goes to cold market and cold market chooses. You let the market tell you what they want."

## Copy Techniques (DFY Delivery Analysis)

### Immediate Validation

> "I want to congratulate you on making one of the MOST IMPORTANT decisions of your entrepreneurial journey"

Opens by making buyer feel smart for purchasing.

### "While Your Competitors..." Contrast Structure

Three parallel comparisons:
- While competitors have NO IDEA → you're about to install the exact system
- While most entrepreneurs are a few bad months from corporate → you cover costs on day 1
- While everyone is a "backup dancer for algorithms" → you have automatic clients

**Pattern:** While [bad thing competitors do] → you [good thing you now have]

### "It's Your Turn" Repetition

Builds momentum through repeated phrase:
- It's your turn to wake up to stripe hits...
- It's your turn to never worry where your next client is coming from...
- It's your turn to cover all your costs on the first day of every month...
- It's your turn to have a vacation-proof business...
- It's your turn to finally join the 1% club...

### Specific Outcomes (Miles' Numbers)

- "100-300 buyers every week"
- "4-6 high-profit clients daily"
- "Cover all living costs and business expenses on the first day of every month"
- "Opening 10 spots and having them fill in 6 hours"

### Anti-Content Angle

- "professional backup dancer for social media algorithms"
- "spending 2 hours creating content that gets 12 likes"
- "deleting Instagram off their phone"

This resonates with coaches tired of the content hamster wheel.

### Mini Case Studies by Type

**Agencies:** inconsistent revenue → covering expenses on day 1, everything else pure profit

**Consultants:** chasing clients + following up on invoices → always sold out with waitlist, 10 spots fill in 6 hours

**Coaches:** 2 hours content for 12 likes → deleting Instagram, 100-300 automatic buyers/week

## Funnel Metrics Benchmarks

### Benchmark Data (from Skool)

**Product:** £11 front-end

| Metric | Value | Target |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Link CTR | 1.28% | 1%+ is good |
| CPC | £0.36 | Varies |
| Opt-in rate (from outbound clicks) | 3.26% | 3%+ for short form |
| Sales page conversion | 10% | Higher is better |
| Bump take rate | 200% | Amazing |

### Miles' Feedback

#### Use Outbound Clicks as Denominator
> "Your opt-in rate is actually 3.26%. Always take outbound clicks from FB."

**Why:** Page views include refreshes, bots, etc. Outbound clicks = real humans.

#### 3.26% Opt-in is "Okay"
> "This opt-in rate is okay considering it's a short form funnel."

#### 10% Sales Page Conversion is Low
> "Only a 10% conversion rate on sales page is low."

**How to fix:**
- Test the headline
- Add more context — "who it is for, pay off & PROOF it works"

#### Consider Unchecking Order Bumps
> "Consider unchecking order bumps if selected & add more proof to check out page"

**Why:** Pre-checked bumps can hurt conversion if people feel tricked.

## What to Track

- **Outbound clicks** from Meta (not page views)
- **Opt-in rate** = opt-ins / outbound clicks
- **Sales conversion** = purchases / opt-ins
- **Bump take rate** = bump purchases / orders
- **AOV** = total revenue / orders

### If Conversion is Low, Test:

1. **Headline** — biggest impact
2. **Context** — who it's for, payoff, proof
3. **Pre-checked bumps** — may help or hurt depending on audience sophistication
4. **Proof on checkout page** — testimonials, guarantees

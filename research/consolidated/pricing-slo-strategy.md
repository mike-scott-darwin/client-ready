# Self-Liquidating Offers & Pricing Strategy

Consolidated research on SLO economics and front-end pricing decisions.

---

## Core Concept: SLO = Customer Acquisition, Not Profit

> "Self-liquidating offers break even on ad spend to acquire buyers (not leads), with profitability coming from AOV stacking and high-ticket backend."

The goal is $0 CAC, not revenue. If you acquire a buyer for $35 and make $35, you have a self-liquidating offer. Profit comes from backend.

---

## 2026 SLO Economics

### The Reality Check

> "Low ticket offers had a moment back in 2020. As soon as the cost of clicks went up and competition flooded in, those funnels flopped."

**What changed:**
- CPCs rose
- Competition flooded in
- Funnels that worked in 2020 no longer work
- Expect break-even, not profit

### Current Requirements

- **20+ creatives** before launching paid traffic
- **$27+ pricing** to cover ad costs in 2026 (avg CPC $0.83)
- **Realistic expectations** — break-even is success

## Optimal Front-End Pricing

### The Sweet Spots

$7, $17, $27, and $47 are all proven sweet spots. Neither is universally superior — depends on backend structure and traffic source.

**Cold audience sweet spot:** $17-$67

**Can go to $197 if:**
- Solid testimonials
- Super defined USP (micro outcome)
- Authority in the space

### $17 vs $27 Buyer Quality

**$17 buyers:**
- More impulse purchases
- Higher volume
- Better for building large buyer lists fast
- May be less qualified for high-ticket backend
- "A $7 buyers list is about as effective as a freebie list" for $300+ backend products

**$27 buyers:**
- More considered purchase (still impulse, but higher commitment)
- Lower volume, higher quality signal
- Better suited for $5K+ backend
- Filters out tire-kickers earlier

### The Real Lever: Offer Stack Perception

The $10 difference matters less than:
- **Stack perception**: Does it feel like a steal?
- **Bundle psychology**: "Stack it. Add bonuses that complement the core offer to make your buyer feel like they're getting VIP access for a latte's worth of cash."
- **Price anchoring**: Striking out ~~$497~~ → $47 matters more than $17 vs $27

## Pricing Options Analysis

### Option A: Stay at $27 (Recommended for High-Ticket Backend)

**Rationale:** Targeting coaches who can pay $5K+ high-ticket. A $27 buyer is more qualified than a $17 buyer.

**Actions:**
- Improve sales page conversion (target >2.5%)
- Strengthen order bump copy (target 30%+ take rate)
- Track qualification rate to high-ticket calls

### Option B: Drop to $17, Increase Bumps

**Rationale:** Maximize front-end volume, make money on bumps.

**New structure:**
- Front-end: $17
- Bump 1: $57 (increase from $47)
- Bump 2: $47 (increase from $37)
- Max checkout: $121 (higher than current $111)

**Risk:** More buyers, but may attract less qualified leads for $5K backend.

### Option C: Test Both with Split

**Rationale:** Let data decide.

**Setup:**
- Traffic source A → $17 landing page
- Traffic source B → $27 landing page
- Track: CVR, AOV, OTO rate, and qualification-to-call rate
- Run for 100+ sales each before deciding

> "Think of SLOs not as your golden goose, but as strategic tools designed for building your email list rapidly and cost-effectively. If they make you some money in the process, consider that a delightful bonus rather than the norm."

Only 2.5% of ecommerce visitors convert. SLO attracts buyers (38% retention rate) vs. lead magnets that attract tire-kickers.

## Backend is Where Profit Lives

### Real Case Study: $27 → $12k Funnel

1. **$27 front-end** — Low barrier entry
2. **Twin $97 order bumps** — "No-brainer" positioning
3. **$1,000 "Slack Adjuster"** — Explicitly positioned to recover ad spend
4. **$12,000 backend service** — Implementation call → Closing call → Sale

What made it work:
- Each step positioned as logical next step
- The $1,000 tier specifically addressed the buyer's pain point (traffic cost recovery)
- Multi-step sales process with email/SMS sequences

### Case Study: $22 → $480 (Ashley's Breakdown)

- $22 Brand & Business Toolkit (front-end)
- $27 order bump (60% take rate — exceptional)
- $97 upsell (10% take rate — normal)
- Coaching membership backend

**Key insight:** "Buyers are more likely to continue buying." The $22 creates a psychological buying frenzy that makes subsequent offers easier to close.

## Warning Signs

1. **$17 may not self-liquidate** — Math doesn't work at current CPCs without exceptional bump rates
2. **One creative won't scale** — Need 20+ before launching paid traffic
3. **Ascension isn't automatic** — Plan for months of nurturing between $97 and $5k+
4. **Testing requires budget** — $100+/day minimum to get meaningful data
5. **Backend is mandatory** — Without $5k+ offer, funnel is a list-builder, not a business

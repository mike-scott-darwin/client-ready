## NEW / sharper (beyond offer.md + June 2 synthesis)

### 1. Order Bump & OTO design framework (the big one)
A bump/OTO must **never feel required** to get results from the front-end purchase — that kills front-end conversion AND uptake. It may only do one of two things:
- **(1) Add convenience** — automation, speed, done-for-you, integration.
- **(2) Solve the next problem** that naturally arises once the front end solves the first. ("Problem → solution → next problem → solution.")

Generate them with AI: "Here's what I sell; give me order bumps/OTOs that either add convenience or solve the immediate next problem."

**Maria Wendt example (Miles cited as brilliant):** core product = make more money per customer → OTO1 = tool that tells you what your bumps should be (convenience) → OTO2 = diagnostic that finds where in the funnel you lose money (next problem) → OTO3 = AI course to create products fast (convenience).

**Client Ready mapping:** bumps already follow next-problem ($37 DM Scripts → start convos; $67 Templates → don't build from scratch; $97 Playbook → now close). Structure is right; fix the *headline framing* (below).

### 2. Direct-response bump headlines with the guarantee baked in
"Integrate this DM script and turn your next 5 DMs into a sales conversation — or your money back." Every low-ticket has a money-back guarantee anyway → put it in the bump headline. Signal: "mine is better than yours."

### 3. Sell the outcome, not the category
Audits/calculators/tools convert poorly on the front end because the problem-aware market already knows. Sell the tangible result they're *already* looking for ("instantly know how much you're making in 5 min/day"), not a solution they aren't searching for ("audit your business"). "You know they need it because you're the consultant; they don't."

### 4. Launch before the product exists
Spend day one on the sales page only, go live with ads, and when the first sale lands you have 24h to build/deliver (automation: "automation hiccup — delivered within 24h; reach out if it doesn't arrive"). Validate demand before building. Order bumps only; defer OTOs until after first sale. (Less urgent for CR — products exist — but the rule for any *new* snack.)

### 5. Short-form first → add sections one at a time → lifts bump uptake
Every funnel starts short-form. "The less you say, the more *curiosity* purchases you get — and curiosity buyers take fewer bumps." So low bump rates are often a thin-sales-page problem.
- Fix: add ONE section at a time, A/B each: who it's for (niches/countries), what's inside, "what this does to your life" (emotional), who you are, proof + guarantee.
- Kill a section if no significance after ~200 views.
- Adding good context without dropping front-end conversion → bumps/OTOs rise naturally (people understand what they bought).

### 6. Checkout (step 2) benchmark = 30%
Two-step order form: step 2 should convert ~30%. Below 20% → reposition proof on the checkout page; strip friction fields (phone, coupon codes — anything not required). Bumps can themselves drag checkout conversion down (people feel they must buy more to succeed).

### 7. Order-bump price is testable both ways
A $12 bump that didn't sell, doubled to $24, sold. But if they won't take it at $27, they won't at $47. One client sells a $47 AI-library bump at 50% uptake. If you get 20–30 sales and ~1 bump, the offer's just not good enough — replace it.

### 8. Scaling a winner (Post ID into multiple ad sets)
Don't turn off the other ads in the set — scale the ad set. Copy the winning **Post ID into multiple ad sets** to push more spend behind the one proven ad (keeps accumulating social proof/comments). Ad "refreshes" rarely work; instead have AI study the winning *angle* and build new ads around it.

### 9. Change-multiple-things at low volume
At low volume you can change several things at once (hunting 50%+ swings). One-thing-at-a-time discipline only matters at ~500–1,000 sessions/day.

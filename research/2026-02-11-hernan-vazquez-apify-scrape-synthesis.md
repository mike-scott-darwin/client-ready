# Hernan Vazquez Apify Scrape Synthesis

**Source:** Apify Meta Ad Library scrape (26 ads, Feb 11 2026)
**Prior research:** `competitors/hernan-vazquez.md` (Jan 25 research)
**Raw data:** `2026-02-11-hernan-vazquez-meta-ads-apify.json`
**One-sentence summary:** Hernan has quietly shifted his primary front-end from the book ($4.95) to the Rapid Scale Framework ($5), runs 50-100 AI-generated creatives per week (up from 10), and 31% of his ads use comment-trigger DM automation with no link URL.

## Key Findings (New — Not in Jan 25 Profile)

### 1. Primary Front-End Has Shifted
The Jan 25 profile focused on `lowticketbook.com` (the $4.95 book). But his **dominant front-end is now the Rapid Scale Framework ($5)** via `rapidscaleframework.com/of`. Only 1 of 26 ads drives to the book. 5+ ads drive to the Framework. New domain, new product, new funnel.

### 2. Creative Volume Is 5-10x Higher Than Documented
Jan 25 profile quoted "10 new creatives per week." His current ads claim **50-100 new ads per week in 10-15 minutes using AI**. "AI does the heavy lifting. Images. Copy. All of it."

### 3. Comment-Trigger DM Automation (31% of Ads)
8 of 26 ads have NO direct link. They use comment triggers:
- "Drop BUNDLE below"
- "Say Workshop"
- "Say Seven"
- "Drop a line"

These are his OLDEST running ads (May 2025) — 9+ months active. Engagement-first strategy that presumably triggers automated DMs.

### 4. Entirely New Product Ecosystem
The Jan 25 profile only knew about `lowticketbook.com`. He's built `rapidscaleframework.com` with 6+ URL paths:

| Path | Product | Price |
|------|---------|-------|
| /of | Rapid Scale Framework | $5 |
| /retargeting-of | Retargeting Playbook | $7 |
| /Workshop | Low Ticket Workshop | $27 |
| /validate | Validation Workshop | Unknown |
| /asc | ASC Training | Free |
| /apply | High-ticket application | Application |

Plus `RapidScaleMastery.com/apply` for a higher tier.

### 5. Transparent Upsell Disclosure as Trust Pattern
Every long-form $5 Framework ad includes: "Fair warning though... There IS an upsell on the next page. I'm also write a newsletter for advertisers... No pressure to buy it. But I wanted to be upfront about it. Because I hate when people pretend there's no backend." Verbatim in 3+ ads. Deliberate trust-building.

### 6. "Targeting Is Dead, Creative Is Everything" (Core Thesis)
Jan 25 profile said "results not education." His actual positioning is sharper: targeting is dead because Facebook's AI handles it now. Creative IS the targeting. References Hormozi's launch as proof. 2-campaign system:
- Campaign 1: Creative Testing (broad, 3-5 new creatives Monday, kill losers Wednesday)
- Campaign 2: Scaling Machine (Advantage+ Sales, higher budget, only proven creative)

### 7. Newsletter as Backend Upsell (Not $97 Membership)
The $5 Framework upsells to a **newsletter for advertisers** — monthly breakdowns. This is either a reposition of the $97 membership or a new product entirely.

### 8. Copy Length Spectrum Testing
Same product ($5 Framework) tested across wildly different lengths simultaneously:
- **Ultra-short** (1 sentence): "How to launch and scale campaigns that work... in 10 minutes or less."
- **Medium** (~700 words): Authority + name-drops + blue shirt analogy
- **Mega-long** (~1,800 words): Full story arc, restaurant analogy, transparent upsell

### 9. Format Segmentation Is Deliberate
- **Image ads** = long-form sales copy in the body (copy IS the ad)
- **Video ads** = short body text, video carries all the information
- Never mixes these — no long copy on video, no short copy on image

## Implications for Client Ready / Main Branch

### Convergent Patterns Across All 4 Competitors Today

| Pattern | Cat Howell | Miles Stutz | Hernan Vazquez | Main Branch |
|---------|-----------|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| Comment-to-DM automation | 4 ads | 3 ads | 8 ads (31%) | None |
| Multiple front-end products | 4 URL variants | 2 products + $17 hidden | 6+ URL paths | 1 product |
| AI mention in ads | No | Yes (2 ads) | Yes ("AI does heavy lifting") | No |
| Copy length variety | Long + short | Long + short | Ultra-short + medium + mega-long | Medium only |
| Ultra-short video (<10s) | 5 ads | 4 ads | No | No |
| Long-form video (10+ min) | No | 4 ads | 11 ads | 2 assets |
| Transparent pricing in ad | "$67" | "$9" | "$5" / "$4.95" / "$7" | No (24 of 26) |
| Social proof in ad copy | Revenue stories | "1,090+ students" | "$100M+ ad spend", name-drops | None |
| Evergreen ads (5+ months) | N/A | 4 ads (Sep 2025) | 15 ads (May-Sep 2025) | N/A (too new) |

### Key Takeaways for Main Branch

1. **Comment-trigger ads are a proven pattern across all 3 competitors.** 15 ads total use comment automation. Main Branch has zero. This is the most consistent gap.

2. **Price transparency in ads is universal.** Cat says $67, Miles says $9, Hernan says $5. Main Branch only mentions free trial in 2 of 26 ads. Every competitor makes the buy obvious.

3. **Copy length variety matters.** Hernan tests the same product across 1-sentence, 700-word, and 1,800-word versions. Main Branch has one copy length per block.

4. **Multiple front-end pathways are standard.** All 3 competitors run 2-6+ entry points. Main Branch has 1.

5. **AI-as-positioning is emerging.** Miles and Hernan both mention AI in ads. Main Branch's core value prop IS AI-native but the ads don't position it that way.

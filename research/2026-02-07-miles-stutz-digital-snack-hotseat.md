# Miles Stutz — Digital Snack Hot Seat (Marie's Agency)

**Summary:** Live coaching call showing how to derive digital snacks from solved client problems, with current pricing and validation benchmarks.

## Key Findings

1. **$9 price point is current best practice** — "Below $10 when you start, that's what we currently have best practices with. $9 would be really good." Previously $17+ was standard; market has shifted.

2. **Digital snacks = reverse-engineered case studies** — Take a problem you solved for a client that generated money. Turn it into a product. Example: "Helped client generate $21K from existing audience with a mid-ticket VSL" → digital snack teaches that exact process.

3. **Order bumps carry the model** — Miles has 0.5-1.5% front-end conversion (well below 3% floor, far below Hernan's 7% target) but achieves 1.2 ROAS because 3 order bumps carry the offer. Don't stress front-end conversion — optimize bumps first.

4. **Validation thresholds:**
   - First $300: aim for 1-3 sales (you're in the red, that's expected)
   - $300 with 0 sales: change the snack, not the copy
   - Next $300: target 0.8-0.9 ROAS via order bumps
   - You'll KNOW when you have a winner (5-6 sales in $300 = winner)

5. **AI product creation workflow:**
   - Tell AI: "Interview me about how I created [outcome] for [client]"
   - Use Whisper Flow for voice-to-AI
   - Feed AI an example product to model after (Miles uses No-Phone Offer PDF)
   - 60-90 minutes for a brand new product
   - Output: case study-based PDF. Nothing else needed.

6. **Testing priority (one at a time):**
   - Change the OFFER first (biggest impact)
   - Then headline
   - Then price
   - Then design
   - Only change one variable per $300 test

7. **Funnel optimization order:**
   - Step 1: Validate front-end (get sales)
   - Step 2: Add order bumps (get to break-even)
   - Step 3: THEN add OTOs (don't think OTOs until bumps are carrying)
   - Step 4: Scale with CBO

8. **Multiple digital snacks from one engagement:**
   - From one client win, Miles extracted 3+ digital snacks: the offer creation process, the VSL, the social media posting sequence
   - Each is a $9 front-end with its own bumps
   - Can swap headlines/pre-headlines in 20 minutes and retest

## Implications for Reference Files

- **offer.md pricing:** $27 vs $9 is a testable hypothesis. Bumps absorb the revenue gap. Lower price = higher conversion = more people in funnel.
- **Front-end product:** Current product is a prompt system. Miles' framework says make it a case study first, tools second. More proof, more concrete.
- **Product creation for DFY clients:** Miles' AI interview workflow is exactly what the DFY onboarding form does. Validates the approach.
- **Funnel optimization sequence:** Aligns with current strategy (bumps → OTOs → scale). Confirms order of operations.

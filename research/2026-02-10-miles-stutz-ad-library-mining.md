# Miles Stutz Ad Library Mining

**Source:** Meta Ad Library screenshots (40-50+ active ads, Jan-Feb 2026)
**One-sentence summary:** Miles runs 40-50+ active ads dominated by women-in-photos creatives, long-form narrative copy, and anti-organic angles — testing at 3-4x Client Ready's current volume with dual copy lengths per image.

## Key Findings

### Creative Types (What the Images Look Like)

| Creative Type | Est. % | Description |
|---------------|--------|-------------|
| **Women (lifestyle/headshot)** | ~40% | Stock or sourced lifestyle photos of women. Not his clients. Pure scroll-stop strategy. |
| **Phone/device mockups** | ~15% | Smartphone screens showing Stripe notifications, product interfaces, checkout pages. "Proof of mechanism" visuals. |
| **Dark text cards** | ~15% | "Google Notes" style — dark background, bold text overlay. His most consistent-performing format per his own teaching. |
| **Bold color (pink/magenta)** | ~10% | Pattern interrupt colors that break feed norms. Newer — more visible in Feb than Jan captures. |
| **Video thumbnails** | ~10% | Small minority. Despite teaching video has "more sustainability," he's still mostly static. |
| **Other (carousel, misc)** | ~10% | Testing variety. |

**Key observation: Miles barely uses his own face in cold traffic ads.** He sells a personal brand coaching program, but leads with lifestyle imagery and product mockups — not selfies or talking-head thumbnails. Trust comes from copy, not face recognition at the cold traffic level.

### Copy Patterns

**1. Dual Copy Length Testing (Confirmed Live)**
Miles runs both short-form (3-5 lines) and long-form narrative versions on the SAME image. This is the "one short form + one long form per image" strategy from his teaching, confirmed in the live ad library. He's systematically A/B testing copy length.

**2. Long-Form Narrative Dominates**
The majority of visible ads feature substantial text blocks — long-form story-driven copy. Short-form is present but the ratio looks ~70/30 long/short.

**3. Ad Copy Skeleton (Reconstructed)**

```
1. Hook: Personal story OR provocative statement OR case study name-drop
2. Pain: What they're doing wrong (organic, sales calls, content treadmill)
3. Bridge: "Here's what I discovered..." / "That's when I built..."
4. Mechanism: Digital Snacks / Daily Client Funnel / specific system name
5. Proof: Case study with specific numbers (income claims)
6. Stack: What they get (digital snack, templates, system)
7. CTA: "Learn More" button / "Grab it here" / link
```

**4. Hook Patterns**

| Pattern | Example | Frequency |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Personal confession | "I signed a $15K client yesterday" (framed as personal experience, not promise) | High |
| Anti-organic attack | "Spending 2 hours creating content that gets 12 likes" | High |
| Case study name-drop | "Allan's 9-page PDF made him $5K in 72 hours" | Medium |
| Provocative statement | From emails: "conspiracy theory?" / "this is ridiculous" | Medium |
| Speed claim | "First sale in 72 hours" / "100 customers in 7 days" | Lower |

### Volume

- **40-50+ active ads** across two captures (Jan 30 and Feb 10)
- **3-5 creative treatments per angle** — multiple images + copy lengths per thesis
- **Persistent winners + constant new tests** — some ads appear in both captures, new ones added between
- **3-4x Client Ready's current volume** (14 primaries). Comparable to Cat Howell's ~78.
- Matches his teaching: "Fire quick. Let them run. Over CPA? Turn it off. But launch a lot."

### CTA Patterns

- **"Learn More"** button (most common)
- **"Grab it here"** / link in copy body
- **Some comment CTAs** ("Reply BUGATTI" from emails, "Comment [WORD]")
- Headlines reinforce CTA: "Get the system" / "Start today"

## New Observations (Not in Existing Research)

These findings are NEW compared to what's already in `competitors/miles-stutz.md` and `consolidated/miles-stutz-funnel-playbook.md`:

### 1. He Doesn't Lead with His Face
Despite being a personal brand, cold traffic creatives are overwhelmingly not selfies. This suggests face recognition is a retargeting/warm strategy, not a cold acquisition strategy. **Implication:** Michael doesn't need to be the creative. Product mockups and conceptual images can carry cold traffic.

### 2. Phone Mockup = Outcome Visualization Through Device
Showing a phone screen with Stripe notifications is Miles' version of "outcome visualization." It's concrete, visual, and aspirational. **Client Ready equivalent (no income claims):** Show the offer document on a phone screen. Show a "just booked" notification. Show the landing page going live. Show a DM saying "I need this." The device is the frame; the content changes from dollars to clarity moments.

### 3. Pink/Magenta Is Newer
Bold color creatives appeared more in the Feb capture than Jan. Suggests active testing of pattern-interrupt colors. **Implication:** Color variety in creatives matters. Client Ready's teal/amber is consistent but could test one bold accent color for interrupt creatives.

### 4. Anti-Organic Is His Biggest Category
~30% of active ads attack the content treadmill. This is the angle with the most creative variations, suggesting it's performing well and he's scaling it. **Implication:** Client Ready has "Content Merry-Go-Round" in research but hasn't turned it into ads yet. This is a validated angle worth building.

### 5. Video Is Still Minority
Despite Miles teaching that video "has more sustainability when it works," only ~10% of his active library is video. Static is still the workhorse. **Implication:** Don't rush to video. Static ads at volume is the proven path.

### 6. Short-Form Runs Alongside Long-Form on Same Image
Not alternating — both live simultaneously on the same creative. This means the same scroll-stopping image gets tested with a 3-line hook AND a full narrative. **Implication:** Batch 003's compressed versions should be paired with the SAME images as their parent primaries, not treated as separate creative units.

## Implications for Client Ready

### Potential New Work

1. **Device mockup creatives** — Show the Client Ready system on a phone/laptop: the offer doc, the "I need this" DM, the landing page. Outcome visualization without income claims.

2. **Anti-content angle ads** — Build 5 primaries around "Content Merry-Go-Round" angle. Miles validates this as his #1 performing category. Client Ready has the research; hasn't produced the ads.

3. **Creative variety expansion** — Test beyond conceptual images: stock lifestyle photos (not necessarily women — coaches at laptops, messy desks, clean single-page documents), product mockups, bold-color text cards.

4. **Paired short/long per image** — Instead of treating compressed versions as separate assets, run each compressed version paired to the same image as its parent primary. A/B test copy length on identical creative.

5. **Bold accent color test** — Add one non-teal/amber color (deep coral? bright white-on-black?) for pattern interrupt creatives specifically.

### What NOT to Change

- Don't add income claims. Miles' FTC exposure is his problem.
- Don't adopt "bro" energy. The engineering voice IS the differentiation.
- Don't use fake urgency. "Or don't. I'm not going to chase you." is stronger positioning.
- Don't prioritize video over static. Miles proves static at volume wins.
- Don't lead with Michael's face for cold traffic. Product/concept images work.

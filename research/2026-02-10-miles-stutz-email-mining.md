# Miles Stutz Email Mining — Jan 10 to Feb 10, 2026

## Summary

40 emails in 31 days across two overlapping campaigns: "Digital Snacks Install" ($2K done-for-you funnel build) and ongoing content/nurture. High-volume, story-driven email sequence that blends vulnerability, case studies, urgency, and fear-of-missing-out.

## Campaign Structure

### Two Distinct Phases

**Phase 1: Install Launch (Jan 10–Feb 2)** — ~28 emails in 23 days
Hard sell for "Digital Snacks Funnel Install" at $2K. Done-for-you funnel build with 100-customer guarantee. Constant urgency (spots limited, price rising, doors closing).

**Phase 2: Content/Nurture (Feb 3–10)** — ~12 emails in 7 days
Shifted to value content, YouTube video pushes, origin stories, and mindset emails. No direct CTA to buy — building trust for next launch.

### Email Cadence

- Launch phase: 1-2 emails per day (aggressive)
- Nurture phase: 1 email per day
- Total: ~40 emails in 31 days

## Copy Patterns & Tactics

### 1. Vulnerability as Authority

Miles' strongest differentiator. He leads with failure stories, not success stories:

- **$2M agency collapse:** Had to shut down, refund all clients, took $300K in loans, COO sexually harassed his friend, 60 contractors couldn't deliver. "It was 100% my fault."
- **ADHD medication origin:** Started in dad's basement on ADHD meds, friends unfollowed him, called "the influencer guy." No degree, no job, no proof.
- **Hiring fear:** "I was TERRIFIED" about hiring again after 2024 collapse. Full transparency about the emotional cost.

**Pattern:** Open with the worst moment → own the mistake → extract the lesson → position the current offer as the solution born from that failure.

**Contrast with Client Ready:** Michael has a version of this (7-year search, burning down misaligned offers). Miles goes deeper and more raw. The vulnerability is his brand — every story ends with "that's why I built this system differently."

### 2. Anti-Organic Positioning

Consistent through every email — organic marketing is the enemy:

- "Organic marketing doesn't scale without you"
- "You're always working IN the business... there's no off switch"
- "When I started with DMs in 2020, you could send 50 messages and book 10 calls. Today? You're lucky if you book 2."
- "Content merry-go-round: Post, hope, repeat"

**The argument:** Organic = unscalable job. Paid traffic + low-ticket funnel = scalable business. This is the same thesis as Client Ready's "Content Merry-Go-Round" angle but Miles frames it as his lived experience (ran a DM agency, it collapsed).

### 3. Low-Ticket as Gateway

Core mechanism positioning:

- Low-ticket digital product ($9-$97) pays for ad spend same day
- Buyers become high-ticket clients through automated pipeline
- "Customers fund your ad spend" — self-liquidating funnel
- Case study: Allan's 9 PDF made him 5K in 72 hours

**Specific claims made:**
- Allan Miles: 100 customers in 7 days, 14K in sales
- Raeed Al-Dossry: 500+ total sales in 3 months
- Ferenc Nemeth: 472 offers sold, 292 new clients, profitable from DAY 1
- Starr Codd: $18,600 in ONE WEEK
- Alex Spiga: First 5.6K sale WITHOUT a sales call

### 4. Urgency Mechanics

Heavy urgency throughout — multiple types stacked:

| Urgency Type | Examples |
|-------------|----------|
| Scarcity | "3 spots left," "2 spots left," "1 spot remaining" |
| Deadline | "Monday 11:59 PM EST," "closes tonight" |
| Price anchoring | "$2K now, raising price after" |
| Window closing | "Low-ticket funnels are working like crazy. But this window? It's closing." |
| Social proof pressure | "8 smart people already took us up," "we might sell out before deadline" |
| Competitor timing | "Before your competitors figure this out" |

**Observation:** The urgency is relentless — almost every email has a deadline or scarcity element. This is effective but aggressive. Could fatigue the list over time.

### 5. "Done For You" as Differentiator

The Install offer's core differentiator vs. courses:

- "We BUILD the entire funnel FOR you"
- "We write your ads FOR you"
- "We literally click 'go live' WITH you"
- "This isn't a course or group program"
- Team value anchor: "Getting a $750K/year team for $2K"

**Price anchoring math:**
- Senior Funnel Strategist: $80K-$120K/year
- Conversion Copywriter: $75K-$100K/year
- Funnel Designer: $60K-$85K/year
- Media Buyer: $70K-$100K/year
- Total: $285K-$405K/year → "You're getting them for $2K"

### 6. Subject Line Patterns

| Pattern | Examples |
|---------|---------|
| Personal/confessional | "I have to confess something Michael," "I was TERRIFIED Michael" |
| Curiosity + shock | "I had to shut down my $2M agency (and refund all my clients)" |
| Name personalization | Most subjects include "Michael" |
| Specific numbers | "Allan's 9 PDF made him 5K in 72 hours," "$750,000 per year" |
| Brackets for framing | "[TRUTH] what stops experts from making real money," "[PROOF]" |
| Open loops | "Will this actually work for MY business?" |
| Urgency | "7 hours left," "36 hours left," "FINAL CALL" |

### 7. Email Structure (Consistent Pattern)

```
1. Personal hook (story, confession, or provocative statement)
2. Pain amplification (what they're doing wrong)
3. Story/case study (proof it works differently)
4. Mechanism explanation (how the system works)
5. Offer details (what they get)
6. Urgency (deadline, spots, price)
7. CTA (reply "BUGATTI" or click link)
8. P.S. (reframe or additional urgency)
```

Short paragraphs. Heavy line breaks. Lots of white space. Conversational — reads like a DM, not a newsletter.

### 8. Two Offers in Sequence

| Offer | Price | Positioning |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **Bugatti** (Jan 10-26) | Not stated (high-ticket) | 1-on-1, 90-day, WhatsApp access, weekly calls, "me in your pocket" |
| **Digital Snacks Install** (Jan 27-Feb 2) | $2,000 | Done-for-you funnel build, 100-customer guarantee, team access |

Bugatti closed first → Install opened immediately. Seamless transition with the Install positioned as the more accessible option.

## Competitive Analysis vs. Client Ready

### Where Miles Overlaps

| Topic | Miles' Version | Client Ready Version |
|-------|---------------|---------------------|
| Anti-organic | "Organic doesn't scale without you" | "Content Merry-Go-Round" angle |
| Offer-first | "Digital Snacks" = low-ticket product first | "Before the Funnel" = validate offer first |
| Paid traffic | Ads as the path to freedom | Same — paid traffic over organic |
| Speed | "Customers within hours/days" | "One afternoon to validate" |
| Anti-course | "This isn't a course" | "$27 vs $997 course" positioning |

### Where Miles Differs (His Strengths)

1. **Volume + velocity:** 40 emails in 31 days. Aggressive but keeps him top of mind.
2. **Raw vulnerability:** Agency collapse story, ADHD origin, hiring fear — deeply personal.
3. **Specific revenue claims:** Allan 14K, Raeed 500+ sales, Starr $18.6K week. Specific numbers everywhere (FTC risk but effective).
4. **Done-for-you positioning:** "We build it FOR you" — removes execution barrier entirely.
5. **Team value anchor:** $750K team for $2K — powerful price framing.
6. **Reply-based CTA:** "Reply BUGATTI" — drives engagement, builds relationship, avoids link fatigue.

### Where Client Ready Wins

1. **Alignment focus:** Miles never talks about building something you believe in. His frame is pure scale/revenue.
2. **Earlier-stage clarity:** Miles assumes you have expertise to package. Client Ready helps you FIND the offer.
3. **Sustainability angle:** "You can't grow into pain" — Miles' agency collapse actually proves this, but he doesn't frame it that way.
4. **Lower barrier to entry:** $27 vs $2,000.
5. **AI mechanism:** Miles doesn't use AI positioning at all.
6. **FTC safety:** Client Ready's reviewed copy is cleaner. Miles makes specific income claims liberally.

### What Client Ready Could Learn

1. **Story-first emails:** Every Miles email opens with a story. Not a tip, not a lesson — a story. This is why they get opened and read.
2. **Vulnerability depth:** Miles goes further into failure. The agency collapse email is his best — it's a full confession. Michael's misalignment story could go deeper.
3. **Reply-based CTAs:** "Reply [WORD]" instead of click-link. Creates conversation, signals to email providers (deliverability), builds relationship.
4. **Cadence during launches:** 1-2/day during a launch window is aggressive but standard for this market.
5. **Case study format:** Specific person → their before → what they did → specific result → timeframe. Allan's email is a masterclass in this.
6. **Price anchoring through team cost:** Comparing offer price to team salary costs is a powerful frame Client Ready could adapt for the $27 offer.

## Implications for Reference Files

- **Content Merry-Go-Round angle:** Miles' anti-organic positioning is similar but comes from agency experience. His "organic doesn't scale without you" line is stronger than current variations.
- **Email strategy:** When Client Ready builds email sequences, Miles' story-first + reply-CTA pattern is proven in this market.
- **Voice contrast:** Miles is raw, aggressive, high-volume. Client Ready is grounded, engineering-minded, lower frequency. Both work — important not to copy Miles' tone, but his structural patterns are transferable.
- **Case study template:** The Allan email format (person → backstory → what they tried → what they did differently → specific result → timeframe → lesson) is worth adapting for Client Ready testimonials.

# Meta Ads for Low-Ticket Coach Offers (2026)

## Summary
Our research reveals that Meta advertising for low-ticket coaching offers ($47-$97 range) in 2026 requires a mobile-first approach with creative diversity as the primary lever for success. The platform has shifted dramatically toward AI-driven optimization, making creative quality and volume more important than granular targeting. Self-liquidating offers remain crucial for sustainable customer acquisition, with front-end pricing between $47-$97 proving optimal for quality lead generation while covering ad spend.

## Key Insights
- **Creative is the new targeting:** Meta's algorithm now uses creative content as the primary signal for audience matching
- **Short-form vertical video dominates:** Accounting for 82% of internet traffic, with 15-45 second Reels-style content performing best
- **"Ugly ads" outperform polished content:** Raw, social-native creatives see 53% higher completion rates than professional productions
- **Advantage+ automation reduces CPL by 12-76%:** Outperforming manual interest targeting in nearly all coaching verticals
- **Self-liquidating economics require $90-110 AOV:** Front-end offers need strategic bumps/upsells to achieve profitability

## Data Points
- **CPM Benchmarks:** $3-$20 for Facebook, $22.50 average for B2B
- **CPA Targets:** <$100 for low-ticket coaching offers
- **Creative Volume:** 30+ new creatives monthly to combat fatigue
- **Ad Completion Rates:** 53% higher for sub-15 second videos
- **Order Bump Conversion:** 30%+ target for $37-$97 bumps

## Contrarian Findings
1. Interest-based targeting is now largely obsolete, with Advantage+ performing better even for niche coaching audiences
2. Higher-priced bumps ($37/$67/$97) convert better than cheaper alternatives
3. Video-only landing pages underperform hybrid formats combining video + text
4. Advantage+ campaigns work for premium coaching offers, not just low-ticket

# Cat Howell Hot Seat — Scaling Low-Ticket Funnels

Source: Cat Howell Profit Lab Hot Seat Call, February 3, 2026

---

## Key Insight: The AOV Ceiling

The hardest thing about low-ticket isn't getting sales — it's scaling.

> "You're not, you're like, I'm just gonna double my budget and I'm gonna double my results. It doesn't fucking work like that."

**Why scaling breaks:**
- When you add spend, Facebook reaches the **unaware segment**
- These people are further from conversion
- CPAs rise, conversion rates drop
- If your AOV can't absorb higher CPAs, you bleed money

**The solution:** Build up AOV *before* trying to scale.

---

## Validation Thresholds

| Sales | Status |
|-------|--------|
| 5-10 | "Onto something" but NOT validated |
| 30 | Minimum to trust split test data |
| 100 | Truly validated — numbers stabilize |

> "The first six sales that come in, I'm like, oh, this page is the winner for sure... But when I leave it 30, 40 sales, sometimes it's the total opposite."

**Implication:** Don't optimize off small samples. Wait for 30+ sales before calling a winner.

---

## AOV Levers

### 1. Order Bumps (Highest Impact)

Cat changed her order bumps and AOV dropped significantly. She tracks everything on a spreadsheet and only noticed because of that tracking.

**Bump vs Upsell positioning matters:**
- Sherry moved an upsell to a bump → it started selling
- Same offer, different placement = different conversion
- Test bump order (17/33 vs 33/17 can have "crazy results")

### 2. Front-End Price Point

> "When I did a $17 promo, my AOV went right down. I was getting more sales, more volume, more customers, higher conversion rate, but they were not buying as many of the order bumps and upsells than when I was selling at $47 or $67."

**The tradeoff:**
- Lower price → Higher conversion rate, lower AOV
- Higher price → Lower conversion rate, potentially higher AOV
- Net effect varies — must test

### 3. Membership Trial as Upsell

Cat sells Profit Lab with a trial. Works well.

> "Maybe test out having a membership upsell where you're doing two months or something and you're charging a hundred bucks up front, which would immediately bring your average order value to like $60, which will put you in the green."

Alternative: "Get 2 months for price of 1"

## Awareness Levels Strategy

### The Spectrum

| Level | Headspace | Messaging |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| **Aware** | "I want [your product]" | Direct sales page works |
| **Solution Aware** | "I need X type of solution" | Handle objections, show why you > alternatives |
| **Problem Aware** | "This isn't working" (symptoms) | Connect symptoms to solution |
| **Unaware** | "Isn't this just how it is?" | Education-first, shift beliefs |

### When You Hit Each Level

- **$100-500/day:** Mostly aware audience, good CPAs, "flow" state
- **$500-1000/day:** CPAs start rising, hitting problem aware
- **$1000+/day:** Definitely hitting unaware, need strategy changes

### Unaware Strategy

1. **Create separate landing page** — doesn't look like a sales page
2. **Educate first** — shift their beliefs, show them they have a problem
3. **Long-form content** — video ads, engagement posts, VSLs
4. **Engagement campaigns** — cheap ($3-25/day), builds omnipresence

> "The sales pages we're working with right now... that's super aware, solution aware. But for unaware, that sales page is gonna be a little bit too intense for them."

## Engagement Campaigns

Cat runs separate campaigns for IG and Facebook placements.

**Structure:**
- $3-25/day budget
- 2 posts per ad set
- Rotate content weekly (check what got organic engagement first)
- Target: Cold audience + Warm (engaged last 30 days)

**Why it works:**
- Cheap (video views/engagement = cheapest objectives)
- Builds omnipresence ("you're everywhere on my feed")
- Social proofs your content
- Auction performance: engagement helps you compete

**Comment CTA tip:** "Comment [WORD] and I'll send you X" drives engagement → improves auction performance. But need ManyChat automation or you'll drown.

## Hyper-Segmentation for Scale

When broad funnel hits ceiling, create specific funnels for each avatar.

**Example:** Instead of "how to sell low-ticket on autopilot" (broad):
- "How to sell low-ticket to fill your agency client base" (specific)

Sometimes only the ad creative changes. Sometimes you need a new landing page. But the back-end offer stays the same.

**Skool example:** "How to grow your card reading membership" — super niche, same product.

---

## Free Stuff Timing

**DON'T do free when first validating:**
> "When you're first getting started out, fuck the free shit. Don't do that. You must sell your offer. Direct response straight up."

**DO free stuff when:**
- Offer is validated (30-100+ sales)
- CPAs are rising from hitting unaware
- You're at $500-1000+/day and need to nurture

Free options: Quiz, webinar, challenge, education page

## Evergreen Challenges

Jan runs live challenges every 2 weeks — burning out.

**Cat's recommendation:** Evergreen it.

**How to maintain urgency:**
- "You only have access for 5 days" (creates consumption urgency)
- Community assignments with rewards (discount, bonus access)
- Weekly live call where people can get hot seats

> "The thing that works really well with live challenges is how people are participating. When you go evergreen, give them assignments and reward that."

## The "Song of Your Soul" Test

> "If you are just chasing the money and it's not a song of your soul, shit gets hard in business... You will give up."

Doesn't need to be your life's purpose. But it has to excite you, put a skip in your step.

**Procrastination as signal:** Sometimes procrastination isn't laziness — it's your body telling you this isn't aligned.

> "Observe that energy and not push against it. A lot of the times it's not a thing that's why you're hitting procrastination. It's not, it doesn't excite you. It's not a song of your soul anymore."

## Sherry's Case Study

**Situation:**
- 114 sales in 30 days (first time doing low-ticket)
- $17 front-end
- $30 CPA
- $20 AOV → Not profitable

**Problem:** Front-end converting but back-end not converting. No membership upsell ready.

**Cat's advice:**
- Need $60+ AOV to be profitable at $30 CPA
- Add membership upsell ($100+ with trial)
- One or two converting upsells at $50-200 would fix it

**Key insight:** Sherry's bumps were selling, upsells weren't. She moved upsell → bump and it started converting. Same offer, different placement.

## Jan's Case Study (400% Funnel)

**Situation:**
- Selling $50 challenge every 2 weeks
- 600+ students, $1200 back-end
- Conversion dropped from 2% → 1-1.5%
- Spending $600-1000/day, only $400 coming in some days

**Problems identified:**
1. Hitting unaware audience at scale
2. Broad targeting ("doctors, entrepreneurs, coaches")
3. Live challenge every 2 weeks = burnout

**Cat's recommendations:**
1. Evergreen the challenge (5-day access window)
2. Hyper-segment: Create specific funnels per avatar
3. Build unaware messaging: "If you're only closing 1% of leads, that's not good"
4. Add free quiz at THIS stage (validated, hitting unaware)

### Pearson's Law: What You Track Enhances

> "What they have shown is that what you track enhances and optimizes. They use this in the sporting industry with athletes."

The act of tracking itself improves results. Not just having a dashboard — manually entering numbers daily forces you to engage with the data.

> "It forces me to get in my numbers every day to get up in personal, rather than just opening a dashboard, looking at it, and then I'm done."

### Facebook Ads Manager Reporting Columns

Set up columns in this order:

1. **Frequency** (FIRST — most important for fatigue)
2. Results
3. Cost per result
4. Purchase ROAS
5. AOV (Average Purchase Conversion Value)
6. Results value (revenue)
7. Amount spent
8. CTR (Link click-through rate)
9. CPC (Cost per link click)
10. Landing page views
11. Cost per landing page view

**How to set up:** Columns → Customize Columns → Search and check each metric → Drag to reorder

---

### Frequency: The Fatigue Indicator

| Frequency | Status | Action |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| < 2 | Healthy | Can scale |
| 2 | Starting to fatigue | Monitor closely |
| 3+ | Fatigued | Do NOT scale — roll out new creatives first |

> "If you try to scale a campaign with fatigued ads, you're gonna blow that up. It's gonna backfire."

**At $1000/day spend:** Refresh creatives at least once a week.

**Quick fix for fatigued winner:** Duplicate the ad and change ONE thing (headline OR image). You know the core works — just create variation.

---

### Why Single Ads Beat Multi-Variant Testing

Multi-variant (testing multiple headlines/images in one ad):
- Pros: Less fatigue, saves time
- Cons: **Impossible to know which creative got the sale**

Single ads:
- Pros: Know exactly what works, can duplicate and tweak
- Cons: More management, faster fatigue

> "When I find a winner, the first thing I'll do is duplicate this and change the headline. Or test a different image. But I know that this variation works. So all I have to do is just tweak one little thing."

---

### Higher CPA Can Mean Better Customers

> "Some of my greatest campaigns with return on ad spend and AOV were my most expensive cost per purchase. So I don't mind paying more money. You're gonna pay more money for a higher quality customer."

**Don't optimize for lowest CPA.** Optimize for:
1. ROAS (are you profitable?)
2. AOV (are customers spending?)

A $97 CPA with $182 AOV (1.87 ROAS) beats a $58 CPA with $100 AOV (1.72 ROAS).

---

### Soft Stats: When to Care

**Click-through rate and CPC matter when:**
- Ad is brand new (first 800-1000 impressions)
- Campaign is tanking
- Diagnosing why something stopped working

**If CPC is $20+ on a new ad:** Kill it immediately. Don't wait.

**If soft stats are tanking:** Usually means frequency is up → ad is fatiguing → need new creatives.

---

### The Profit Rollercoaster Is Normal

Cat showed her profit graph — wild daily swings.

> "I really needed a lot of emotional regulation skills to not turn things off because look at what happened the day after. High profit just from that one funnel netting over a thousand dollars in profit."

**Key insight:** Look at 14-30 day trends, not daily swings. If you're profitable over 30 days, you're doing well — even with multiple dips.

---

### Conversion Rate Calculation

**Formula:** Customers ÷ Landing Page Views = Conversion Rate

Use landing page views, not link clicks. Not everyone who clicks waits to land.

**Cat's benchmark:** 5-6% conversion rate on sales page (with video)

She removed video → conversion dropped. Added video back → returned to 5-6%. Only noticed because of the tracking sheet.

---

### Why You Don't Need Hyros

> "A lot of people are gonna tell you to use tools like Hyros... it's really expensive. If you have CAPI (Conversion API) installed, you don't need any of those tools. Server side tracking is all you need."

Manual spreadsheet + CAPI = sufficient for most low-ticket funnels.

---

### Daily Tracking Workflow

1. Open Ads Manager
2. Set date to yesterday
3. Note: How many customers? (Results column)
4. Note: How much revenue? (Results Value column)
5. Check Ads Reporting for total spend
6. Enter into spreadsheet
7. Review: Did I send an email? Any changes made?
8. Check frequency on active campaigns
9. If frequency > 2: Plan creative refresh

Time: ~10 minutes/day

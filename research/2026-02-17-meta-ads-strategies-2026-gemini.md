# Meta Ads Strategies 2026 — Gemini Deep Research

## One-Sentence Summary

Creative IS targeting post-Andromeda; broad is default; the two-campaign system (sandbox + winners) is consensus; "Silent Review" and "ugly" static ads are outperforming polished content; and naked VSLs are dying — hybrid pages with text + video + visual proof win.

## Key Findings (New Insights Beyond ABO/CBO Research)

### 1. The "Silent Review" Format (Trend of 2026)
User scrolls through the product (PDF, course, spreadsheet) without speaking — facial expressions + text overlays only. Feels native, breaks the pattern of "shouting" marketers. Lowers sales resistance.

**Application for Client Ready:** Screen recording of the 5 AI prompts in action, or scrolling through the offer document template with reaction shots and text overlay.

### 2. "Ugly" Static Ads Outperforming Polished Graphics
Screenshots of tweets, Notes app entries, high-contrast text on plain backgrounds — driving 60-70% of conversions for many accounts. They read as content, not ads.

**Application:** Your one-liners batch is already perfect for this format. Screenshot a one-liner as a Notes app entry or tweet format.

### 3. The "Don't Buy This" Hook
Reverse psychology: "Don't buy this system... if you hate having an offer that sells itself." Filters for high-intent buyers, increases curiosity.

### 4. Naked VSLs Are Dying
Sending cold traffic to a page with ONLY a video is failing. High bounce rates. The winning format is the "Hybrid VSL" — video + long-form readable copy + visual evidence of the product (GIFs, screenshots).

**Application:** Your sales page should show the actual PDF, the AI prompts, the offer document template — visual proof that the thing is REAL.

### 5. The $20 Rule for Low-Ticket Testing
Winners reveal themselves after ~$20 of spend per concept for low-ticket items. Faster than the 72-hour rule at higher budgets.

### 6. GEM (Generative Ad Model) Is Coming
Meta's AI generates entire campaigns from a URL + budget. Commoditizes "average" creative. To win, feed it unique proprietary assets (founder stories, proprietary data) the AI can't hallucinate.

**Implication:** Your case study and personal story are defensible assets. Generic "make money" messaging will get drowned by AI-generated ads.

### 7. Cost Caps for Scaling
High-spend buyers ($5K+/day) use Cost Caps (target CPA bidding) instead of Lowest Cost. Set cap slightly above target CPA — Meta only spends when it finds conversions at that price. Protects profitability.

### 8. 70/20/10 Budget Split
- 70% Scaling (Broad CBO, best creatives)
- 20% Testing (Sandbox)
- 10% Retargeting (optional, or fold into scaling)

### 9. CAPI (Conversions API) Is Critical
Server-side tracking bypasses browser cookie blocking. Improves "Event Match Quality" score, lowers CPMs. High-spend buyers rely on this heavily.

### 10. Radical Creative Variance
Testing minor variations (button colors) is dead. "Radical Variance" = testing entirely different concepts (Silent Review vs Founder Story vs Problem/Solution) to reach different pockets of the broad audience.

## What's NOT Working in 2026

1. **Interest stacking** — restricts AI, raises CPMs without improving CPA
2. **Micro-budget ad sets** — $5/day never exits learning phase (need 50 conversions/week/ad set)
3. **Naked VSL pages** — video-only pages bouncing hard
4. **Manual placements** — "Feed Only" is a mistake, Advantage+ Placements finds cheap inventory
5. **Lookalikes as primary targeting** — algorithm does it better automatically

## Creative Format Rankings (2026)

1. **"Ugly" static text-on-background** — 60-70% of conversions for many accounts
2. **Silent Review** — TikTok-native, high trust
3. **B-roll with text overlay** — still strong
4. **Founder face-to-camera** — essential for brand, not best for cold traffic
5. **AI UGC** — good for volume testing ($100 vs $1000) but buyers spot deepfakes
6. **Human UGC** — highest trust for hero assets

## Landing Page Strategy (2026)

The "Hybrid VSL" structure:
1. Headline (big promise)
2. VSL video (5-15 min, founder + B-roll of product)
3. Long-form text (entire script adapted to readable copy BELOW the video)
4. Visual evidence (GIFs/screenshots of the product being used)
5. Mobile-first (80%+ traffic is mobile)

## Budget Split for $150/day Launch

| Allocation | Budget | Campaign |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Scaling | $105/day (70%) | Broad CBO, 3-5 winning creatives |
| Testing | $30/day (20%) | ABO or CBO sandbox, new concepts |

Note: At launch, flip this — 70% testing, 30% scaling (no winners yet). Reverse ratio once you have proven creatives.

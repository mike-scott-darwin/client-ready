## NEW / sharper than current files

### 1. Operating unit: launch 3 digital snacks at once → double down on winner
Every client launches **3 different low-ticket funnels** simultaneously (~$100–300 spend each), pauses the losers, and doubles down on whichever produces sales. Several clients had 2 of 3 produce sales and were told to run both winners. **Cadence, not single-shot.** Don't launch "the" funnel — launch 3 offers and let the market pick.

### 2. Geo: "Big Six," never US-only, plus a worldwide ad set
- **Big Six = US, UK, Australia, New Zealand, Ireland (+Canada optional).** Tier-1 English-speaking + European.
- US alone = highest CPC. Mixing countries lets Meta distribute for cheaper acquisition.
- Always also run a **worldwide ad set** — often cheapest CPA, biggest scale. Caveat: monitor comments (junk/weird commenters) and sometimes worldwide just doesn't work for a given ad account → tame down to Big Six.
- Note: a client cited *Claude* advising against multi-geo (order-bump cost concerns); Miles overruled it.

### 3. At $10/day: ONE different copy per image (1:1), not same copy ×5
Facebook flags duplicate ads. At $10/day/ad set: **5 images × 5 *different* copies.** Only at **$30–50/day** do you go 5 images × 2 copies (1 long-form, 1 short-form). The "5 images per copy block" rule applies at higher spend, not at launch budget.

### 4. Sales cluster on the FIRST or LAST day of a test
"It either happens on the first day or on the last day." Three sales can land in the final $30 of a $300 test. This is the mechanism behind the $300 rule — people who kill at $270 throw away winners. **Finish the full test.**

### 5. Post-validation optimization ORDER (the money sequence)
After a digital snack gets sales:
1. **Order bumps → 33%+** (optimize copy or replace the bump entirely)
2. **Build 1–2 OTOs** — but ONLY after the low-ticket offer is validated with sales (don't build OTOs for an unproven funnel — wasted effort)
3. **A/B test the front-end headline** (screenshot funnel → AI suggests B variant)
4. **Then** increase budget
- Hard rules:
  - "Do **not relaunch ads before the OTOs are connected** — you will not break even or go profitable without them."
  - "Before your OTO is converting at **5–10%, do not scale your budget.**"
  - A **$113 CPA is fine** *if* bumps + OTOs lift AOV enough to absorb it.
- Tension resolved: order bumps ship with the funnel; **OTOs are a post-validation step**, built only once the front end proves.

### 6. Headline match: ad ↔ sales page (literal)
"Ads fail because the ad says buy pizza and the sales page says buy pasta." Tactic: **screenshot the sales page, put the exact pre-headline + headline into the ad.** Always include 1–3 text-heavy ads that mirror the page headline word-for-word. Then add AI-generated image variants around them.

### 7. ~$2 per qualified click benchmark
Miles pays ~$2/qualified click. **4,400 clicks for a few hundred dollars (sub-$0.10/click) with no sales = junk geo traffic, not a broken sales page.** Diagnostic: ultra-cheap clicks → fix targeting/geo, not the page.

### 8. Meta consolidates budget into one ad (2026 reality)
Meta kills the other ads in a set and dumps budget into one, based on initial impressions (likes/comments/dwell). Everyone on the call hit this.
- Fix: if the budget-hog ad has too-high CPA, **turn off that one ad** (not the whole set) and see if the set recovers — though the set often dies anyway.
- Expect to **launch new ad sets ~weekly** at low/mid budget. Stability comes with higher spend.

### 9. Two-step order form is mandatory
A client wanted to drop the opt-in (show bumps like an open sales page). Miles: "I would **never** run a funnel without a two-step order form." Reasons: enables **abandoned-cart sequences** + **list growth**; 60–70% add to cart and never check out — without the opt-in you can't recover them.

### 10. Daily email = every email is an offer, reply-based, no phone
- Make **every email a CTA/offer** ("reply back to get your offer document").
- Story → tie to the offer → soft reply CTA. Not always urgency (e.g., a story about staying calm in tennis → tie to running ads calmly → "reply if you want to run ads profitably").
- Benchmark: **~2.4% reply rate** across 30 days of daily emails. Low replies = the email messaging isn't there yet (normal early).
- This is the no-phone backend mechanism.

### 11. Stability scales with budget; early grind = ad creation
First ~30 days of low-ticket: most working time is spent making/launching ads. At **$500–700/day** with winning ads, it stabilizes — change less, launch new ads ~once/week. Set expectations accordingly for a relaunch.

## Implications for Client Ready (relaunch as cash flow)

1. **Model is live-validated.** Multiple clients getting sales from low-ticket snacks + bumps on this exact architecture. Client Ready's funnel design is sound.
2. **Adopt the cadence:** launch **3 distinct $47 snacks** (different angles/offers), $10/day each in Big Six + 1 worldwide ad set, finish a $100–300 test each, pause losers, double down on the winner.
3. **Sequence the build:** ship front end + order bumps → validate with sales → THEN build 1–2 OTOs → headline A/B → scale only when OTOs convert 5–10%.
4. **Creative:** lead with text-heavy ads that mirror the landing page headline verbatim; surround with AI image variants; keep the ugly ones in the test.
5. **Keep the two-step opt-in** for abandoned-cart + list.
6. **Daily reply-based email** from day one (~2.4% reply benchmark) as the no-phone ascension path.

# Miles Stutz Apify Scrape Synthesis

**Source:** Apify Meta Ad Library scrape (23 ads, Feb 11 2026)
**Prior research:** `2026-02-10-miles-stutz-ad-library-mining.md` (manual mining)
**Raw data:** `2026-02-11-miles-stutz-meta-ads-apify.json`
**One-sentence summary:** Miles runs two parallel $9 front-end funnels (Digital Snacks + No-Phone Offers) with dramatically more video than manual mining suggested, a comment-to-DM automation path, and dedicated belief-shift ads that pre-sell prospects on paid ads before they ever see a product.

## Key Findings (New — Not in Feb 10 Mining)

### 1. Two Separate Products, Not One
Manual mining treated it as one coaching system. Apify reveals two distinct front-ends with separate domains:
- **digitalsnacks.co** — "Stop being a content slave" ($7-$97 products)
- **phonefreesales.co** — "Close $15K clients without calls" ($9, normally $97)

Different pain points. Different landing pages. Same high-ticket backend. Client Ready has one front-end pathway.

### 2. Video Is 61%, Not 10%
Manual mining estimated ~10% video. Actual: 14 of 23 ads (61%) are video. Massive ramp in Jan-Feb 2026. But many "videos" are 5-9 second animated clips — essentially animated images where the copy does all the selling. Three tiers:
- **Ultra-short (5-9s):** 4 ads — animated scroll-stoppers, copy sells
- **Medium (58s-1:57):** 6 ads — talking head explainers
- **Long (13-20 min):** 4 ads — full behind-the-scenes breakdowns running as cold-traffic ads

### 3. Comment-to-DM Funnel Is Live
3 ads use "comment 'Snacks' below" → DM automation → $17 step-by-step course. This is a third product and a third funnel path. No direct link needed. One ad has zero URL — purely comment-driven.

### 4. "Ads Pay for Themselves" as Deliberate Belief-Shift Campaign
3 ads (Feb 2026) don't sell a product at all. They attack the belief that ads are risky/expensive:
- "I can solve almost every problem inside my business by simply running more ads"
- "Myth: You need thousands. Reality: All of that is bullshit. $10/day is enough."
- "It's free like organic, but with way more scale"

This creates future customers pre-sold on the mechanism before they see a product.

### 5. Anti-Proof Angle (New)
"They don't ask for testimonials. They don't ask for proof. They just need clarity." Reframes proof as a crutch for unclear offers. Sophisticated positioning that aligns with Client Ready's "before the funnel" thesis.

### 6. September 2025 "Content Slave" Copy Is the Proven Winner
Same body copy running across 4 image creatives for 5+ months. No changes. This is his most validated copy block: "I used to be that coach posting 3x daily, responding to DMs at dinner, and giving away my secrets for free..." — with the specific proof: "3,447 Digital Snack Customers → 32 became $2,000+ clients."

### 7. $17 Hidden Third Product
Comment-CTA ads lead to a $17 step-by-step course, separate from the $9 No-Phone Offer. Three price points: $9, $17, and high-ticket backend. Three front-end funnels feeding one backend.

### 8. Explicit AI Mention in Ads
"How I use AI to create ads for me in minutes" and AI for product creation. Positions Miles as forward-thinking. Not in manual mining.

### 9. Strategic Profanity Concentrated in Video
"Stressful as fuck," "All of that is bullshit," "Don't believe the losers." Profanity appears in video ads, not image ads — different tone by format. Deliberate brand voice segmentation.

### 10. Long-Form Video (13-20 min) as Paid Ads
4 ads are YouTube-length content (13-20 minutes) running as cold-traffic Meta ads with "ATTN: Coaches" copy. Full behind-the-scenes breakdowns. Not boosted posts — paid ads designed for deep engagement + comment capture.

## Copy Template (Long-Form No-Phone Offer Ads)

Every long-form ad follows this skeleton:
1. Bold income claim or pain statement
2. "Here's what hit me:" — backstory transition
3. "For [X] years, I thought..." — old belief
4. Grind details (hours, call counts, exhaustion)
5. "But deep down, I knew: This doesn't scale."
6. "Then something clicked:" — pivot
7. "The problem wasn't [X]. The problem was [Y]." — reframe
8. "Think about it:" — rhetorical proof (3 if/then statements)
9. "So I did something different." — action
10. Created ONE document/PDF → sent to X leads → Y bought → $Z revenue
11. "That's when I realized:" — realization
12. Transformation list (arrow-format before/after)
13. Value stack (checkmarks)
14. Social proof: "1,090+ students" / "4.7/5 rating"
15. Price: "$9 (normally $97)"
16. Guarantee: 30-day money-back
17. P.S. reinforcing story

## Implications for Client Ready

### Act On (High Signal)
1. **Belief-shift ads** — Miles runs dedicated ads that change beliefs about MECHANISM (paid ads) before selling product. Client Ready could run "offer clarity" belief-shift ads that sell the mechanism without the product.
2. **Two parallel front-end funnels** — Separate pain points, separate domains, same backend. Client Ready's Offer System ($27) could be complemented by a second entry point targeting a different pain.
3. **Ultra-short video format** — 5-9 second animated clips paired with long copy. Low production, high scroll-stop. Same pattern Cat Howell uses.
4. **Anti-proof angle** — "You don't need testimonials if your offer is clear." Directly applicable to Client Ready's target audience who may not have client proof yet.

### Watch (Interesting, Not Urgent)
5. **Comment-to-DM automation** — Third funnel path requiring ManyChat setup.
6. **Long-form video ads (13-20 min)** — YouTube-length content as paid ads. High-engagement play but requires video production.
7. **Multiple price points ($9, $17, high-ticket)** — Three front-end products feeding one backend. Strategic product ladder.

### Don't Copy
- Revenue claims ($1.4M, $400K/month) without typicality data.
- Strategic profanity in copy — doesn't match Client Ready's voice.
- "91% off" urgency framing — overused in this market.

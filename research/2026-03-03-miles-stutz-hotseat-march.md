# Miles Stutz Hot Seat — March 3, 2026

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Ad Set Level Management (NOT Ad Level)

Don't turn individual ads on/off within ABO ad sets. If an ad set isn't performing, replace the entire ad set with a new one.

> "Whenever I do that, I mess up an ad set. Just look at the performance of an ad set, and if the ad set is not performing, you replace the ad set with a new ad set. Do not go into the ad set and turn off or turn on ads."

**Exception:** CBO campaigns — you can keep adding ads to a single ad set because budget is managed at the campaign level.

### 2. Budget Scaling — 50% Daily Is Now OK

Miles increased winning ad sets by 50% every day during a scaling push and it held. Old 20% rule is outdated.

> "I went ahead and literally increased the budget on the winning ad sets by 50% every day. And for the longest time, people said you can only increase the budget by 20%. Not what's currently the situation."

> "Ad sets that spend more will be more stable, period."

Still recommends monitoring cost closely. 20% is conservative/safe; 50% is aggressive but viable with winning ad sets.

### 3. No OTOs During Validation Phase

Miles validates with a minimal funnel before adding upsells.

> "I only do short form funnel, three order bumps, thank you page. No OTOs."

OTOs are added after validation. This reduces launch complexity and isolates whether the front-end offer converts.

### 4. Pre-Checking Bumps Hurts Conversion

Direct quote from Miles:

> "It only hurts the conversion if you pre-check them."

**Note:** This contradicts Cat Howell's Feb 2026 split test data (pre-checked at 4.0% vs unchecked at 2.3%). Both are directional — test for your own funnel.

### 5. Bump Conversion Target: 30-40% Minimum

Miles expects each individual bump to convert at 30%+ or he optimizes the bump before scaling ads.

> "Your bumps should convert at 30, 40, at like a minimum of 30%."

Margaret's bumps weren't converting. Miles said fix bumps FIRST before doing anything else.

### 6. Sell Bumps Before Building the Product

Dan mentioned testing dummy bumps. Miles confirmed:

> "100% before you create the product."

List the bump on checkout, see if it converts, then build the product. Validates demand before investment.

### 7. Paid Trial > Free Trial for Membership OTO

Miles told Dan to stop offering a free trial and instead use a paid one with self-sorting options:

- Option A: 30-day access for $47
- Option B: 30 days + 1:1 call for $97

> "Instead of framing it as a membership that is MRR, it's like you get 30-day access."

Then send an email after 30 days to continue or cancel. Self-sorting mechanism increases perceived value.

### 8. AI Edition as Bump Product

Miles recommended turning any product into an "AI edition" as a bump:

> "Whatever you've got, turn it into an AI edition and then sell that."

Example: "Use this AI agent to find your niche in 5 minutes." Quick, tangible, tech-forward positioning.

### 9. Specific Payoffs Over Abstract Benefits

Coaching Andy on offer clarity:

> "You can't paint a picture of confidence. You can't paint a picture of overcontrolling. You can't paint a picture of never feeling enough. You must tie those to specific payoffs."

Example: Dating coach selling confidence -> "Approach any woman on the street within 5 seconds." That's a payoff you can paint a picture of.

### 10. Products for Ascension = Quick-Consume PDFs

Lisa asked about product format. Miles:

> "Products that are quick to consume. That's why I love PDFs. You're targeting a very specific problem with a very specific process. Think of an IKEA instruction manual. Very pragmatic, step by step, low on theory. Give people results first, then have them understand why they got results second. These results create ascensions. These products create ascensions the fastest."

### 11. Ad-to-Page Congruence During Validation

> "When you're launching a new digital snack, you want to arrive at the closest data possible to know is the offer in demand. So the first images basically say what the headline is saying on the sales page. 100% congruence between ad and sales page."

**After validation:** Do whatever. Change images, experiment freely. "With a validated digital snack, it's all about childlike curiosity. Be willing to throw stuff at the wall."

> "With cold traffic, your opinion doesn't matter. Your branding generally doesn't matter because people don't know who you are."

### 12. CRO After Validation — The Playbook

Miles showed Margaret the exact sequence:

1. Add 2 text testimonials below the mockup
2. Add "This method works in [X] niches" section listing market segments
3. A/B test ONE change at a time
4. Review data weekly, make one change per week

> "The next question they ask is, is it working? By adding proof, you can answer that question. By adding a guarantee, you can answer that question. The next question is, is this going to work for me? By adding industries, you can answer that question."

### 13. Launch More Ads = Current Meta Philosophy

> "The more often you do it, the better. That's the whole Meta philosophy at the moment. Launch more ads."

Miles does it once a week minimum. Keep winning copy, change images (or vice versa). Feed winning ads into AI to generate similar variations.

### 14. Campaign Structure (Reiterated)

Conversion campaign, 3 ad sets with ABO:
- Ad set 1: Worldwide
- Ad set 2: Lookalike (if available)
- Ad set 3: Country-based targeting

Each ad set: 5 images/videos, each with 1 long-form + 1 short-form copy = 10 ads per ad set.

### 15. CPMs Spiked from Geopolitical Events

> "Let's thank our world leaders for a war that spikes our CPMs no matter what you do. Since those bombs were dropped, my CPMs are like beyond 100 bucks."

External factors cause temporary CPM spikes. Don't kill ads during these periods — wait for normalization.

### 16. B-Roll with Aggressive Text Overlay (Doubled Down)

Miles showed his own ads: random lifestyle footage (eating dinner, walking, driving a car) with aggressive headline overlay + "click the link below."

> "These work for me incredibly well. The amount of comments I'm getting of hateful people... I then get back to the hateful people and I'm arguing with them in the comments. How this breaks down my cost per meal is mind-blowing."

Engagement from arguments in comments causes Facebook to rank ads higher.

### 17. Daily Email = Daily Offer (Early Stage)

> "When I started with this journey, I made an offer by email every single day. Email was a sales channel for me. Now, since I have a copywriter, we're doing a little bit more nurturing. I didn't start with that. I just made offers every single day."

> "Below $3 million a year, probably the most important craft for you to learn is making offers that people buy, period."

### 18. GHL Over Kajabi for Funnels

Miles told Ferenc to stop building funnels in Kajabi and use GHL instead. Keep courses in Kajabi, but all marketing/sales funnels in GHL.

> "Use Kajabi for what it's meant to be, which is a course platform. Use GHL for what it's meant to be, marketing platform to sell people."

### 19. Validation Threshold

Margaret had 2 sales from $150 in ad spend on a minimal funnel. Miles called it close to validated:

> "Within a hundred dollar budget, that looks very promising and I would call it validated."

Plus 2 additional attempted purchases declined by Stripe (high risk flag) — still counts as demand signal.

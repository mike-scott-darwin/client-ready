## The Moat

1. **You eat your own cooking.** Every piece of content, every ad, every presentation is generated from the same reference stack you're teaching. That's unchallengeable proof.

2. **The engine compounds.** vip gets better every week — new skills, new lenses, new automation. Clients benefit from engine improvements without paying more.

3. **Reference files are sticky.** Once a client has 20+ reference files, switching costs are enormous. The system knows their business. Starting over is unthinkable.

4. **Community creates network effects.** Members share reference file patterns. "Here's how I structured my voice.md for a consulting business." Templates emerge organically.

5. **Solo operator advantage.** No team overhead. 95% margins. The AI does the work that would require 3-5 employees at a traditional agency. You're the architect, not the labor.

## Risk

| "Terminal scares people" | Claude Cowork (Anthropic's non-dev agent platform) drops the barrier. Your methodology works with ANY AI tool, not just Claude Code. |
| "Anthropic builds this natively" | They build tools, not methodology. Your value is the curriculum + community, not the file format. |
| "Not enough proof yet" | You ARE the proof. 7 weeks, 42 reference files, automated content engine. First 5 workshop clients become case studies. |
| "Market too small" | $7.7B AI KM market growing 47%/year. You need 75 community members to hit $10K/month. That's a rounding error. |
| "Can't scale 1:1" | Community + workshop are 1:many. Sprint is cohort-based (4-6 people). Accelerator is the only 1:1, and it's high-ticket. |

## Open Questions for Decision

1. **Workshop pricing:** $97 or $197? Lower price = more volume + more sprint conversions. Higher price = better signal of buyer quality.
2. **Community pricing:** $97/month or $197/month? Benchmark: Liam Ottley charges $97, most Skool AI communities are $47-$97.
3. **Sprint format:** 1:1 or cohort? Cohort is more scalable but harder to fill at launch.
4. **V1 → V2 transition:** Kill v1 funnel immediately or run both in parallel?
5. **Paid ads timing:** Start paid ads at launch or wait until organic proves the angle?

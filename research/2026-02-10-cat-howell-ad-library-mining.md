# Cat Howell Ad Library Mining

**Source:** Meta Ad Library screenshot (78 active ads, Feb 2026)
**One-sentence summary:** Cat runs ~78 active ads selling $17-$97 low-ticket offers to coaches/course creators with income-heavy copy, a sticky "ATM machine" metaphor, and 5x the creative volume of Client Ready's current batch.

## Key Findings

### Offer Structure (Comparable to Client Ready)
- $17-$97 low-ticket front end (step-by-step ad campaign setup)
- Upsells to done-for-you and community ($47/mo trial model — same model Client Ready adopted)
- Same target market: coaches, course creators, digital product sellers
- Same mechanism: paid ads + low-ticket funnel + automation
- Direct competitor for cold traffic eyeballs

### Cat's Ad Copy Patterns

**1. "ATM Machine" Metaphor (Dominant)**
- Used across multiple ads: "You've basically created an ATM machine for business"
- Makes abstract outcome (automated ad system) concrete and visual
- Sticky — reader remembers and repeats it
- Client Ready has no equivalent metaphor

**2. Specific Income Numbers (Throughout)**
- "Making 3500/day from my digital courses"
- "Business did $96,000 in low ticket sales"
- "$300+ spent from $30K ad spend"
- "Stripe notifications while you sleep from $17-$97 offers"
- Builds visceral desire through concrete revenue outcomes

**3. Personal Revenue Story Arc (Strongest Pattern)**
- Before: "Dabbling in low ticket for over five years, selling live workshops or challenges ranging anywhere from $44 to $1,000"
- Turning point: "I was hesitant to run ads — mostly because I still had a lot of residual fear"
- After: "By September 2021 I was making 3500/day from my digital courses (spending about 1500/day on ads)"
- Result: "Last month my business did $96,000 in low ticket sales and spent $30,000 on ads"
- Lifestyle: "Didn't have to be on social media four times a day, didn't have to ever sell anything high ticket on the block"

**4. Benefit Bullet Stacking**
- "You can test and validate very quickly with low ticket offers"
- "It's never been easier to advertise with Meta ads (you just need to provide it with specific messaging)"
- "Meta LOVES low offers — because the Purchase event gets fired A LOT, and the algorithm loves this"
- Scannable mid-ad bullet sections that a skimmer can grab without reading narrative

**5. Ad Format Variety**
- Short copy (3-5 lines) alongside long-form narrative
- Image overlays with text ("How to get paid to acquire new customers")
- Multiple creatives per ad (carousel-style with different value props)
- "Learn More" CTA buttons on most ads
- Some "Comment ADD" engagement CTAs

**6. CTA Patterns**
- "Grab it here: [link]" (most common)
- "Comment ADD and I'll send it your way"
- "Access training here: [link]"
- "Grab the training here: [link]"
- Varies CTA language more than Client Ready does

### Volume
- ~78 active ads at time of capture
- Multiple versions of the same angle with different creative treatments
- Tests at roughly 5x Client Ready's current volume (14 primaries)

## What Cat Does That Client Ready Doesn't

| Gap | Cat's Approach | Client Ready Current |
|-----|---------------|---------------------|
| Sticky metaphor | "ATM machine for business" | No dominant metaphor |
| Income numbers | Specific revenue throughout | Avoids income claims (no personal data yet) |
| Short-form ads | 3-5 line versions alongside long-form | Mostly narrative long-form (P4s are closest) |
| Benefit bullets | Scannable mid-ad bullet stacks | Benefits embedded in narrative paragraphs |
| Creative volume | ~78 active | 14 primaries + 14 headlines |
| Format variety | Text overlays, carousels, multiple creatives | Copy-only (image prompts separate) |

## What Client Ready Does That Cat Doesn't

| Advantage | Client Ready | Cat |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Testimonial depth | 6 named people, direct quotes, specific situations | Generic "clients making $X" |
| FTC compliance | Disclaimers distinguishing coaching results from $27 product | Income claims without visible disclaimers |
| Unaware targeting | Dedicated belief-shift and education ads (P6, P7) | Almost all assumes problem/solution awareness |
| Story variety | Each ad tells a different narrative arc | Repetitive personal revenue story |
| Intellectual honesty | "Self-guided. Results depend on your effort." | Implies system alone creates results |

## Implications for Reference Files

### Potential New Work
1. **Metaphor development** — Client Ready needs a sticky metaphor equivalent to "ATM machine." Something visual that captures the offer extraction outcome.
2. **Short-form ad variations** — Add 3-5 line versions of existing primaries. More testing surface without new angles.
3. **Benefit stacking format** — Add bullet-format benefit sections to narrative ads (P3 Direct Response already does this — extend to P1, P2).
4. **Outcome visualization without numbers** — Cat uses income. Client Ready can paint the feeling: "Wake up to a client who found you, understood your offer, and paid — without a single DM." Desire creation without FTC risk.
5. **Volume increase** — Even doubling to 28 primaries with format variations dramatically improves testing surface.

### What NOT to Change
- Testimonial integration is stronger than Cat's. Keep it.
- Unaware ads (P6, P7) are a strategic advantage Cat doesn't have. Keep them.
- FTC disclaimers build trust with the exact audience burned by guru promises. Keep them.
- Don't add income claims without real revenue data. Cat's approach works for her because she has years of documented results. Client Ready doesn't have that yet.

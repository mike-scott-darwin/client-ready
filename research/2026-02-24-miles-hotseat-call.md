# Miles Stutz Hot Seat Call — Feb 24 2026

## Source

Impromptu Google Meet hot seat call. 70 minutes. Miles reviewed ads, funnels, and sales pages for Dave Bess, Matthew, Michael Timinger, Shamayah, Atty Cronin, Raj Mehta, and Dan Thomas.

## Key New Insights

### Over-Converting Bumps = Headline Signal (Biggest Takeaway)

Miles told Michael Timinger: if your order bump converts at 40%+, that is a signal. Whatever you are saying in the bump copy should be pulled UP into the front-end headline.

**His example:** A bump about "use my AI prompt that tells you what you should be selling" converted at 43%. He changed the headline to include "AI-powered system to turn what you used to give away for free into Digital Snacks." Immediate winner.

**The principle:** Bumps are a low-stakes testing ground. High bump conversion means the language resonates. Use that language in the highest-leverage position (the headline) to increase overall throughput.

**Application to Client Ready:** Once bumps have data, check which bump converts highest. That copy tells you what cold traffic actually wants. Pull that language into the front-end headline and ad copy.

### Audience Network Bot Traffic (Active Meta Issue)

Meta's Audience Network is currently generating significant bot traffic. Karin confirmed it has been going on for weeks and is not fully fixed.

**Symptoms:** Suspiciously cheap clicks (0.09 per click), high click volume with zero sales, most spend coming from Audience Network placement.

**Fix:** Turn off Audience Network in placements. Also remove worldwide targeting. These two changes fix most bot traffic issues.

**For Client Ready launch:** Ensure Audience Network is OFF from day one. Do not wait for data to confirm -- it is a known current issue.

### Scale Winners Before Launching New Creative

Miles said he made this mistake "for a very long time" -- always launching new ad sets instead of increasing the budget on winning ones. The goal is to get winners out of Meta's learning phase by spending more on what works.

**Recommendation:** Do both (scale winners AND launch new creative), but do not sleep on budget increases for winning ad sets. A winning ad set with more budget gets more stable.

### Headline Formula (Live Rewrites)

Miles rewrote headlines for multiple people on the call. Consistent pattern:

**Structure:** [Action verb] + [process/system name] + to [specific tangible outcome] + in [timeframe] + guaranteed

**Sub-headline:** The only [system] that [outcome] without [pain 1], [pain 2], [pain 3]

**Examples from the call:**
- "Discover how to lose 10 pounds in 10 days simply by adding potatoes"
- "Follow this 10-day potato protocol to lose 10 pounds in 10 days guaranteed"
- "Launch the daily LinkedIn lead system and get 15-25 qualified leads every week"
- "Use this instant mind hack to instantly quit [habit] even if you have tried everything before guaranteed"

### Sales Page Minimalism

For Raj's page, Miles said: "Have you ever heard the statement, you can say too much?"

**Recommended structure:** Clear headline, testimonial, testimonial, who it works for section, more testimonials. Strip out explanation sections. Let social proof carry the page.

### Payment Gateway Matters

Raj asked about only having PayPal (no Stripe) for international payments. Miles confirmed: "It's definitely an issue because the more payment options you provide, the more sales you're going to get."

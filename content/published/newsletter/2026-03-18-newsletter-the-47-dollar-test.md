# The $47 Test

**Subject line:** Why I don't start with high-ticket

**Preview text:** $47 taught me more than any $5K client.

---

Every guru says the same thing: "Start with high-ticket. Charge what you're worth. $5K minimum."

I'm a coach. I charge high-ticket. And I'm telling you — don't start there.

Here's why.

When you sell a $5K offer to your first client, you learn one thing: someone paid you $5K. You don't know why they bought. You don't know if the offer was clear or if they just liked you. You don't know if it'll work again.

Sample size: 1. Data: useless.

When you sell a $47 offer to 30 people, you learn everything.

---

**What $47 teaches you that $5K can't:**

1. **Who actually buys.** Not who you think your audience is — who actually pulls out their card. At $47, the barrier is low enough that people buy based on the offer, not the relationship. If they're buying, the offer is working.

2. **What words make them buy.** You can test 4 different hooks in a week at $47. At $5K, testing takes months. Speed of learning compounds.

3. **What they actually need next.** Every $47 buyer tells you what they want more of. That's your $5K offer — built by demand, not by guessing.

4. **Whether you can sell without a sales call.** If your $47 offer needs a 30-minute explanation, your $5K offer will need an hour. Clarity at $47 scales to clarity at $5K.

---

The coaches I work with who scale fastest all follow the same pattern:

$47 first. Learn. Then $5K.

Not because they're not "worth" $5K. Because they're smart enough to test cheap and scale expensive.

Think of it like this: $47 is the R&D budget for your $5K offer. You're not selling cheap — you're buying information.

30 sales at $47 = $1,410 in revenue + enough data to build a $5K offer you know people want.

0 sales at $5K = $0 in revenue + a theory.

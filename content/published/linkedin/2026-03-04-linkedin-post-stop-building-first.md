# LinkedIn: Stop Building First

Every coach I've worked with made the same mistake.

Great idea. 3 months building it. Course modules. Workbooks. A logo. Then they launched.

Crickets.

Not because the idea was bad. Because they never tested it.

Engineers don't assume. They validate. Coaching should work the same way.

Before you build anything:
1. Write the offer in one sentence
2. Call 5 people who fit the profile
3. Ask: "Would you pay $Z for this?"
4. 3 out of 5 say yes — build it
5. Less than 3 — rewrite and test again

No course. No funnel. Just a conversation.

The coaches charging $5K+ didn't build first. They validated first. The building was the easy part.

The most expensive thing in coaching isn't a bad funnel. It's a good course nobody wants.

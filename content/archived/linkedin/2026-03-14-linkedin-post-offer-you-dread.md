# LinkedIn: If You Dread Delivering It, It Is the Wrong Offer

A coaching offer should not feel like a sentence.

But I talk to coaches every week who describe their own offer like it is a punishment. The calls drain them. The delivery bores them. The clients frustrate them.

And their solution? Push through. Optimize. Hire a VA. Automate the parts they hate.

Wrong.

If the core delivery makes you want to cancel your calendar, no amount of optimization fixes that. You built the wrong offer.

This is not a mindset problem. It is an alignment problem.

The right offer sits at the intersection of three things: what you are genuinely good at, what your audience will pay for, and what you can deliver without white-knuckling through every week.

Miss any one of those three, and the business becomes a trap.

I know because I have been there. I built an offer that sold well and made me miserable. Tore it down. Rebuilt from the intersection.

The rebuild was uncomfortable. But every Monday since has been better than the one before.

If your offer feels wrong, trust that feeling. The foundation matters more than the revenue.

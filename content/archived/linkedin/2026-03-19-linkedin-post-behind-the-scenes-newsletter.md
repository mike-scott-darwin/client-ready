# Behind the Scenes: The Newsletter

I started a newsletter three weeks ago. Here's what actually happened.

Week one: 47 subscribers. Most of them people I already knew. Open rate was solid because small lists always look good. Wrote about validation-first offer creation. Took me four hours to write because I was overthinking every sentence.

Week two: 83 subscribers. A few came from X, a few from LinkedIn. I cut my writing time in half by starting with a single idea instead of trying to cover everything. The post about "you can't grow into pain" got the most replies.

Week three: 112 subscribers. Nothing viral. No big moment. Just steady, boring growth from showing up and being useful.

Here's what I've learned so far.

The newsletter is the engine. Social is discovery. I write one good newsletter per week, then break it into posts for LinkedIn and X. One input, multiple outputs.

The subscribers who reply are worth ten times more than the ones who just open. Those replies turn into real conversations. Some of those conversations turn into clients.

I'm not sharing this because the numbers are impressive. They're not. I'm sharing it because the real version of building something is slower and messier than anyone posts about.

More updates as this thing grows. Follow along if you want the unfiltered version.

# The Wrong Offer

I spent six months building a coaching program I hated delivering.

On paper it made sense. The market wanted it. I had the skills. The price was right. I even got clients.

But every Monday morning I'd stare at my calendar and feel dread. Not the normal resistance that comes with hard work -- actual dread. The kind where you start hoping clients cancel.

I told myself to push through. "This is just the hard part," I said. "Once I scale, it'll get easier." That's what the gurus say, right? Hustle now, enjoy later.

It didn't get easier. It got worse.

I finally understood something that changed how I build offers: you can't grow into pain. If the work feels wrong at 3 clients, it will feel catastrophic at 30.

The problem wasn't my marketing. It wasn't my pricing. It wasn't my funnel. It was that the offer was misaligned with who I actually am.

When I scrapped it and rebuilt around what I genuinely wanted to teach -- how to validate and create offers that fit -- everything shifted. Not overnight. But the dread was gone. I could show up without white-knuckling through every session.

If you're forcing yourself to deliver something that drains you, the answer isn't "try harder." The answer is a different offer.

The search isn't the failure. Building something misaligned is.

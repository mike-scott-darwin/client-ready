## The Misalignment Trap

Last week, I talked to three coaches. All successful in their 9-to-5s. All struggling to get their coaching business off the ground.

The pattern was identical:
- Build an offer (based on what others are doing)
- Create content (because gurus say you should)

- Wonder if you're just not cut out for this

Sound familiar?

Here's what nobody tells you: If it hurts to show up, you'll eventually burn it down.

## The 15-Minute Discovery

Yesterday, I helped a client extract her real offer. Not the one she thought she should build. The one that was already there.

The conversation went like this:

"What do you research when no one's watching?"
"Systems. Always systems. I'm obsessed with making things simpler."

"What problems do people constantly ask you about?"
"How to stop overthinking and just get started."

"When do you lose track of time?"
"When I'm helping someone turn their mess into a clear plan."

See the pattern?

She wasn't a "business coach" or a "mindset coach." She was a Clarity Architect. Someone who turns chaos into systems that actually run.

That's an offer that sells itself.

## Why This Matters

Your zone of genius isn't something you build. It's something you extract.

It's already there – in the questions people ask you, the rabbit holes you fall into, the problems you solve without thinking.

The trick isn't creating it. It's seeing it.

## What to Do Today

Here's a 2-minute test:

1. What do you research when no one's watching? (Not for content. Not for clients. Just because you want to know.)

2. What intersections excite you when you find them? (When your expertise overlaps with something unexpected – does it light you up?)

3. What decisions feel like discovery vs obligation? (When you're making choices about your business – does it feel like play or homework?)

If the overlaps don't make sense, you probably have the wrong offer.

That's okay. Better to know now than six months into building something you'll eventually abandon.

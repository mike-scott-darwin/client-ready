# The DM Playbook

**Subject line:** 5 DMs will outsell 30 posts

**Preview text:** Stop posting into the void. Start conversations.

---

I'm going to say something that might make content creators uncomfortable.

Your last 30 posts generated zero revenue. Five real conversations would have generated at least one client.

I'm not guessing. I'm doing the math.

---

Here's what happens when you post content and hope:

You write something. You optimize the hook. You stress about the algorithm. You post. You check the likes. Maybe you get 47 hearts and a "So needed this!" comment. Revenue? Zero.

Here's what happens when you have five real conversations:

5 conversations → 2 people say "Tell me more" → 1 becomes a client.

That's not a theory. That's the conversion rate I've seen over and over. One client from five conversations. Try getting that ratio from Instagram posts.

---

**But here's where people mess it up.**

They hear "DMs" and they think cold outreach. The "Hey I noticed your profile and I think you'd be a great fit for my program" energy. Gross. Delete. Block.

That's not what I'm talking about.

I'm talking about genuine conversations with people who are already showing you they need help. They're commenting on your posts. They're watching your stories. They're asking questions in groups. They're raising their hand — you're just not noticing.

---

**The framework is simple:**

1. **Notice engagement.** Someone comments, reacts, or asks a question. They already did the hard part.
2. **Ask a real question.** Not "Can I help you?" Try: "That's a great question — what have you tried so far?"
3. **Listen.** Actually listen. Don't pitch. Don't pivot. Just hear them.
4. **Offer help.** If — and only if — you can genuinely help, say: "I actually built something for exactly this. Want me to send you the details?"

That's it. No scripts. No manipulation. No 47-step follow-up sequence.

---

**Why this works better than content:**

Content is one-to-many. DMs are one-to-one. Content builds awareness. DMs build trust.

You need both. But if you had to pick one to pay rent this month? Five genuine conversations will beat 30 posts every single time.

The coaches I know who are consistently signing clients aren't the ones with the most followers. They're the ones having the most conversations.

---

Here's your homework: this week, have five real conversations. Not pitches. Conversations. Notice who's engaging with your content, ask them a question, and listen.

Then reply to this email and tell me what happened. I want to hear about it.

— Michael

P.S. The best part about conversations? They also tell you exactly what your audience wants. Which makes your content better. Which starts more conversations. It's a loop — not a grind.

# You Can't Grow Into Pain

**Subject line:** I burned down a business that was working

**Preview text:** Best decision I ever made.

---

I built a coaching offer that made money.

And I burned it down.

Not because it wasn't profitable. Because every client call felt like a different kind of 9-to-5. The one I was supposed to be escaping.

I told myself the usual things. "Push through." "It gets easier at scale." "You just need better systems."

Wrong.

You can't grow into pain. If it hurts at $5K/month, it'll hurt worse at $50K/month. You don't get used to misalignment. You just get better at ignoring it — until you can't.

Here's what nobody warns you about when you build a coaching business:

**Making money from an offer doesn't mean it's the right offer.**

If you dread client calls. If you keep tweaking the positioning because something feels off. If you'd rather scroll your phone than show up for the work — that's not burnout. That's misalignment. Your offer is built on the wrong foundation.

---

The rebuild started with one question: What do I actually want to help people do?

Not "what's profitable." Not "what's trending on Skool." What feels like pull instead of push.

That question led me to Client Ready. An offer I can show up for without white-knuckling through every delivery.

**The difference between an offer that makes money and an offer that makes money AND doesn't destroy you:**

1. It's built on skills you actually enjoy using — not skills you happen to have
2. You'd help this specific person for free — payment is a bonus, not the only reason you show up
3. You can explain it in one sentence without cringing
4. After a client call, you feel energy, not drainage

If you're failing 2 or more of those, it doesn't matter how good your funnel is. You're scaling the wrong thing.

---

The first prompt in the Client Ready system isn't about marketing or positioning. It's about alignment. "Find Your Zone of Genius." Because if the foundation is wrong, everything you build on top of it will crack.

Most people skip this step. They jump straight to "How do I get clients?" Wrong question. Right question: "Am I building something I won't abandon in 6 months?"

Test that first.

# Your First $5K Client

**Subject line:** The path from $47 to $5K isn't what you think

**Preview text:** You don't raise prices. You raise proof.

---

Everyone wants to charge $5K. Almost nobody has earned the right to.

That sounds harsh. It's not. It's the most useful thing I can tell you today.

---

Here's what most coaches do: they read a post about premium pricing, get excited, slap $5,000 on a 12-week program they haven't sold yet, and wonder why nobody bites.

The price isn't the problem. The proof is.

Nobody pays $5K to someone who hasn't demonstrated they can deliver a result. It doesn't matter how good your framework is. It doesn't matter how much you believe in it. Without proof, $5K is a gamble — and your client knows it.

---

**So how do you get to $5K?**

You climb the ladder. Each rung earns you the next one.

**Rung 1: $47 validates the idea.** Someone gives you money. Not a lot. But real money for a real offer. This is where you prove that people will pay for what you know. If you can't sell at $47, you definitely can't sell at $5K.

**Rung 2: $297 validates your system.** Now you're not just selling information — you're selling a process. A sprint. A done-with-you experience. This is where you prove your method works in a structured format. Where you get testimonials that say "I followed the process and got [specific result]."

**Rung 3: $5K validates the transformation.** Now you have proof. Multiple people who followed your system and got results. Testimonials with numbers. Case studies with timelines. You're not asking someone to take a chance on you — you're showing them what's already happened for people just like them.

Each rung earns you the next. Skip a rung and you fall.

---

**When to make the jump to $5K:**

Not when you feel ready. When the evidence says you're ready.

- You've delivered consistent results at the $297 level
- You have 3+ testimonials with specific outcomes
- You can articulate the transformation in one sentence
- People are already asking you for something more

That's the signal. Not a feeling. Not a guru telling you to "charge your worth." Evidence.

---

**The uncomfortable truth:**

Charging $5K isn't about confidence. It's about track record.

The coaches who charge $5K with confidence didn't wake up one morning and decide to be premium. They started at $47. Proved it worked. Moved to $297. Proved it worked again. Then $5K was obvious — for them and their clients.

You don't get to $5K by deciding you're worth it. You get there by proving it. Start here.

# The Coaching Graveyard

**Subject line:** Beautiful websites. Zero sales. Sound familiar?

**Preview text:** The graveyard is full of coaches who built before they tested.

---

There's a graveyard out there. You can't see it, but it's massive.

It's full of coaching businesses that died. Not from bad ideas. From never testing them.

---

**Here's what's in the graveyard:**

Beautiful websites that cost $3,000 and got 12 visitors. Six-month signature programs that nobody enrolled in. Perfect brand guidelines — colors, fonts, mood boards — for a business with zero clients. Content calendars planned out 90 days in advance for an audience that doesn't exist yet.

Every single one of these coaches had a good idea. Real expertise. Genuine ability to help people.

And every single one of them built the building before checking if anyone wanted to walk through the door.

---

**The pattern is always the same:**

Step 1: Get excited about an idea.
Step 2: Spend 3-6 months building everything around it.
Step 3: Launch to silence.
Step 4: Assume the idea was bad.
Step 5: Start over with a new idea.
Step 6: Repeat.

The idea was probably fine. The order was wrong.

---

**Here's what validation actually looks like:**

It's not an Instagram poll. "Would you buy this?" means nothing. People say yes to polls the way they say "We should get coffee sometime." They won't follow through.

Validation is real money. Real transactions. Real humans pulling out a credit card and saying "Yes, I want this."

That's the only vote that counts.

Not likes. Not comments. Not "OMG I need this." Money.

---

**The antidote to the graveyard is embarrassingly simple:**

Before you build the website, the course, the brand, the funnel — sell the thing.

Write the offer in one sentence. Take it to five real people. Ask for money. If three say yes, build it. If they don't, rewrite and test again.

Total time: one afternoon. Total cost: nothing but your ego.

Compare that to three months and $5,000 building something nobody asked for. The math isn't hard.

---

**I know this is uncomfortable.**

Testing means you might hear no. Building feels productive. Testing feels vulnerable.

But building without testing is just expensive procrastination. You're not making progress — you're avoiding the only question that matters: will someone pay for this?

# Why I'll Never Tell You to Quit Your 9-to-5

**Subject line:** The most dangerous advice in coaching

**Preview text:** Your job isn't the enemy. Bad strategy is.

---

Every week I see a coach on Instagram tell their audience to "take the leap." Burn the boats. Quit your job. Trust the universe.

I will never tell you that.

Not because I don't believe in you. Because I've done the math.

---

Here's the math:

Average coach making $0-$2K/month on the side. Rent is $1,800. Health insurance is $400. Food, car, phone — another $1,200. That's $3,400/month in non-negotiable expenses.

Someone on the internet who doesn't know your name tells you to quit your job. You do. Now you have a runway of... whatever's in your savings. And a coaching business that hasn't proven it can feed you.

That's not a leap of faith. That's a math problem. And the answer is no.

---

**I built Client Ready while keeping a day job.**

Not because I lacked ambition. Because I had a mortgage. And a family. And the discipline to know that ambition without revenue is just a hobby with pressure.

The 9-to-5 wasn't the enemy. It was the safety net that let me test, fail, adjust, and test again — without panicking about rent.

That's not weakness. That's engineering.

Engineers don't remove the scaffolding until the building stands on its own. Your job is scaffolding. Your coaching business is the building. Don't tear one down before the other is ready.

---

**Here's when you quit:**

Not when you feel ready. Not when a guru tells you to. When the numbers say so.

- 3-6 months of consistent revenue that covers your expenses
- Savings runway of at least 6 months on top of that
- A pipeline that doesn't depend on one platform or one launch

That's the checklist. Not vibes. Not vision boards. Revenue, runway, pipeline.

---

**The coaches who make it long-term?** They didn't leap. They built a bridge. One plank at a time. Side hustle → validated offer → consistent clients → full-time.

It's not as exciting as "I quit my job on stage at a Tony Robbins event." But it works. And it keeps working.

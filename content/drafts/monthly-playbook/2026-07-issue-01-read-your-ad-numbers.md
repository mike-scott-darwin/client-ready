# The Self-Liquidating Funnel: Make Your Ads Pay for Themselves (Before a Single Upsell)

**Subject line:** make your ads pay for themselves (before OTOs)

**Preview text:** My real $27 front end + $37/$67/$97 bumps, dissected — the exact stack that pushes AOV past ad cost.

---

*The Monthly Playbook · Issue #1 · One skill. My real funnel. Plain English.*

**TLDR:** A "self-liquidating" funnel means your **front-end offer + order bumps** cover what you paid to get the buyer — so acquisition trends toward free and everything after (OTOs, community) is profit. This issue: I dissect my **real $27 front end and my real $37 / $67 / $97 order bumps** — why each is built the way it is, why the first bump converts at **44%**, and how to read from your own ad numbers whether you're liquidating. Bumps first. OTOs after.

The mistake almost everyone makes: they bolt a $197 upsell onto a front end that doesn't pay for itself. **Wrong order.** You get the front end + bumps working *first* — that's the highest-intent, highest-leverage moment in the whole funnel — *then* OTOs are gravy. An OTO can't save a leaky checkout.

Here's exactly how I built mine.

## Part 1 — My $27 front end, and why it's built to be irresistible

**The offer: the Client Ready Offer System — "validate your $5K offer in one afternoon."** Five deliberate levers:

1. **Result + speed, not a topic.** Not "an offer course" — *"your validated offer, in one afternoon."*
2. **Digital-snack price.** $27 is an impulse — bought without asking anyone. (Honest note: at $27 the front end is a *buyer-acquisition* price; it doesn't fully liquidate alone — the bumps + ladder do. More below.)
3. **Brutally narrow.** For coaches stuck under $5k — not "entrepreneurs."
4. **Stacked, anchored value.** ~~$197~~ → $27, itemized so it *feels* like $197.
5. **A named mechanism.** "The Offer System," not "a PDF."

## Part 2 — My 3 bumps, dissected (and why Bump 1 hits 44%)

Each bump is the **literal next step** after the last. That's the whole secret to a high take-rate — it feels like *finishing the purchase*, not a second sale.

**🟢 Bump 1 — $37 · First-Sale DM Scripts** ~~($197)~~
You just built your offer. Now what? Get the first sale. → 10 copy-paste DM scripts to send your warm list this week.
→ **Take-rate: ~44%.** Why so high: cheapest, most relevant, and it's the *immediate* next action. **This is the bump you optimize toward 50%.**

**🟡 Bump 2 — $67 · Plug & Play Sales Page Kit** ~~($297)~~
DMs start conversations — a page closes them. → templates the AI auto-fills *from the offer doc they just built.* The "do it faster, skip the blank page" bump.

**🔴 Bump 3 — $97 · The $5K Client Close Scripts** ~~($497)~~
For the bigger client — the word-for-word high-ticket close + 3 real annotated calls. Highest price, lowest take-rate, and that's fine — it catches the ready buyer.

**The five rules those bumps follow — steal them:**
1. **Completes the last purchase** (relevance = 80% of take-rate).
2. **Ordered small → large** ($37 → $67 → $97). Cheapest yes first; commitment compounds.
3. **Sells speed / done-for-you** ("skip the blank page," "AI fills it").
4. **One sentence, price on the checkbox, never pre-checked.**
5. **Anchored value** ($197 pack → $37). A price with nothing to compare to is just a cost.

## The honest part: $27 doesn't liquidate alone — and that's on purpose

At $27, even with bumps, cold traffic won't fully pay for itself day one. **That's a deliberate choice:** price low → maximum buyers → recover across the *whole ladder* — bumps, then the $197 OTO, then $47/mo community + this $37/mo newsletter. I judge it on **90-day value per buyer (~$240)**, not front-end AOV.

The sequence still holds: **get the front end + bumps as close to CPA as you can first** (that's Part 1 + 2), *then* the OTOs and continuity finish the job. Bumps before OTOs — always.

> **The math to run:** (front-end price + average bump revenue per buyer) vs CPA. The closer to 1, the more you can scale on cold traffic before the backend even kicks in.

- **Sequence: front end → bumps → THEN OTOs.** Never bolt an upsell onto a leaky checkout.
- **Bump 1 = the literal next step** = my 44% take-rate. Relevance beats discount.
- **One bump ≈ 2× the buyer** ($24.74 → $51.80, in my real data).
- **AOV vs CPA** tells you, in five seconds, whether you can scale.

**FAQ**

**My $27 doesn't liquidate — am I doing it wrong?** No — low front ends rarely do alone. Get bumps covering most of CPA, then let OTOs + continuity finish it. Judge on 90-day value, not the first sale.

**How many bumps?** 2–3, each a real next step. If one feels bolted-on, cut it — refunds are your annoyance meter.

# The Self-Liquidating Funnel: Make Your Ads Pay for Themselves (Before a Single Upsell)

**Subject line:** make your ads pay for themselves (before OTOs)

**Preview text:** My real ad + checkout numbers — the front-end and bump combo that turns a $17 buyer into a $50 cart.

---

*The Monthly Playbook · Issue #1 · One skill. My real funnel. Plain English.*

**TLDR:** A "self-liquidating" funnel means your **front-end offer + order bumps** claw back what you paid to get the buyer — so acquisition trends toward free and everything after (OTOs, community) is profit. This issue, with my real numbers: why my ads look scary on their own, how my order bumps turn a ~$17 buyer into a **~$44–50 cart**, and why my best bump converts at **44%.** Bumps first. OTOs after.

The mistake almost everyone makes: they bolt a $197 upsell onto a front end that doesn't pay for itself. **Wrong order.** You get the front end + bumps working *first* — the highest-intent moment in the whole funnel — *then* OTOs are gravy. An OTO can't rescue a leaky checkout.

Here's my real funnel, both sides.

---

## Side 1: the scary side — my ad costs

![My ad account — real numbers](https://assets.buttondown.email/images/8b9cfbd3-23cd-4a58-92cc-82dca2e7ea70.png)
*My account, early tests — a case, not a promise.*

On the ad side alone this looks rough: **cost per purchase $65–$122**, ROAS below 1.0. If you only ever look at Ads Manager, you'd panic and kill everything.

The number Ads Manager buries is **AOV — Average Order Value** (what a buyer actually spends). *It's not a default column — build it: Columns → Customize → Create Custom Metric → `Purchases Conversion Value ÷ Purchases`.* Because the real story isn't on the ad side — it's at the **checkout.**

## Side 2: the checkout — where the funnel actually makes its money

Look what the bumps do. A **~$17 front end** turns into an **average cart of ~$44–50** — the buyer more than *doubles* in value the moment they check out. That lift is the whole game, and it's 100% order bumps.

**The standout: my best bump converts at 44%.** It's the **"AI Offer Extraction + 24-Hour Launch" bump** — a *done-for-you / do-it-faster* offer. The DIY-flavored bumps (templates, playbook) sat around 14%. **Lesson: the done-for-you bump wins, every time.**

---

## Part 1 — Make the front end impossible to scroll past

My $27 **Client Ready Offer System** — "validate your $5K offer in one afternoon." Five deliberate levers:

1. **Result + speed, not a topic.** *"Your validated offer, in one afternoon"* — not "an offer course."
2. **Digital-snack price.** Cheap enough to buy on impulse, no permission needed.
3. **Brutally narrow.** For coaches stuck under $5k — not "entrepreneurs."
4. **Stacked, anchored value.** ~~$197~~ → $27, itemized so it *feels* like $197.
5. **A named mechanism.** "The Offer System," not "a PDF."

---

## Part 2 — Order bumps that hit 44% (the 5 rules)

1. **It completes the purchase they just made.** They built the offer → the bump *launches* it for them. Relevance = 80% of take-rate.
2. **Sell done-for-you / speed.** My 44% bump does the work *for* them ("AI extracts it + 24-hour launch"). People pay to skip the effort — that's why it beat the DIY bumps 3-to-1.
3. **Ordered small → large.** Cheapest yes first; commitment compounds.
4. **One sentence, price on the checkbox, never pre-checked.** A bump is a reflex, not a decision.
5. **Anchor the value.** "$197 pack → $37, only at checkout." A price with nothing to compare to is just a cost.

Stack 2–3 of these and your ~$17 buyer becomes a ~$50 cart — exactly what my checkout shows.

---

## The honest part: $27 doesn't liquidate alone — on purpose

Notice: my cart (~$44–50) is still *below* what a buyer cost me on those ads ($65–122). At a $27 entry, cold traffic won't fully pay for itself day one — **that's a deliberate choice.** Price low → maximum buyers → recover across the *whole ladder*: bumps, then the $197 OTO, then $47/mo community + this $37/mo newsletter. I judge it on **90-day value per buyer (~$240)**, not the first sale.

The sequence still rules: **get front end + bumps covering as much of CPA as you can first,** *then* OTOs and continuity finish the job. Bumps before OTOs — always.

> **The math:** (front-end price + average bump revenue per buyer) vs CPA. The closer to 1, the more you can scale on cold traffic before the backend even kicks in.

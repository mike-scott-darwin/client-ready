## The WHAT: why that bump hits 44%

My 44% bump is a **done-for-you** offer — "AI extracts your offer + 24-hour launch." The DIY-flavoured bumps (templates, playbook) sat around 14%. Same checkout, 3× the take-rate. Four things make a bump convert like that:

1. **It completes the purchase they just made.** They built the offer → the bump *launches it for them.* The literal next step. (Relevance is 80% of take-rate.)
2. **It sells done-for-you / speed.** People pay to *skip the work* — that's why mine beat the DIY bumps 3-to-1.
3. **It's a reflex, not a decision.** Impulse price, one sentence, price on the checkbox, never pre-checked.
4. **The value is anchored.** "~~$197~~ → $37, only at checkout."

That's the *what.* Now here are the two tools to run it.

## 🎁 Tool 1 — The bump swipe file (fill in the blanks)

Three bumps, small → large, each the next obvious step. Swap the brackets for your offer:

**Bump 1 — your 44% candidate (done-for-you):**
> *Yes! Add [the done-for-you version of your thing] for $[17–37]. You just got [what they bought] — this does the hard part for you: [the work it removes], ready in [timeframe]. Add it before you close this tab.*

**Bump 2 — the "do it faster" tool:**
> *Yes! Add [the templates/tool] for $[47–67]. [What they just got] works better with this — [skip the blank page / cut it to an hour]. Only at checkout.*

**Bump 3 — the bigger outcome:**
> *Yes! Add [the advanced asset] for $[97]. The first two get you started; this gets you [the bigger result]. You'll want it the first time [trigger moment].*

Rules baked in: relevance first, sell speed, one sentence, anchor the price, never pre-check.

## 🧮 Tool 2 — The self-liquidation calculator

Copy this. Plug in your numbers:

```
Front-end price:                 $ ______
+ Bump 1 (price × take-rate):    $ ______   ← aim for 40%+
+ Bump 2 (price × take-rate):    $ ______
+ Bump 3 (price × take-rate):    $ ______
= YOUR TRUE AOV (avg cart):      $ ______

Your cost per buyer (CPA):       $ ______

AOV ≥ CPA  →  you self-liquidate. Scale all day.
AOV < CPA  →  the gap is what OTOs + community + continuity have to cover.
```

*To get AOV + CPA: build the AOV column in Meta — Columns → Customize → Create Custom Metric → `Purchases Conversion Value ÷ Purchases`. CPA is your "cost per purchase" column.*

# Stop Chasing Certifications

**Subject line:** You don't need permission to help people

**Preview text:** Another $3K course isn't the answer.

---

I need to say something that might sting.

That certification you're thinking about? The one that costs $3K and takes 6 months? The one that'll finally make you feel "ready" to charge real money?

You don't need it.

I know that sounds reckless. Hear me out.

---

There are two types of coaches in the world:

**Type A** has 4 certifications, a 12-week program they spent 6 months building, a perfect website, and zero paying clients.

**Type B** has no certifications, a one-page offer they wrote in an afternoon, an ugly landing page, and 15 paying clients.

Type A feels ready. Type B is ready.

The difference? Type B skipped the permission step. They didn't wait for a certificate to tell them they were allowed to help people. They just helped people.

---

Here's the uncomfortable truth about coaching certifications:

**They're procrastination dressed up as preparation.**

Nobody who hired me ever asked about my credentials. Not once. They asked: "What do I get? How fast? How much?"

That's it. Credentials are for your confidence, not theirs.

And that $3K you were going to spend on another certification? That's 64 Client Ready systems. Or your first month of ad spend. Or the capital to test your offer with real humans.

---

I'm not saying expertise doesn't matter. It does. Massively.

But expertise isn't the same as credentials. Expertise is: you've done the thing, you know the thing, you can help someone else do the thing.

You already have that. You've probably had it for years. What you don't have is a clear offer that communicates it.

**The gap isn't knowledge. It's clarity.**

You don't need another course to fill the gap. You need to sit down for one afternoon and extract the offer that's already inside your experience.

---

Here's what I'd do instead of buying another certification:

1. Write down every result you've helped someone achieve — paid or unpaid
2. Find the pattern — what problem do you keep solving?
3. Package that into one sentence: "I help [who] do [what] so they [outcome]"
4. Charge for it

That's it. No certificate required. No permission needed.

The first person who pays you will teach you more about coaching than any certification ever will.

$47. One afternoon. Build the offer, skip the certification. You already know enough.

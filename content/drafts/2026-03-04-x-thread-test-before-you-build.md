# Thread: Test Before You Build

**Repurposed from:** Newsletter — "Test Before You Build"

---

1/6

Every coach I've worked with made the same mistake.

They had a great idea. Spent 3 months building it.

Course modules. Workbooks. A logo.

Then they launched. Crickets.

---

2/6

Not because the idea was bad.

Because they never tested it.

They assumed people wanted it. They didn't ask.

---

3/6

Here's what engineers do before they build anything:

They validate.

1. Write the offer in one sentence
2. Call 5 people who fit the profile
3. Say "Would you pay $Z for this?"
4. 3/5 say yes → build it
5. Less than 3 → rewrite and test again

---

4/6

The coaches charging $5K+ didn't build first.

They validated first.

The building was the easy part — because they already knew people wanted it.

---

5/6

The worst version of this mistake:

Building something great that nobody wants.

3 months of work. You're proud of it. And you have to either sell something people didn't ask for — or start over.

---

6/6

Test first. Build second.

One afternoon. Five conversations. That's all it takes to know.

Stop building things nobody asked for.

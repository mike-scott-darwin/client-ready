# Thread: Stop Buying Prompt Packs

## Tweet 1 (Hook)
Stop buying prompt packs.

They're the "get rich quick" of AI.

Here's what actually works:

## Tweet 2 (Problem)
Everyone's chasing the perfect prompt.

"Top 100 ChatGPT prompts for coaches."
"The exact prompt that made me 10K."

So you copy-paste it. And the output is... generic garbage.

Not because the prompt was bad. Because you gave AI nothing to work with.

## Tweet 3 (The Insight)
AI is an amplifier. Not a generator.

Feed it chaos → get polished chaos back.

Grammatically perfect. Professional sounding. Completely useless.

Feed it structured context about YOUR business → get output that sounds like you and sells like you.

## Tweet 4 (The System)
The fix isn't a better prompt. It's a better system.

Before you ask AI anything, it needs to know:

- Who you are (your story, your method)
- Who you serve (real language, real pain)
- How you sound (your voice, not "professional")
- What you've decided (and why)

That's context engineering. Not prompt engineering.

## Tweet 5 (Proof)
I write every piece of content, every ad, every email through the same system.

Same AI. Same tools anyone has access to.

The difference: I built my context first. AI reads it before it writes anything.

The output sounds like me because it knows what "me" means.

## Tweet 6 (The Reframe)
Prompt packs are ingredients without a kitchen.

Nobody's selling you the kitchen because it's not sexy. Building your context is boring. It's documentation. It's decisions. It's knowing your offer cold.

But it's the only thing that makes AI actually useful for your business.

## Tweet 7 (CTA)
Stop collecting prompts. Start building your context.

Know your offer. Know your audience. Know your voice. Write it down.

Then AI becomes a multiplier instead of a random word generator.

That's the system. No hacks required.

# The Offer Underneath

**Subject line:** The problem isn't the algorithm

**Preview text:** It's what's underneath your content.

---

Posting 3x a week for 12 months and nobody's buying.

I hear this every week. Same story, different coach. "I'm consistent. I'm showing up. The algorithm must be broken."

Wrong.

The algorithm is fine. The content is fine. The problem is what's underneath it: the offer.

Here's what most coaches get backwards:

They think content drives sales. So they post more. They try reels. They write threads. They batch-produce a month of content on Sunday. And nothing changes.

Because content doesn't drive sales. **A clear offer drives sales.** Content just amplifies whatever's underneath it.

If the offer is fuzzy — "I help people achieve their potential" — no amount of content fixes that. You're amplifying confusion.

If the offer is clear — "I help coaches validate a $5K offer in one afternoon using 5 AI prompts" — even mediocre content works. Because people immediately understand what you do and why they need it.

**The test is simple.** Can you explain your offer in one sentence? Not your elevator pitch. Not your bio. One sentence that makes someone say "How?"

If you can't, the content isn't the problem.

---

Here's what I'd do:

1. **Stop posting for 48 hours.** Seriously. The content treadmill can wait.
2. **Write down what you actually help people do.** Not the transformation. The specific outcome. The deliverable.
3. **Say it to five people.** Watch their face. If they tilt their head, it's not clear enough.
4. **Rewrite until someone says "How much?"** That's your offer.

Then go back to posting. But now you're amplifying something worth amplifying.

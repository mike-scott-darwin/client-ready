# Your AI Doesn't Know Your Business

I used to start every AI session the same way.

"I run a coaching business that helps business owners create and validate offers. My audience is solo entrepreneurs who are stuck in the idea phase..."

Every. Single. Time.

Fifteen minutes of context-setting before I could get anything useful. And even then, the outputs were generic. They sounded like everyone. They could have been written for any coaching business on the planet.

I was using the most powerful AI tools in history and getting content-mill results.

The problem wasn't ChatGPT. It wasn't Claude. It wasn't the prompts.

The problem was that my business knowledge lived in my head — and AI can't read minds.

So I started writing it down. Not in random docs. In structured reference files. Soul.md — why I exist. Offer.md — what I sell. Audience.md — who I serve. Voice.md — how I sound.

Four files changed everything.

Now every AI output reads my reference stack before generating anything. No re-explaining. No generic. Every ad, email, VSL, and social post sounds like me from the first draft.

The shift isn't better prompts. It's better context.

Your AI doesn't know your business. That's not a feature request. That's the problem to solve.

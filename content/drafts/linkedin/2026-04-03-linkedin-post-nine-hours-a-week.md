# Nine Hours a Week

I run my entire business in nine hours a week.

Not because I'm lazy. Not because my business is small. Because codification eliminated the bottleneck that eats most people alive: context-switching.

Before the reference stack, my week looked like this: Monday, re-explain my business to the AI tool I'm using for ads. Tuesday, re-explain it to the one I'm using for content. Wednesday, re-explain it for emails. Every single day started with the same 20 minutes of "here's my business, here's my audience, here's my voice."

That's not work. That's friction. And it adds up to hours per week of pure waste.

Now I type a command. The system reads 48 reference files. The output sounds like me. First draft, minimal edits.

Ads that used to take an afternoon take 30 minutes. Social posts that took an hour take 10 minutes. VSL scripts that took a week take a day.

Not because AI is magic. Because AI with context skips the 80% of work that was just getting the machine up to speed on who I am.

The nine hours aren't the goal. They're the byproduct of a system where every tool, every workflow, every output draws from the same deep well of business context.

You can't time-hack your way to nine hours. You have to build the context layer that makes nine hours possible.

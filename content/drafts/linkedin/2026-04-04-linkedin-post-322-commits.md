# 322 Commits of Thinking

My business reference stack has 322 commits. That's 322 times I refined how I think about my business.

Not 322 posts. Not 322 pieces of content. 322 moments where I got clearer about who I serve, what I believe, and how I sound.

Most of those commits are small. A tweak to audience.md after a sales call revealed a new objection. An update to voice.md after I noticed a phrase I keep using. A new research file synthesized into the reference stack.

But they compound. Commit 1 was rough. Generic. Could have described any coaching business. Commit 322 is razor-specific. It captures nuances about my audience that took months to discover. It encodes beliefs I didn't even know I held until I had to write them down.

This is what I mean when I say codification is a practice, not a project. You don't write your reference files once and you're done. You live with them. You refine them. They evolve as your business evolves.

And here's the part that matters for AI: every commit makes every future output better. Because the AI reads the latest version. It benefits from all 322 refinements even though it only sees the current state.

Your reference stack is a living document. It's the closest thing to actual business memory that exists.

322 commits in. The outputs I get today would be unrecognizable to commit-1 me.

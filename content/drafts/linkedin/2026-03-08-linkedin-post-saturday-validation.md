# LinkedIn: The Week I Stopped Planning and Started Asking

Last Saturday I scrapped my entire content calendar.

Not because it was bad. Because I realized I was planning content for an audience I hadn't talked to in two weeks.

So I sent 15 DMs. Simple message: "What's the one thing you're stuck on with your coaching business right now?"

11 people replied. 8 of them said some version of the same thing: "I don't know how to explain what I do."

Not marketing. Not funnels. Not pricing. Clarity.

That one afternoon of conversations gave me more direction than a month of content planning ever could.

The lesson: Your audience will tell you exactly what to build. But you have to ask. And then you have to shut up and listen.

Most coaches skip both steps. They build what they think the market wants, then wonder why nobody buys.

The market isn't hiding. You're just not asking.

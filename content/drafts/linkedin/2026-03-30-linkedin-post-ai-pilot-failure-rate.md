# 95% of AI Pilots Fail

McKinsey says 95% of enterprise AI pilots fail to reach production. I believe it. I've seen the same pattern at every scale.

Company buys AI tool. Team gets excited. They run a few experiments. Outputs are... okay. Generic. Not quite right. Needs heavy editing.

Within six weeks, the tool becomes shelfware. "AI isn't ready for our use case." "Our business is too nuanced." "We need a custom solution."

No. You need context.

The tool works fine. The model is capable. But it's generating blind. It doesn't know your business, your customers, your voice, your positioning, your competitive landscape, or your compliance requirements.

You handed it a powerful engine with no fuel.

The 5% that succeed? They invested in context before they invested in prompts. They documented what makes their business different. They structured it so AI could consume it. They built reference, not just ran commands.

This is why I'm obsessed with codification. Not because I love documentation. Because it's the missing layer between "AI is cool" and "AI actually runs part of my business."

The tool isn't the bottleneck. The context is. And context doesn't come from better models. It comes from you, writing down what you know.

# The $200/Month Problem

I tallied up what I was spending on AI tools before I built this system.

ChatGPT Plus: $20. Claude Pro: $20. Jasper: $49. Copy.ai: $36. A scheduling tool: $30. A "brand voice" AI: $29. Random one-off tools: another $30-40.

Over $200 a month. And you know what I got? Five different tools that all produced generic content because none of them actually knew my business.

Each tool had its own silo. I'd re-explain my business in every one. Copy-paste my "brand voice" into settings that did almost nothing. Build custom templates that sort of worked until they didn't.

The dirty secret of the AI tool market: most of these products are thin wrappers around the same models, charging you a premium to avoid learning how the models actually work.

Now I use one system. It costs less than half of what I was paying. Every output is better because the context is deeper. And I never re-explain my business. Ever.

The difference isn't which tools you use. It's whether your tools share context. Mine do. They all read from the same reference stack.

If you're paying for five AI tools and none of them sound like you, the answer isn't a sixth tool. The answer is building the context layer that makes any tool work.

Stop subscribing to solutions. Start building the foundation they all need.

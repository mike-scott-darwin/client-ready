# Platform-Agnostic by Design

Last week Claude updated their model. The week before that, GPT shipped a new feature. Gemini launched something too. Every week there's a new "game-changer."

I didn't change anything.

Not because I'm behind. Because my system was built to be agnostic from day one. Reference files are markdown. Plain text. They work with Claude, GPT, Gemini, Llama, or whatever launches next Tuesday.

This is the trap most people fall into. They build workflows on top of a specific tool. Custom GPTs. Claude projects. Platform-specific configurations. And then the platform changes its API, or a better model drops, and they're locked in or starting over.

My reference stack has survived three major model transitions without changing a single file. Because the files aren't prompts for a specific model. They're structured knowledge about my business. Any model can read them. Any model can use them.

The tool-of-the-week cycle is exhausting. New AI app every week. New Chrome extension. New "AI agent" that'll change everything. And none of them fix the actual problem: your business knowledge isn't structured.

Structure your knowledge once. Use it everywhere. Forever.

That's not a feature of a specific AI tool. That's architecture. And architecture outlasts every tool cycle.

# Prompt Packs Are Dead

Unpopular opinion: prompt packs are the snake oil of AI.

"500 ChatGPT prompts for business owners!" "$27 for the ultimate prompt library!"

You buy them. You try a few. The outputs are... fine. Generic. Interchangeable with what anyone else using the same prompts would get.

Three weeks later they're sitting in a Google Drive folder you'll never open again.

Here's what prompt sellers don't tell you:

The prompt is a vehicle. The context is the engine.

A great prompt with no business context produces generic work. An average prompt with deep context produces work that sounds like you.

The difference isn't the instruction you give AI. It's what AI knows about you BEFORE you give the instruction.

I have 48 reference files that every AI interaction reads before generating anything. My soul, my offer, my audience, my voice, my proof, my decisions — all structured so AI can consume them.

When I say "write an ad," AI doesn't ask "for what business?" It already knows. It knows my beliefs, my audience's pain points, my brand voice, my compliance guardrails.

That's not a prompt. That's architecture.

Stop buying prompts. Start codifying context.

# I Am Not an AI Consultant

AI consultants charge you to set up tools. Then they leave. And when the tools change — which they will, every quarter — you need them again.

That's the business model. Dependency.

I don't want you to depend on me. I want you to depend on your reference stack.

Here's the difference: a consultant configures your AI tools. I help you codify your business so any AI tool works. The consultant's value disappears when the platform updates. Your reference files survive every update because they're about your business, not about the tool.

I've watched companies pay $20K for "AI transformation" consulting. The deliverable? A set of custom GPTs and a workflow doc. Six months later, OpenAI changed their API and everything broke.

Meanwhile, my markdown files from nine months ago still work perfectly. Because they describe my business, not a specific tool's configuration.

The whole AI consulting industry is built on a false premise: that the hard part is the technology. It's not. The hard part is knowing your business well enough to write it down.

No consultant can do that for you. They can't codify your soul. They can't articulate your voice. They can't document your audience's real objections. Only you can.

What I do is give you the structure and the system. What goes inside it is yours.

That's not consulting. That's architecture.

# Validation Before Building

"Build it and they will come."

Wrong.

Build it and you'll spend three months wondering why nobody's buying. Then you'll tweak the landing page. Then the pricing. Then the name. Then you'll scrap the whole thing and start over.

I've watched this cycle more times than I can count. Smart people with real expertise spending weeks perfecting an offer they've never shown to a single human being.

Here's what actually works: validate before you build.

Put the idea in front of five people who match your audience. Not your mom. Not your business bestie. Real potential buyers. Describe the outcome. Name a price. See if they flinch.

If three out of five say "how do I pay you?" -- you have something. Build that.

If they hesitate, ask why. The feedback you get in that conversation is worth more than any course on offer creation.

The uncomfortable truth is that most offers fail because the creator never tested the core assumption. They assumed the market wanted it because they wanted to build it.

Test, validate, build. In that order. Always.

# Context Over Prompts

Everyone is chasing the perfect prompt. I was too.

I spent months collecting them. Organized by use case. Marketing prompts. Content prompts. Strategy prompts. Sales prompts.

My prompt library had 200+ entries. My results were still mediocre.

Then I ran an experiment. I took the most basic prompt possible — "write me an ad" — and instead of engineering the prompt, I engineered the context. I wrote four reference files: what my business is, who it serves, how it sounds, and why it exists.

Same dumb prompt. Dramatically better output.

That broke my brain. Because everyone in the AI space is selling prompts. Templates. Frameworks. "$97 for 1,000 ChatGPT prompts." And none of them address the actual bottleneck.

The bottleneck is never the instruction. It's the information.

A prompt says "do this." Context says "here's everything you need to know about my business before you do anything."

One is a single-use command. The other is a compounding asset.

I stopped building prompt libraries and started building reference stacks. 48 files now. Every AI tool I use reads them automatically. The prompts I use are embarrassingly simple. The outputs are embarrassingly good.

Context compounds. Prompts are disposable.

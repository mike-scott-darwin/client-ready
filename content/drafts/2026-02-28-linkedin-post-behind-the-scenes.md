# LinkedIn: I Burned Down a Profitable Offer

I built an offer that made money.

And I burned it down.

Not because it wasn't selling. Because showing up for it every day felt like a different kind of 9-to-5. The one I was supposed to be escaping.

Here's what nobody tells you about coaching:

Making money from an offer doesn't mean it's the right offer. If you dread client calls, if you feel like a fraud delivering it, if you keep tweaking the positioning because something feels off — that's misalignment.

You can't grow into pain. You'll either burn out or burn it down. I chose the second option.

The rebuild started with one question: What do I actually want to help people do?

Not "what's profitable." Not "what's trending." What feels like pull instead of push.

That question led to Client Ready. An offer I can show up for without white-knuckling.

If you're building something that looks right on paper but feels wrong in practice — trust that feeling. The problem isn't your work ethic. It's the foundation.

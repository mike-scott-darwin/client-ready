# Decision: Replace "Anti-Guru" with "Alignment Engineer"

## What Changed

"Anti-guru" is retired as a positioning label. Replaced by **"Alignment Engineer"** — positioning FOR building aligned offers instead of AGAINST gurus.

The voice stays direct, practical, no-BS. The engineering mindset stays. What changes is the framing: we stop defining ourselves by what we reject and start defining ourselves by what we build.

## Why

- "Anti-guru" has become its own category — Dan Koe, Cat Howell, Miles Stutz, half of coaching Twitter all use it. It no longer differentiates.
- Saying "I'm not a guru" is now the most guru thing you can say.
- Positioning AGAINST something is inherently reactive. Positioning FOR something is proactive.
- The alignment philosophy ("you can't grow into pain," the 3-question diagnostic, "if the overlaps don't make sense, you have the wrong offer") is genuinely unique — nobody else is saying it.
- Gemini research confirms: "many anti-guru positions have become formulaic and less differentiating."

## The Shift

| Before (Anti-Guru) | After (Alignment Engineer) |
|---------------------|---------------------------|
| "I'm not like those gurus" | "I build offers that fit who you actually are" |
| "Rejects hype, fluff, overcomplicated advice" | "Engineers clarity from confusion" |
| "Anti-guru" as tone attribute | "Alignment-first" as tone attribute |
| Content pillar: "Anti-Guru — contrarian takes" | Content pillar: "Alignment — build right, not fast" |
| "Here's what gurus get wrong" | "Here's what I built this week and why" |
| Oppositional energy | Builder energy |

## What Stays

- Direct voice, short sentences, "Wrong." as a complete sentence
- "Test, validate, build" methodology
- "You can't grow into pain" — this becomes MORE prominent, not less
- Engineering language: extract, validate, install, system
- The 9-to-5 reality angle
- Confidence without hype
- Calm, grounded energy

## What Goes

- "Anti-guru" as a label or tone attribute
- Content hooks that define us by opposition to gurus
- "Every piece of content reframes the guru narrative" — too reactive
- "Does it reject guru fluff?" in the voice checklist

## What Gets Added

- "Alignment-first" as a tone attribute: "Builds offers that fit who you are, not who you think you should be"
- Content pillar evolves: "Alignment" replaces "Anti-Guru" — covers building right, offer-life fit, the diagnostic
- New hooks: builder-identity hooks that show the work, not the stance
- Alignment diagnostic becomes headline-level, not buried in audience.md

## What Changes (Reference Files)

1. **voice.md:**
   - Quick Reference: "anti-guru" → "alignment-first" in tone
   - Anti-positioning → Positioning (what we stand FOR)

   - Content Hook Pattern 4: evolve "Anti-Guru Setup" to "Alignment Reveal"

2. **content-strategy.md:**
   - Content Pillars: "Anti-Guru" pillar → "Alignment" pillar
   - Hooks Library: "Anti-Guru Hooks" section → "Alignment Hooks"

3. **main-angles.md:**
   - Angle 4 "Engineering Approach" and Angle 5 "Reality Over Perception" merge into a stronger "Alignment Engineer" angle

## Implementation Note

This is an evolution, not a revolution. The voice doesn't change — just what it's pointed at. We stop punching at gurus and start building in public. The audience still gets the directness and the contrarian edge — but through showing what aligned building looks like, not through attacking what misaligned coaching looks like.

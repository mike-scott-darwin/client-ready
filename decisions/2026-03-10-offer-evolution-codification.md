# Client Ready Offer Evolution: From Discrete Prompts to Knowledge Codification

## Status: PARKED — Launch Gate Active

**This decision is parked until the current offer has 30 front-end sales.**

The insight is real. The timing is wrong. Ship v1 first, evolve from market data, not another research cycle. "Good enough to launch, then refine."

**Revisit triggers:**
- 30 front-end sales completed
- Market language testing via content shows clear signal (engagement data on "context > prompts" narrative)
- A buyer explicitly asks for the codification layer

**Do not:**
- Restructure the $47 product before launch
- Rewrite landing page copy for the evolved mechanism
- Build new assets for v2 before v1 is in market

**Do continue:**
- Testing "context > prompts" as a content angle (tweets, LinkedIn, newsletter)
- Collecting research on market language that resonates
- Noting buyer feedback once the funnel is live

---

## Situation

Through building Client Ready — 54 research files, 39 decisions, 42 reference files, 8 automated cron jobs, 752 engine commits — a gap has emerged between what we sell and what we actually do.

**What we sell:** 5 AI prompts that extract your offer in one afternoon ($47). Discrete prompting.

**What we actually do:** A reference-file-driven operating system where codified business identity (soul, offer, audience, voice) produces every output — ads, content, VSLs, emails, sites — and compounds over time.

The mechanism on the sales page says Extract → Validate → Build → Automate. But the actual practice is Extract → Codify → Generate → Compound. The codification layer is the breakthrough, and it's not in the product yet.

Market research (2026-03-09) confirms:
- $7.7B AI knowledge management market growing 47% annually — but entirely enterprise
- Nobody occupies the intersection of "structured reference files + AI-native business OS + non-developer"
- Every Skool AI community teaches selling AI services — none teach business owners to codify for themselves
- "Prompt engineering" is being replaced by "context engineering" in market language
- AI burnout is real: businesses try dozens of tools, none produce usable results without business context
- Claude Code adoption among non-developers is growing (50%+ of usage at Anthropic is non-dev roles)

## The Core Insight

Discrete prompts are a point-in-time tool. Reference files are a compounding asset. The business should eventually sell the asset, not the tool. But the current product is good enough to launch and learn from.

## Options Considered

### Option A: Evolve Client Ready's Mechanism (Recommended — After Launch Gate)

Keep the same soul, audience, and voice. Evolve the product stack so the mechanism centers on codification, not discrete prompts. Same brand, deeper product.

### Option B: New Offer Under Client Ready (Multi-Offer)

Keep current $47 product, add codification product for later-stage buyers. Rejected: sells something you no longer believe in alongside something you do.

### Option C: Separate Business

New repo, new brand. Rejected: fails the soul test — same soul.md, same voice.md.

## Decision

**Option A — but only after 30 front-end sales of the current product.**

The evolution is real. The pattern of evolving before shipping is also real. Launch what exists. Let buyers tell you what v2 should be.

## What Changes (When Gate Is Met)

### After 30 sales
- **offer.md** — Update mechanism from "Extract → Validate → Build → Automate" to "Extract → Codify → Generate → Compound"
- **content-strategy.md** — Elevate "context vs prompts" from sub-angle to primary narrative
- Restructure $47 deliverable: from 5 prompts to a starter reference stack
- Update landing page copy

### Right now
- Nothing in the product or funnel changes
- Continue testing the narrative through organic content
- Keep collecting research (this file stays open)

## Risk

**Low (with gate).** The gate prevents premature optimization. Content testing provides signal without disrupting the funnel. If the narrative doesn't resonate in content, the evolution may need different framing before it touches the product.

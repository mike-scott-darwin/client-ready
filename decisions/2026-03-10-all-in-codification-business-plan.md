## The Model: Licensed AI Architecture for Knowledge Codification

### What Devon Built (The Blueprint)

Devon built **vip** — an AI engine that any business can plug into:

```
vip (ENGINE — shared, licensed)
├── .claude/skills/        ← /think, /ads, /vsl, /organic, /site, /wiki
├── .claude/lenses/        ← compliance, review, audit
├── .claude/reference/     ← domain rubrics, compliance rules
└── docs/                  ← philosophy, architecture, setup guides

user-repo (DATA — per business, owned by user)
├── reference/core/        ← soul.md, offer.md, audience.md, voice.md
├── research/              ← all research, mining, synthesis
├── decisions/             ← strategic choices documented
└── outputs/               ← everything generated FROM reference
```

**The genius:** The engine is the same for everyone. The data is unique per business. Same skills + different context = different outputs. Users pay for engine access + community. They own their data.

### What Michael Builds (The Adaptation)

**Same architecture. Different positioning. Different audience.**

Devon positions vip as a tool for solopreneurs building businesses.
Michael positions the engine as a **knowledge codification system for businesses that need AI to actually work.**

The target isn't "people who want to build a business with AI." It's **"businesses that are failing because AI doesn't know them."**

## The Moat (Why Competitors Can't Catch You)

1. **You eat your own cooking.** Every piece of content, every ad, every presentation is generated from the same system you're licensing. That's unchallengeable proof.

2. **The engine compounds.** vip gets better every week — new skills, new lenses, new automation. License holders benefit automatically.

3. **Network effects.** Community members share reference file patterns. "Here's how I structured voice.md for a law firm." Templates emerge organically. The library grows.

4. **Switching costs.** Once a business has 20+ reference files, the switching cost is enormous. Starting over is unthinkable. This is sticky revenue.

5. **Data advantage.** You see how hundreds of businesses structure their knowledge. Patterns emerge. You build better templates, better rubrics, better skills. The gap widens.

6. **First mover.** Nobody is selling this exact intersection: structured reference files + AI architecture + non-developer + licensed engine. By the time someone copies it, you have 12 months of compound advantage.

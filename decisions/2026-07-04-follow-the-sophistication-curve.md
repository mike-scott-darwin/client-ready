## Context

Synthesizing five months of Miles Stutz ad mining (Jan vintage → Feb 11 → the Feb 22 full reset → Jul 4) plus his May/Jun coaching calls revealed that his ads don't evolve randomly — they follow a **predictable maturation curve**, and the endpoint he converged on is Client Ready's own thesis ("an offer so clear it sells without a call").

The trajectory (full detail in `research/competitors/miles-stutz.md` → Trajectory section):
- Complexity → simplicity (4-rung ladder → two clean $17 front-ends)
- Price floor only rose ($7 → $9 → $17)
- Format converged to 100% static
- **Angle migration up the Schwartz sophistication ladder: attack-the-enemy → explain-the-mechanism → sell-the-identity**
- Proof went from generic claims → named specificity, compounding into a moat
- The testing *method* stayed fixed while products/angles churned

Before this, CR treated each Miles mining as a one-off "what to steal" list. This decision converts the *pattern* into a standing operating principle.

## Options Considered

- **A: Keep treating each mining as a standalone steal-list.** React ad-hoc to whatever's newest.
  - Pros: simple. Cons: misses the trajectory; CR keeps re-deriving the same read and risks chasing his latest tactic without understanding where the curve is going.
- **B: Adopt the trajectory as a standing operating principle (recommended).** Codify "method fixed, offer/angle variable" and "follow ~one step behind on the sophistication curve" as how CR reads and responds to Miles going forward.
  - Pros: turns a competitor into a leading indicator; gives a repeatable lens for every future mining; keeps CR's differentiation (upstream + FTC-clean) intact.
  - Cons: requires discipline not to over-fit to one operator's account.
- **C: Get ahead of him / lead the frontier.** Try to out-innovate the angle curve.
  - Pros: first-mover if right. Cons: CR lacks the spend/volume to prove a frontier; wastes budget testing what a $750K/yr account will prove for free. Rejected.

## Decision

**Option B.** Adopt the Miles trajectory as a standing operating principle with four commitments:

1. **Method is fixed infrastructure; offer and angle are the variables.** CR's ad-strategy layer (already in `offer.md`) is treated as settled — volume launch, $10–20/day/ad set, broad + Big-Six + worldwide, finish the full $300/first-or-last-day test, radical creative variance, static-first, CBO. Don't re-litigate the method every campaign. Spend creative energy on offer and angle.
2. **Follow ~one step behind on the sophistication curve — deliberately.** Miles is at emotion/identity; CR is moving from mechanism → emotion (the just-codified Absolution / Offer-Closes-Itself / Tool-Fatigue angles). Let his spend prove the frontier, then follow in CR's honest, FTC-clean voice. Don't try to lead it (Option C).
3. **Proof is a compounding moat — start now.** The single highest-leverage gap. Run the 48-Hour Validation Sprint as the case-study engine from the first sales; accumulate named, niche-diverse wins over time the way he did (Diego → 7 students).
4. **Hold the structural edges he validated:** never lower the $47 floor; resist funnel sprawl (winners narrow); static-first; and pressure-test a stickier, outcome-based product name against the flat "Offer System."

## Why

A data-driven operator running hundreds of ads over five months is the cheapest R&D CR will ever get. His account is a leading indicator of where the coaching-offer market is heading. Reacting ad-hoc (A) wastes that signal; trying to lead the frontier (C) wastes budget CR doesn't have. Following one deliberate step behind, in a voice he can't copy (upstream + FTC-clean), is the asymmetric play. This decision is the *general principle*; `2026-07-04-clarity-sells-angle-territory.md` was its first application.

## What Changes

- Codified as a standing lens — no new files required beyond the Trajectory section already added to `research/competitors/miles-stutz.md`.
- Future Miles minings should update the Trajectory table and re-check: which stage of the sophistication curve is he in now, and is CR one step behind?
- Reaffirms existing commitments (don't lower $47, static-first, proof-collection via the Validation Sprint) rather than changing them — this decision is the *why* that ties them together.

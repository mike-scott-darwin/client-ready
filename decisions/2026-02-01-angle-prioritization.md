# Angle Prioritization — February 2026

## Situation

Client Ready has 6 proven angles in `main-angles.md` but needs differentiated positioning against competitors (Cat Howell, Miles Stutz, Hernan Vazquez). All three assume buyers already have an offer — leaving a gap for validation-first messaging.

## Research

Synthesized from:
- `research/competitors/cat-howell.md`
- `research/competitors/miles-stutz.md`
- `research/competitors/hernan-vazquez.md`
- `reference/core/audience.md`
- `reference/proof/angles/main-angles.md`

**Key insight:** Competitors target people ready to scale. Client Ready targets people who don't know what their offer is yet — or have built offers that hurt to show up for.

## Options Considered

| Angle | Pros | Cons |
|-------|------|------|
| Year 2-3 of the Search | Deep emotional resonance, lived experience | Narrow audience |
| Before the Funnel | Contrarian, clear positioning | May confuse solution-aware |
| Misalignment | Unique story, builds trust | Requires vulnerability |
| Content Merry-Go-Round | Specific pain, paid traffic alternative | May not be universal |
| Validation Before Infrastructure | Tactical, logical | Less emotional |
| Anti-Guru / No-BS | Brand consistent | Already implicit in voice |
| Aligned Offer | Sustainability framing | Softer sell |

## Decision

Prioritize 3 angles for development:

1. **"Before the Funnel"** — Cold traffic positioning against competitors
2. **"Misalignment"** — Trust-building, unique story only Michael can tell
3. **"Content Merry-Go-Round"** — Specific pain point, paid traffic as alternative

### Rationale

- All three are **unique to Client Ready** (competitors don't use them)
- All three work for **cold traffic** (broad enough)
- Together they cover: logical positioning (#1), emotional trust (#2), specific pain (#3)
- "Year 2-3 of the Search" saved for retargeting once traffic exists

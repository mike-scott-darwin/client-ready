# Voice Dump Process -- Adapted from Devon's Walk-to-Research Cycle

## Context

Devon (Main Branch creator) uses a voice-dump-to-research cycle as his primary thinking process. He walks for 60 minutes, records his thoughts, transcribes them, and feeds them into /think as research seeds. This spawns parallel research agents that pressure-test his instincts against market reality. The synthesis becomes decisions, which become reference file updates.

Mike needs an adapted version of this for a 9-to-5 schedule with limited time windows.

## Options

### Option A: Full Devon Process (60-minute walks, daily)

Walk daily, record 60 minutes, transcribe, spawn 15-20 research agents, synthesize into 5-7 decisions per day.

**Pros:** Maximum clarity, rapid iteration

**Cons:** Unrealistic for a 9-to-5er. Devon builds the engine full-time.

**Effort:** High

### Option B: Compressed Voice Dump (15-30 minutes, 2-3x per week)

Record during commute, lunch, or evening walk. One focused question per session. Transcribe, paste into /think, spawn 3-5 targeted research agents. One decision per session.

**Pros:** Fits a real schedule. Still captures the "downloads" that only come away from the screen. Compounds over weeks.

**Cons:** Slower iteration than Devon's pace. May lose momentum if skipped.

**Effort:** Low-Medium

### Option C: Weekend Deep Think (one 60-minute session per week)

One dedicated walk per weekend. Record everything. Full Devon-style research wave. Multiple decisions in one sitting.

**Pros:** Concentrated depth. Mimics Devon's intensity in a single window.

**Cons:** One week between sessions means ideas go stale. Harder to maintain the daily association your soul.md describes.

**Effort:** Medium

## Decision

**We chose: Option B (Compressed Voice Dump) with Option C as a weekend supplement.**

The voice dump only works if it's frequent enough to keep you associated -- connected to why you do this. A 15-minute recording 2-3 times during the week keeps the thread alive. A longer weekend session gives depth. This matches the soul.md principle: "The test: Does the work feel like pull or push?"

## Consequences

### What Becomes Easier

- Vision clarity compounds weekly instead of waiting for a "strategy day"
- Reference files stay current with your actual thinking, not a snapshot from weeks ago
- Content ideas emerge naturally from the voice dumps (content-strategy.md gets fed)
- You stop guessing what to work on -- the voice dump tells you

### What Becomes Harder

- Requires discipline to actually leave the screen (feels unproductive at first)
- Transcription adds a step before the AI work can begin

### What We're Accepting

- Slower pace than Devon's daily cycle -- that's fine, different life stage
- Voice dumps may feel awkward at first -- this passes after 2-3 sessions
- Not every session produces a decision -- some are pure research, and that's valuable too

## What Changes

A new reference file at reference/domain/voice-dump-process.md captures the repeatable process so it persists across sessions. soul.md does not change -- it already articulates the philosophy. This is the operational companion to what soul.md describes.

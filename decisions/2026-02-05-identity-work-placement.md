# Decision: Where Does Identity Work Live in the Offer Stack?

## Situation

Today's research surfaced a key insight: the transformation prospects NEED is an identity shift — from "reactive tactic-chaser" to "intentional business architect." But prospects don't WANT identity work. They want to know what to sell.

**The tension:**
- Front-end must solve the surface problem ("What's my offer?")
- Deeper transformation (identity shift) drives better results and retention
- Identity work positioned wrong = woo that kills conversion
- Identity work positioned right = "implementation support" that creates testimonials

**Key quotes from research:**

> "People want a transformation of character, not just a service." — Million Dollar Roadmap

> "Income leaps follow identity leaps, not the other way around." — Steph Hughson

> "If you do not have the business that you want, you have not become the person who can run it yet." — Alex Charfen

> "Subconsciously, you will not grow into pain." — Client Ready soul.md

---

## The Identity Shift Required

| FROM (Old Character) | TO (New Character) |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| Reactive (chasing tactics) | Intentional (building from vision) |
| Dissociated (executing without feeling) | Associated (connected to why) |
| "Am I cut out for this?" | "I know exactly what I do" |
| Social media backup dancer | Business owner |
| Building by default | Building by design |
| Grinder | Architect |

---

## Current State

**$27 Offer System** already does implicit identity work:
- Zone of genius extraction = surfacing who they are
- "What do you do that nobody else does?" = identity question disguised as offer question

**DFY Onboarding Form** already has some depth:
- Business vision questions
- Who they want to serve
- What success looks like

**Gap:** No explicit identity transformation framework. The shift happens accidentally, not intentionally.

---

## Options

### Option A: Deepen DFY Onboarding Form

**What:** Add Old Character → New Character questions to the existing DFY intake.

**Where:** `reference/domain/funnel/dfy-onboarding-form.md`

**Questions to add:**
1. How would you describe yourself in your business right now? (3 sentences)
2. Who do you need to become to run this offer effortlessly?
3. What beliefs, habits, or fears must that version of you be free of?
4. Describe your ideal day when this business is working.
5. What do you want to be FREE of? (hours, tasks, stress)

**Pros:**
- No change to sales flow
- Better inputs = better DFY deliverables
- Creates deeper client relationship
- Testimonials become transformation stories, not just tactic results

**Cons:**
- Only affects $397 buyers (small segment)
- Form already 45-90 min — adding more may reduce completion
- Doesn't help front-end or mid-tier buyers

**Effort:** Low (30 min to update form)

---

### Option B: Create "Soul Extraction" Module for Sprint

**What:** Add a structured identity module to Sprint ($297) delivery — Week 1 before any funnel work.

**Format:**
- 15-min Loom explaining why identity matters for sustainability
- 4-5 written prompts they complete before first group call
- Hot seat in Week 1 call surfaces gaps

**The Prompts:**
1. What does your ideal work day look like? (hours, tasks, energy)
2. What are you trying to escape? (be specific)
3. What would make you abandon this offer in 6 months?
4. Who do you need to become to show up for this business daily?
5. What's one belief you need to drop to make this work?

**Positioning:** "Before we build your funnel, let's make sure it fits your life. Most people build something they'll burn down in 18 months. Let's not do that."

**Pros:**
- Differentiates Sprint from DIY competitors
- Sets up better results (aligned offer = they follow through)
- Creates transformation language for testimonials
- Retention: they stay because it's THEIR offer, not a template

**Cons:**
- Adds complexity to Sprint delivery
- Some buyers may resist "woo" framing (needs tactical positioning)
- Requires creating new content

**Effort:** Medium (2-3 hours to create module)

---

### Option C: Build Identity Reflection into Community ($47/mo)

**What:** Monthly "Alignment Check" as part of community value — keeps members connected to why.

**Format:**
- Monthly prompt in Skool (5 min reflection)
- Optional hot seat for "I'm stuck" moments
- Quarterly "Is this still the right offer?" review

**Monthly Prompts (rotating):**
- "What's one thing you're doing in your business that drains you? Should it stay?"
- "Are you showing up as the person who can run this business?"
- "What would your future self tell you to stop doing?"
- "Rate your offer fit 1-10. What would make it a 10?"

**Pros:**
- Retention play — they stay because it's not just tactics
- Low effort to implement (just prompts)
- Catches misalignment before churn
- Differentiates from tactic-only communities

**Cons:**
- Only affects community members
- May feel "soft" to action-oriented buyers
- Requires ongoing facilitation

**Effort:** Low (1 hour to create prompts, ongoing 15 min/month)

---

### Option D: Leave Front-End Alone, Bank for Later

**What:** Keep the $27 → bumps → OTOs focused on tactical surface problems. Revisit identity work when scaling or adding backend offers.

**Rationale:**
- Front-end is working (structure validated)
- Identity work may be premature before product-market fit confirmed
- Can add later without restructuring

**Pros:**
- No risk of breaking what's working
- Focus on traffic and conversion first
- Identity work becomes V2 enhancement

**Cons:**
- Misses opportunity to differentiate now
- Results may plateau without deeper transformation
- Competitors could add this layer first

**Effort:** None

---

## Recommendation

**Do A + B. Skip C for now. Reject D.**

**Rationale:**

1. **Option A (DFY Onboarding)** is low-effort, high-signal. Better inputs = better outputs. Do this immediately.

2. **Option B (Sprint Soul Extraction)** differentiates the $297 and improves results. This is where identity work has the most leverage — they've committed but haven't built yet. The "before we build, let's make sure it fits" frame is tactical, not woo.

3. **Option C (Community)** is good but premature. Build the community base first, then add retention layers.

4. **Option D** is the safe choice but misses the insight. The research is clear: transformation of character > service delivery. Waiting = leaving value on the table.

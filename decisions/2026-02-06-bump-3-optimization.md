# Bump 3 Optimization for Self-Liquidating Funnel

## Situation

Current Bump 3 (Traffic & Launch Kit at $67) may be solving a **future problem** rather than an **immediate need** at checkout. Someone buying a $27 "validate your offer in one afternoon" system isn't thinking about Facebook ads yet — they haven't validated anything.

To hit Cat Howell's 50%+ combined bump rate and $100+ checkout AOV, Bump 3 needs to convert. The current positioning may be creating friction.

**The question:** Should we replace Bump 3 with something that solves a problem buyers KNOW they have at checkout?

---

## Current State

| Bump | Price | Promise | Timing |
|------|-------|---------|--------|
| 1: DM Scripts | $17 | First conversation in 5 minutes | Today |
| 2: Templates | $37 | Don't start from scratch | This week |
| 3: Traffic Kit | $67 | First 10 paying customers | This month |

**Issue with Bump 3:** "Traffic" and "Facebook ads" feel premature. The buyer is still figuring out their offer — why would they need traffic yet?

---

## Options

### Option A: Keep Current (Traffic & Launch Kit) — $67

**What it is:** AI ad generator, targeting playbook, launch checklist, A/B testing framework, "First 10 Buyers" roadmap.

**Pros:**
- Already built and documented
- No fulfillment changes needed
- Appeals to action-oriented buyers who think ahead

**Cons:**
- Solves tomorrow's problem, not today's
- May feel disconnected from the front-end purchase
- Likely lowest conversion of the three bumps

**Best for:** Keeping if initial data shows acceptable conversion (15%+ individual take rate).

---

### Option B: Loom Offer Review — $67

**What it is:** Michael records a 15-minute personalized Loom reviewing their completed offer document.

**Pros:**
- Solves the #1 fear: "What if I do this wrong?"
- Human touch at low-ticket = rare and valuable
- Personalized feedback, not just templates
- Natural next step after completing front-end
- Scalable (10-15 min per buyer)

**Cons:**
- Requires fulfillment time (15 min per buyer)
- Dependent on buyer actually completing the offer doc
- May create support questions

**Fulfillment:** Buyer submits offer doc via form → Michael records Loom → Delivered within 48 hours.

**Promise:** "Get expert eyes on your offer — know it's right before you build anything."

**Best for:** Building relationship and trust. Good if planning to ascend buyers to Sprint/DFY.

---

### Option C: Done-For-You Offer Document — $67

**What it is:** Michael's team runs the AI prompts FOR them and delivers a completed offer document.

**Pros:**
- Highest likely conversion — "I don't want to do the work"
- Instant gratification — they get the OUTPUT, not just the process
- Premium feel at low-ticket price
- Eliminates friction entirely

**Cons:**
- Requires intake form + fulfillment work
- 24-48 hour delivery time
- May reduce engagement with the front-end product
- Could cannibalize OTO 2 (DFY at $397)?

**Fulfillment:** Buyer fills intake form → Team runs prompts → Delivers polished offer doc in 24-48 hours.

**Promise:** "Skip the work. Get your complete offer document delivered — we build it, you review it."

**Best for:** Maximum conversion. Good if you have capacity for light fulfillment.

---

### Option D: Offer Accelerator Call — $67

**What it is:** 30-minute 1-on-1 call to extract their offer together, live.

**Pros:**
- Highest perceived value
- Guarantees they won't get stuck
- Live human interaction at $67 is unusual
- Creates immediate relationship

**Cons:**
- High fulfillment time (30 min per buyer)
- Scheduling friction
- Doesn't scale well
- May cannibalize Sprint ($297) which includes calls

**Fulfillment:** Buyer books via Calendly → Michael or team member runs extraction live.

**Promise:** "Extract your offer in 30 minutes — together. Leave the call with a validated offer."

**Best for:** High-touch, low-volume. Not recommended at scale.

## Comparison Matrix

| Option | Conversion Potential | Fulfillment Effort | Scalability | Risk |
|--------|---------------------|-------------------|-------------|------|
| A: Traffic Kit | Low-Medium | None | High | Low |
| B: Loom Review | High | Medium (15 min) | Medium | Low |
| C: DFY Offer Doc | Highest | Medium (30-60 min) | Medium | Medium (cannibalization?) |
| D: Live Call | Medium-High | High (30 min + scheduling) | Low | High (cannibalization) |

---

## Recommendation

**Test Option B (Loom Review) first.**

Rationale:
1. Solves the immediate fear ("Am I doing this right?")
2. Moderate fulfillment effort (15 min per buyer)
3. Builds relationship for ascension
4. Doesn't cannibalize higher offers
5. Can upgrade to Option C later if conversion is strong

**Alternative:** If you have capacity for light fulfillment, Option C (DFY Doc) likely converts highest.

---

## Test Protocol

1. Replace Bump 3 with new offer
2. Run for 30 purchases (per Cat's guidance)
3. Track individual Bump 3 take rate
4. Track combined bump rate
5. Compare to baseline (if available)

**Success criteria:**
- Individual Bump 3 take rate: 20%+
- Combined bump rate: 50%+
- Checkout AOV: $100+

---

## Decision

**Status:** CLOSED

**Selected:** Option E — Offer Validation Kit ($67)

**Rationale:**
- Bumps must be hands-off / digital / scalable
- OTOs are where proximity is sold (Sprint, DFY, Community)
- Validation Kit solves immediate problem ("Am I doing this right?") without requiring fulfillment
- One-time asset creation, scales infinitely

**What's included:**
- AI Validation Prompts — Paste offer doc, get instant feedback
- 10 Real Offer Reviews — Recorded Looms of Michael reviewing actual offers (done once)
- "Good Offer" Checklist — 15-point self-assessment
- Before/After Examples — 5 offers with annotations

**Promise:** "Validate your offer before you build — know it's right without guessing."

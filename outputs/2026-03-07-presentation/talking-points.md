## Slide 1 -- Title
"I built a complete business system in 2 months. One person,
no team, no agency. Let me show you how."

## Slide 2 -- The Problem
"Everyone's using AI to go faster. But faster at the wrong thing
is just expensive failure. The real problem is alignment -- does AI
even know what your business is about?"

## Slide 3 -- What If
"What if instead of 'write me 50 ads,' you could say 'help me
figure out what my offer actually is -- then write ads that match.'
That's the difference between AI as executor and AI as thinking partner."

## Slide 4 -- Two Repos
"The engine has the skills -- how to write ads, how to research,
how to check compliance. My repo has the data -- who I am, what I sell,
who I serve. Same engine, different data, completely different business.
That's how it scales."

## Slide 5 -- Reference Stack
"Four files drive everything. Soul is why I exist. Offer is what I sell.
Audience is who buys. Voice is how I sound. Update one file and every
downstream output -- ads, emails, tweets, VSLs -- automatically reflects
the change. No rebrief."

## Slide 6 -- The Numbers
"322 commits of documented thinking. 89 research files. 38 strategic
decisions. 85 output files. One person in 2 months. That's not AI
replacing work -- that's AI amplifying a human who knows what they want."

## Slide 7 -- The Loop
"Research, decide, codify, create. The loop is what matters. Most people
skip straight to create and wonder why the output is generic. The thinking
IS the product."

## Slide 8 -- The Bot
"My Mac Mini runs overnight. Deep research at 2 AM. Summary to my phone
at 5:30. Content drafted at 6. I wake up and approve from Telegram.
15 minutes a day."

## Slide 9 -- For Agencies

Every deliverable after that is automatically aligned. Voice consistency
across your entire team. Every strategic decision documented and traceable.
And it compounds -- month 6 output is dramatically better than month 1

## Slide 10 -- Takeaway

you get a business."

## Audience Q&A -- Likely Questions and Answers

**"How long did this take to set up?"**
"The reference files took about 2 weeks of real thinking -- not typing,
thinking. Articulating your offer, your audience, your voice. That's the
hard part. After that, the system compounds. Two months in and I have
322 commits of documented business thinking."

**"Can this work for client businesses?"**
"That's the whole point. The engine is separate from the data. You clone
the engine, build a new reference stack for each client, and the same
skills produce aligned output for completely different businesses. Soul.md
for a fitness coach looks nothing like soul.md for a SaaS company, but
the engine treats them the same."

**"What does this cost to run?"**
"Claude Code subscription. Gemini API for deep research -- less than a
cent a day. OpenRouter for the bot -- maybe 4 cents a day. The M1 Mac
Mini was reconditioned. Total monthly cost is under 100 bucks. Compare
that to a VA or a junior copywriter."

**"What about clients who can't articulate their offer?"**
"That's the most valuable part. The /think skill walks them through
research, decision, codification. By the time they have offer.md written,
they understand their business better than they did before. The process IS
the value. They're not paying you to type -- they're paying you to help
them think clearly."

**"Isn't this just fancy prompt engineering?"**
"Prompt engineering is one message. This is a system -- 4 reference files,
38 documented decisions, compliance lenses, domain rubrics. When I say
'write me an ad,' the AI reads my offer, my audience stuck patterns, my
voice anti-patterns, and checks against FTC compliance rules. That's not
a prompt. That's an operating system."

**"What happens when the reference files are wrong?"**
"You get consistently wrong output -- which is actually better than
inconsistently wrong output, because you can diagnose it. If every ad
sounds too salesy, you check voice.md. If the audience targeting is off,
you check audience.md. The reference files make problems visible and
fixable. Without them, you're debugging in the dark."

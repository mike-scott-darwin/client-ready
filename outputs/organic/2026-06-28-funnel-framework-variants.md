# Framework Video Variants

Same framework, two different doors in. Post these across the next 1–2 weeks against the original so the algorithm finds which entry point lands (offer.md: "test entirely different concepts, not button colors").

---

## Variant B — "The Wrong Order" (cross-out cold open)

**Angle:** Show the order most coaches actually build in, cross it out live, then reveal the right one. High pattern-interrupt. Voice.md Pattern 2 ("Wrong").

**Hook [0:00–0:04]:**
"This is the order most coaches build their business in. And it's exactly backwards."

**Script:**
- **[0:04–0:18]** "Watch. Most people start by posting content for months. Then they build a course. Then a funnel. Then — dead last, if ever — they think about whether the offer actually sells." *(on screen: cross out each as you say it)* "No wonder it doesn't work. You built the whole house before checking if anyone wants to live in it."
- **[0:18–0:26]** "Flip it. Here's the order that actually works." *(board flips / re-numbers)*
- **[0:26–0:36]** "One: validate the offer first. Real money, not surveys. This is the step everyone skips and it's the one everything else sits on."
- **[0:36–0:46]** "Two: a low-ticket offer, forty to a hundred bucks, that pays for your ads. Three: paid traffic — seven days of real signal beats three months of posting."
- **[0:46–0:56]** "Four: a welcome email sequence — the first two weeks after someone buys is when they buy again. Five: the high-ticket offer, sold without a sales call."
- **[0:56–1:04] CTA:** "Validate, self-liquidate, traffic, email, backend. The whole framework's free in my bio. Save this so you stop building backwards."

**On-screen — the interrupt (left side, crossed out):**
```
❌ HOW MOST COACHES BUILD
   1. post content for months
   2. build a course
   3. build a funnel
   4. ...figure out the offer (maybe)
```
**On-screen — the fix (right side, revealed):**
```
✅ THE ACTUAL ORDER
   1. validate the offer (real money)
   2. self-liquidating front end
   3. paid traffic
   4. welcome sequence
   5. backend — no sales call
```
**Sticky caption:** "Save this. Stop building backwards."

**B-roll:** screen recording where you literally strike through the wrong list, then the right one fades in. Face cam in corner.

## Variant C — "12 Months of Posting" (problem-first / PAS)

**Angle:** Open on the pain the audience is living — the content merry-go-round — then hand them the framework as the exit. Maps to audience pain point #2 + content-ideas #16.

**Hook [0:00–0:04]:**
"Posted for a year and made basically nothing? It's not your content. It's your order of operations."

**Script:**
- **[0:04–0:14]** "Here's what the content treadmill never tells you: posting is step *three* in a five-step system. If steps one and two aren't in place, no amount of posting saves it. You can go viral and still not sell anything."
- **[0:14–0:26]** "Step one — the one you skipped — validate the offer with real money before you build anything. Not a poll. Not 'what would you guys want?' A purchase. If it doesn't sell to a few people, it won't sell to a few thousand."
- **[0:26–0:38]** "Step two — a low-ticket offer that pays for ads, so you're not dependent on the algorithm noticing you. That's the leverage. Now traffic is something you *buy*, on demand, not something you beg for."
- **[0:38–0:50]** "Then — and only then — step three: paid traffic. Step four: an email sequence that follows up while people are still warm. Step five: the high-ticket offer they buy without a single call."
- **[0:50–1:00] CTA:** "If you've been grinding on content with nothing to show — you weren't bad at it. You were doing it out of order. Free framework's in my bio. Start at step one."

**On-screen — the reframe:**
```
"my content isn't working"
        ↓
posting is STEP 3.
you skipped 1 and 2.

1. validate the offer ← start here
2. self-liquidating front end
3. paid traffic
4. welcome sequence
5. backend, no call
```
**Sticky caption:** "Save this. You're not bad at content — you're out of order."

**B-roll:** open on a fast scroll through a "dead" content feed (low views), then cut to the clean numbered board. Reframe energy: calm, not preachy (voice.md).

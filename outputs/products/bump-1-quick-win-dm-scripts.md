## The 5 Scripts

### Script 1: The Reconnection

**Use when:** Reaching out to someone you haven't talked to in months (or years).

**Why it works:** No pitch, just genuine reconnection. Opens the door without pressure.

```
Hey [Name]!

Random thought — I was [doing something that reminded you of them] and thought of you.

How have things been? Last I remember you were [something you remember about their situation].

Would love to catch up if you have a few minutes sometime.
```

**Example:**

```
Hey Sarah!

Random thought — I was listening to a podcast about career transitions and thought of you.

How have things been? Last I remember you were thinking about starting your own coaching practice.

Would love to catch up if you have a few minutes sometime.
```

**Follow-up (if they respond positively):**

```
So good to hear from you!

I've actually been working on something new — helping [type of person] with [outcome]. Totally selfish ask, but would you be open to giving me feedback on it? Just 15 min.

Either way, great to reconnect.
```

### Script 2: The Value Drop

**Use when:** You have something genuinely helpful to share (not a pitch disguised as value).

**Why it works:** Leads with giving, not asking. Positions you as helpful.

```
Hey [Name],

I put together [resource/insight/template] for [type of person] dealing with [specific problem].

Given [what you know about their situation], thought you might find it useful.

No strings attached — just figured I'd share since I made it anyway.

[Link or attachment]
```

**Example:**

```
Hey Marcus,

I put together a quick checklist for coaches figuring out their first offer — the exact things I wish I'd known when I started.

Given that you mentioned you're working on your consulting positioning, thought you might find it useful.

No strings attached — just figured I'd share since I made it anyway.

[Link]
```

**Follow-up (if they engage):**

```
Glad it was helpful!

Quick question — what's the biggest thing you're stuck on right now with [topic]? Always curious what people are working through.
```

### Script 3: The Soft Pitch

**Use when:** You're ready to mention your offer to someone who might be interested.

**Why it works:** Asks permission before pitching. Low pressure, high respect.

```
Hey [Name],

Been working on something and you came to mind.

I'm helping [specific type of person] with [specific outcome]. Given [what you know about them], thought it might be relevant.

Would you be open to hearing about it? Totally fine if not — just didn't want to assume.
```

**Example:**

```
Hey David,

Been working on something and you came to mind.

I'm helping coaches who are stuck at the "I know I can help people but can't seem to get clients" phase — basically helping them nail their offer and start getting paid.

Given that you mentioned struggling with this exact thing last time we talked, thought it might be relevant.

Would you be open to hearing about it? Totally fine if not — just didn't want to assume.
```

**If they say yes:**

```
Awesome — here's the quick version:

[2-3 sentence description of your offer]

If that sounds interesting, happy to share more details or hop on a quick call to see if it's a fit.

What questions do you have?
```

### Script 4: The "I'm Building Something"

**Use when:** You want feedback AND you want to plant seeds for future sales.

**Why it works:** People love giving feedback. It's flattering, low-commitment, and often leads to "actually, I'd buy that."

```
Hey [Name],

I'm building something new and could use your brain for 10 minutes.

Working on [what you're building] for [who it's for]. Since you're [why they're relevant — either target customer or someone who knows the space], I figured you'd have good perspective.

Would you be open to a quick call or voice note exchange? Just want honest feedback — not trying to sell you anything.
```

**Example:**

```
Hey Lisa,

I'm building something new and could use your brain for 10 minutes.

Working on a system to help coaches validate their offers before spending months building courses nobody buys. Since you've been in the coaching space for a while, I figured you'd have good perspective.

Would you be open to a quick call or voice note exchange? Just want honest feedback — not trying to sell you anything.
```

**During/after feedback:**

If they give feedback and seem interested, it's natural to ask:

```
This is super helpful. Quick question — is this something you'd personally be interested in, or do you know anyone who would be?

No pressure either way, just trying to figure out if I'm on the right track.
```

### Script 5: The Follow-Up

**Use when:** Someone showed interest before but the conversation died.

**Why it works:** Most sales happen in the follow-up. One message isn't enough.

```
Hey [Name],

Circling back — we chatted about [topic] a few weeks ago and I wanted to check in.

Still working on [the thing they expressed interest in]? No worries if timing isn't right — just didn't want to leave you hanging.
```

**Example:**

```
Hey Tom,

Circling back — we chatted about your offer positioning a few weeks ago and I wanted to check in.

Still working on getting clarity on that? No worries if timing isn't right — just didn't want to leave you hanging.
```

**If they respond that they're still interested but haven't moved forward:**

```
Totally get it — life happens.

Quick thought: what's the ONE thing that's blocking you from moving forward on this? Sometimes it helps to name it.
```

## How To Use These

### Step 1: Make a List

Write down 20-30 people who:
- Know who you are
- Might have the problem you solve
- You haven't talked to recently

This includes: old colleagues, past clients, people from courses/communities, social media connections you've actually interacted with.

### Step 2: Pick the Right Script

| Their Situation | Script to Use |
|-----------------|---------------|

| They might be a good fit | The Soft Pitch |
| You want feedback + sales | The "I'm Building Something" |

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Going straight to pitch**
Wrong: "Hey! I have this amazing program..."
Right: Start with connection, THEN offer when relevant.

**Mistake 2: Being too vague**
Wrong: "I help people with their business"
Right: "I help coaches figure out their first offer so they can start getting paid"

**Mistake 3: Not following up**
One message isn't enough. Most people are busy, not uninterested.

**Mistake 4: Sending to cold contacts**
These scripts are for WARM outreach. People who know you. Don't DM strangers.

**Mistake 5: Pitching everyone**
Not every conversation needs to end in a sale. Some are just conversations. That's fine.

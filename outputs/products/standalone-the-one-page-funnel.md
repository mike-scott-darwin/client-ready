## The Case Study: Why Most Coaching Landing Pages Don't Convert

### The Problem With "Pretty" Pages

Here's what most coaches do when they need a landing page:

They hire a designer. Or they buy a Canva template. Or they spend three weeks tweaking colors, fonts, and hero images.

Then they send traffic to it. And nothing happens.

**The page looks good. But nobody buys.**

Here's why: a pretty page is not a clear page. And cold traffic doesn't care about your brand colors. They care about five things:

1. Who is this for?
2. Why am I here?
3. Why should I care?
4. Why should I believe you?
5. What do I do next?

If someone can't answer all five within 5 seconds of landing on your page, they bounce. Doesn't matter how good the design is.

### What Actually Converts

I tested this. Spent months looking at what works across practitioners running $100K+/month in paid traffic — Cat Howell, Miles Stutz, Hernan Vazquez.

The winning format isn't a pretty page. It's the **Hybrid VSL.**

Here's what that means:

**A naked VSL** is a page with just a video. No text. No proof. Just "watch this." These are dying. Cold traffic bounces because they don't know what they're watching or why they should care. Bounce rates are brutal.

**A text-only sales page** works but misses people who'd rather watch than read.

**The Hybrid VSL** combines both:

1. **Headline** at the top — the promise, visible instantly
2. **Video** — 5-15 minutes, you talking + showing the product
3. **Full text below the video** — the entire sales pitch as readable copy
4. **Visual evidence** — screenshots, GIFs of the actual product being used
5. **Mobile-first** — because that's where your traffic is

**Why it works:** Readers can read. Watchers can watch. Skimmers can scroll the screenshots. Everyone gets the same pitch in their preferred format.

This is the page structure inside this training. Not theory — the template you build with.

## Component 1: The Hybrid VSL Page Structure

### The 7 Sections (In Order)

Your one-page funnel has 7 sections. Every section has a job. Skip one and the page breaks.

---

#### Section 1: The Headline

**Job:** Stop the scroll. Tell them what this is and who it's for.

**Format:**
```
[Main headline: The transformation promise]
[Sub-headline: Who this is for + timeframe]
```

**Example:**
```
Validate Your $5K+ Offer Before You Build Anything
For coaches who are done guessing — extract, validate, and sell in one afternoon.
```

**Rules:**
- Must match your ad headline. "One pizza, get pizza." If your ad says "validate your offer in one afternoon" and your page says "build a sustainable coaching business" — you just lost them.
- No more than 15 words for the main headline
- Sub-headline adds specificity (who + timeframe)

---

#### Section 2: The Video

**Job:** Build trust. Show the product. Prove you're real.

**Format:** 5-15 minute video. You talking + screen recordings of the product.

**Structure:**
1. **Hook (0-30 sec):** Restate the headline promise
2. **Problem (30 sec - 2 min):** The pain they're feeling — articulate it better than they can
3. **Solution (2-5 min):** What the product is, how it works, what they get
4. **Proof (5-8 min):** Results — yours, clients, or walk through the product live
5. **CTA (last 30 sec):** "Scroll down and grab it"

**Rules:**
- Don't read a script robotically. Talk like you're explaining this to a friend.
- Show the actual product. Screen share the PDF. Run through the prompts. Show the template. Visual evidence is what separates your page from scam pages.
- You don't need professional production. Phone + screen recording app. Done.

---

#### Section 3: The Problem Section

**Job:** Make them feel understood. Agitate the pain.

**Format:** 3-5 short paragraphs. Written like you're in their head.

**Template:**
```
You know that feeling when [specific frustration]?

You've tried [thing they've tried]. You've bought [courses/coaching].
And you're still [stuck at the same place].

Here's the thing most people won't tell you: [the real reason they're stuck].

It's not [what they think the problem is]. It's [what the problem actually is].
```

**Rules:**
- Use their language, not yours. If they say "I can't figure out my offer," don't write "optimal value proposition development."
- Be specific. "Stuck" is vague. "Spending 45 minutes on sales calls with people who ghost you" is real.

---

#### Section 4: The Solution Section

**Job:** Show them what they're getting. Make it concrete.

**Format:** Product name + what's included + how it works.

**Template:**
```
[Product Name] is a [format] that helps you [outcome] in [timeframe].

Here's what's inside:

- **[Component 1]** — [What it does for them]
- **[Component 2]** — [What it does for them]
- **[Component 3]** — [What it does for them]

Here's how it works:

Step 1: [First thing they do]
Step 2: [Second thing they do]
Step 3: [What they walk away with]
```

**Rules:**
- Components are deliverables, not features. "5 AI prompts that extract your zone of genius" not "AI-powered extraction technology."
- Three steps max for the "how it works." If it takes more than 3 steps to explain, simplify.

---

#### Section 5: Visual Evidence

**Job:** Prove the product is real. Kill the "is this a scam?" objection.

**Format:** Screenshots, GIFs, or screen recordings of the actual product.

**What to show:**
- The PDF / guide / template — actual pages, not mockups
- The AI prompts in action — screen recording of running a prompt
- A completed example — what their output will look like
- Before/after of someone who used it

**Rules:**
- Real screenshots only. Not mockup generators. Not stock photos. The actual product.
- GIFs are better than static images — movement catches attention on scroll.
- Mobile screenshots at 1:1 or 9:16 ratio (most traffic is mobile).

---

#### Section 6: Social Proof

**Job:** Let other people do the selling.

**Format:** Testimonials, results, screenshots of wins.

**What works best:**
- Screenshots of actual messages (DMs, emails, Skool posts)
- Specific results ("landed 3 clients in first week")
- Photos of real people (increases trust)
- Video testimonials (highest trust)

**If you don't have testimonials yet:**
- Show your own results (the case study IS proof)
- Show the process working (screen record yourself using the product)
- Be honest: "This is new — here's my guarantee"

**Rules:**
- 2-3 testimonials is enough. Don't pad with weak ones.
- Place them after the solution section — proof comes after the pitch, not before.

---

#### Section 7: The CTA + Pricing

**Job:** Tell them exactly what to do next. Remove friction.

**Format:**
```
[Price with anchor: ~~$197~~ $47]
[What they get — bullet recap]
[Guarantee]
[Button: "Get Instant Access" or "Start Now"]
```

**Rules:**
- One button. One action. Don't give them 3 options.
- Restate the guarantee next to the price.
- If you have bumps, they appear on the checkout page — not on the landing page.

## Component 2: The AI Copy Generator

Three prompts that turn your offer document into landing page copy. Use ChatGPT, Claude, or any AI assistant.

**Prerequisite:** You need a completed offer document. If you don't have one, the $47 Client Ready Offer System creates it.

---

### Prompt 1: Headline + Sub-headline Generator

```
I need you to write landing page headlines for my offer.

Here's my offer document:
[Paste your complete offer document]

Generate 5 headline options. Each must:
- Be under 15 words
- State the transformation promise (not the product name)
- Be specific enough that someone knows if this is for them

For each headline, write a sub-headline that adds:
- WHO this is for
- The TIMEFRAME they'll get results
- One "without" statement (what they avoid)

Format:
Headline: [headline]
Sub-headline: [sub-headline]
```

**Pick the one that feels most like you.** If it sounds like something a guru would say, it's wrong. If it sounds like something you'd say to a friend at a coffee shop, it's right.

---

### Prompt 2: Problem + Solution Section Writer

```
Using my offer document below, write two sections for my landing page:

[Paste your offer document]

SECTION 1: THE PROBLEM (3-4 paragraphs)
- Write in second person ("You know that feeling when...")
- Use the specific pain points from my offer document
- Articulate the problem better than my ideal client can
- End with: "Here's what most people won't tell you: [the real reason they're stuck]"
- Tone: direct, empathetic, no fluff. Like a smart friend telling you the truth.

SECTION 2: THE SOLUTION (structured)
- Start with: "[Product Name] is a [format] that helps you [outcome] in [timeframe]."
- List what's included as bullet points (deliverables, not features)
- Add a 3-step "Here's how it works" section
- Tone: clear, practical. No hype words (amazing, revolutionary, game-changing).

Words to use: validate, test, extract, install, system, practical, clear, simple, proven, today
Words to avoid: revolutionary, incredible, amazing, life-changing, secrets, hack, crush it, manifest
```

```
6. SOCIAL PROOF SECTION — Write the section header and placeholder text for where testimonials go

7. CTA SECTION — Price with anchor, bullet recap of what's included, guarantee, button text

Tone: direct, practical, conversational. Short sentences. No fluff. Like a smart friend explaining something clearly.
```

## Component 3: The 5-Second Test

**Run this test on every page before you spend a dollar on traffic.**

Open your page. Look at it for 5 seconds — literally count to five — then look away.

Answer these five questions:

| # | Question | What You're Checking |
|---|----------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Who is this for?** | Target audience visible instantly |
| 2 | **Why am I here?** | The payoff / result they'll get |
| 3 | **Why should I care?** | Unique differentiator (what makes this different) |
| 4 | **Why should I believe?** | Trust signals, proof, credibility |
| 5 | **What do I do next?** | Clear CTA — one action, obvious |

**If you can't answer all five in 5 seconds, the page isn't ready.**

### How to Fix Each Failure

| Failed Question | The Fix |
|----------------|---------|
| "Who is this for?" not clear | Add specificity to the sub-headline: "For coaches who..." |
| "Why am I here?" not clear | Your headline doesn't state the outcome. Rewrite it. |
| "Why should I care?" not clear | Add a "without" statement: "without sales calls / without quitting your job" |
| "Why should I believe?" not clear | Add proof above the fold: testimonial snippet, result, or credibility marker |
| "What do I do next?" not clear | Make the button bigger, more obvious, or move it higher |

### The "Send It to a Friend" Test

Send your page to someone who doesn't know your business. Ask them: "What does this page sell and who is it for?"

If they can't answer both in one sentence, your page isn't clear enough.

## Component 5: The Mobile-First Checklist

80%+ of your paid traffic will be on mobile. If your page doesn't work on a phone, you're burning money.

### Before You Launch

- [ ] **Font size:** Body text is 16px+ (readable without zooming)
- [ ] **Button size:** CTA buttons are thumb-sized (minimum 48px height)
- [ ] **Button placement:** First CTA visible without scrolling on mobile
- [ ] **Image loading:** All images load in under 3 seconds on 4G
- [ ] **Video:** Plays on mobile without autoplay issues
- [ ] **No horizontal scroll:** Nothing overflows the screen width
- [ ] **Headline visible:** Full headline visible without scrolling
- [ ] **Whitespace:** Sections have breathing room — not crammed together
- [ ] **Forms:** If you have a form, fields are easy to tap and type on phone
- [ ] **Test on actual phone:** Not just browser preview — actually open it on your phone

### The 3-Second Rule

If your page takes longer than 3 seconds to load on mobile, you lose 53% of visitors. Check page speed:

1. Open Google PageSpeed Insights
2. Paste your URL
3. Check the mobile score
4. Target: 70+ on mobile

**Common fixes:**
- Compress images (use TinyPNG or Squoosh)
- Remove unnecessary scripts
- Use lazy loading for below-the-fold images

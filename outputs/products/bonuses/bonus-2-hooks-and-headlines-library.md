# 55+ High-Converting Hooks & Fill-in-the-Blank Headlines

The first line decides everything. If it doesn't stop the scroll, nothing after it matters — the best offer in the world dies behind a weak hook.

So here are 60 templates. Every one has [fill-in-the-blank] slots. Drop in your offer, your buyer, your pain point, and you've got a hook. Use them for posts, ads, email subject lines, page headlines, DMs — anywhere the first line has to earn the second.

**How to use this:**
- Fill the brackets with details from your offer document.
- Say it out loud. If it doesn't sound like you, cut a word.
- Keep it specific. "Coaches" is weak. "Coaches still working a 9-to-5" is a hook.
- No income or earnings claims. No fake urgency. No hype words.

Fill them in. Test them. Keep the ones that land.

---

## Curiosity

Open a loop. Make them need the next line.

1. The real reason [your ideal client] can't [get the outcome] isn't what you think.
2. Here's what nobody tells you about [topic / the thing they're struggling with].
3. I changed one thing about [my offer / my process] and [specific non-money result] followed.
4. Most [ideal client] are one small fix away from [the outcome]. Here's the fix.
5. There's a reason [common thing they do] keeps not working. It's not effort.
6. The thing that finally worked for [me / my clients] wasn't [the obvious thing].
7. Everyone's focused on [popular tactic]. The actual lever is [the real one].
8. What [the outcome] actually takes — minus the part everyone skips.
9. I almost quit [the thing] before I noticed [the small detail that changed it].
10. The quiet difference between [ideal client who stays stuck] and [one who breaks through].

---

## Pain / Problem

Name the ache before you name the fix.

11. [Painful situation]? You're not bad at this. You've got the wrong [offer / setup / approach].
12. Tired of [specific frustration] with nothing to show for it?
13. If you're [doing the exhausting thing] and still [not getting the result], read this.
14. Stuck explaining what you do every single time someone asks? Here's why.
15. [Ideal client], the problem isn't [what you blame yourself for]. It's [the real cause].
16. You keep [effort] and [prospects] keep saying "let me think about it." Here's what's really happening.
17. The most exhausting part of [their work] isn't the work. It's [the real pain].
18. You didn't fail at [thing]. [The thing] was built to keep you stuck.
19. [Frustrating outcome] over and over? Stop blaming [the wrong culprit].
20. That knot in your stomach when someone asks "so what do you charge?" — let's fix that.

## Outcome / Transformation

Show them the after. No dollar figures — the feeling.

21. From [before state] to [after state] — in [realistic timeframe].
22. Imagine [specific desirable outcome] — without [the thing they dread].
23. Wake up to [a client / a reply / a result] that showed up while you slept.
24. Stop [the draining activity]. Start [the outcome], on repeat.
25. Picture [ideal client] finally able to [do the thing they've wanted to do].
26. [The outcome] — clear enough that people say "I need this" before you finish your sentence.
27. What your day looks like once [the offer / the system] is doing the work: [short picture].
28. Go from [confused about X] to [dead clear on X] this [timeframe].
29. [The result] without [the grind everyone assumes it requires].
30. Your [offer] does the selling. You do the [thing you actually love].

## Contrarian ("Wrong")

Take the common advice. Break it. Rebuild it.

31. [Common advice everyone repeats]. Wrong. Here's what actually works.
32. Everyone says [do X to get Y]. Wrong. [X] is exactly why you're stuck.
33. "[Popular belief]." No. The opposite is true, and here's why.
34. You don't need [the thing everyone's selling]. You need [the real thing]. Period, full stop.
35. [Widely accepted rule]? I broke it, and [specific non-money result] got better.
36. Stop [the thing everyone tells you to do]. It's costing you [the outcome].
37. The advice to [common tactic] is quietly wrecking [ideal client]'s [thing].
38. More [effort / content / hustle] isn't the answer. [The real answer] is.
39. You can't [grow / scale / push] your way out of [the wrong thing]. You fix it or you burn it down.
40. Nobody's coming to save your [offer / funnel / business]. Good news: you can fix it yourself.

## Social Proof

Point at the pattern without inventing a result.

41. Every [ideal client] I talk to has the same [problem]. Here's the pattern.
42. I keep hearing the same sentence from [ideal client]: "[the thing they say]." Here's what it means.
43. The [ideal client] who break through all do this one thing first.
44. There's a reason the same [type of person] keeps [getting the result]. It's repeatable.
45. What [ideal client] who finally [got clear / got unstuck] have in common.
46. After enough conversations with [ideal client], one thing is obvious: [the insight].
47. The [ideal client] who charge more don't have [more of X]. They have [the one thing].

---

## Question

Ask the question already in their head.

48. What if [the thing you blame] isn't actually the problem?
49. Why do [ideal client] keep [doing X] when it clearly isn't working?
50. What would change if you could [outcome] in [timeframe] instead of [the long way]?
51. Ever wonder why [common frustrating thing] happens no matter how hard you try?
52. Can someone understand what you sell in 60 seconds? Be honest.
53. What's stopping you from [the outcome] right now — really?
54. If your offer had to sell itself with no explanation, would it?

---

## Direct / Offer

Say the thing. Name the offer. Give the next step.

55. [Offer name]: [the outcome], in [timeframe]. Here's how it works.
56. I built [offer] for [ideal client] who want [outcome] without [the thing they dread].
57. Want [the outcome]? Here's the exact [system / process] — [number] steps, that's it.
58. Don't buy [offer]... unless you're done with [the pain it removes].
59. [Outcome] or your money back. That's the whole deal.
60. Here's what to do next: [single clear action]. That's it. Let's go.

---

## Quick Guide: Which Category, When

- **Cold audience, ad or post** → Curiosity, Pain, or Contrarian. They don't know you yet — you have to earn the second line.
- **Warm audience, email or DM** → Outcome, Question, or Direct. They know you; get to the point.
- **Page headline** → Outcome or Direct. The buyer landed here on purpose — tell them what they get.
- **Subject line** → Curiosity or Question, kept short.

**One rule for all 60:** specific beats clever. A boring, precise hook aimed at exactly one person beats a slick one aimed at everyone. Fill the brackets with real detail from your offer and it'll do the work.

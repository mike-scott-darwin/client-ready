# AI Offer Prompt Library

You already have the 5 core prompts that extract your offer. These are the next 9.

Think of them as the second shift. The core prompts pull the offer out of your head. These sharpen it, name it, price it, and put it to work — the one-liner, the objections, the story, the lead magnet, 30 days of content, the DM, and a pricing sanity check.

Every prompt does one thing. Copy it, paste it into ChatGPT or Claude, and give it real answers. The AI structures what you know. It doesn't invent it for you. Garbage in, garbage out — so answer honestly.

Before you start, have these ready (you built them with the core prompts):
- Your Zone of Genius statement
- Your Ideal Client Profile
- Your core pain point
- Your offer document

Let's go.

---

## Prompt 6: Refine and Name the Offer

**What it does:** Takes your rough offer and tightens it into something clear, plus gives you name options that describe the result instead of the category.

```
Here's my current offer:

[paste your offer document, or describe your offer in a few sentences]

My ideal client is:

[paste your Ideal Client Profile]

I want you to do two things.

FIRST, sharpen the offer. Rewrite it so it passes this test: a stranger
should understand what they get, who it's for, and why it matters within
60 seconds — with zero explanation from me. Cut anything vague. If a phrase
could apply to any coach, replace it with something specific to me.

SECOND, give me 10 name options for this offer. Rules for the names:
- Describe the RESULT or the timeframe, not the category (not "Coaching
  Program," not "Mastermind")
- Plain language a tired person could understand
- No hype words — keep it plain and grounded

For each name, add one line explaining what it signals to the buyer.
```

**What to look for:** A version of your offer you could say out loud without stumbling, and 2-3 names that make you think "that's actually what I do." If every name feels generic, your offer isn't specific enough yet — go back and add detail about who it's for.

## Prompt 7: Write Your One-Liner 10 Ways

**What it does:** Generates 10 different one-sentence versions of your offer so you can pick the one that lands, instead of marrying the first thing you wrote.

```
Here's my offer and who it's for:

Offer: [paste your sharpened offer or one-line description]
Ideal client: [paste your Ideal Client Profile]
Core pain I solve: [paste your core pain point]

Write my offer as a one-sentence statement 10 different ways. Use a
different structure for each one. Include versions built on these patterns:

1. "I help [specific person] [specific outcome] in [timeframe]."
2. "[Outcome] — without [the thing they dread]."
3. A version that leads with the timeframe.
4. A version that leads with the pain.
5. A version that names the exact end result they'd brag about.
6. A "from [before state] to [after state]" version.
7. A version phrased as what they'll be able to do, not what I deliver.
8. A version short enough to fit in a social media bio.
9. A version that names who it's NOT for.
10. The plainest, most boring, clearest possible version.

Keep every version specific. No hype words. No income or earnings claims.
Number them so I can pick.
```

**What to look for:** Read them out loud. The winner is the one you can say without cringing and that a prospect could repeat back to a friend. Pick one to lead with and keep two as backups to test.

## Prompt 8: Generate Objection Responses

**What it does:** Surfaces the real reasons someone hesitates and writes calm, non-pushy responses to each — so you're never caught flat-footed in a conversation.

```
Here's my offer:

[paste your offer document or description, including the price if you have one]

My ideal client is:

[paste your Ideal Client Profile]

Do two things.

FIRST, list the top 8 objections this specific buyer is likely to raise —
the real ones, including the ones they think but don't say out loud (price,
timing, "will this work for me," "I've tried things before," trust, etc.).

SECOND, for each objection write a response that:
- Agrees with the real concern underneath it (don't argue)
- Reframes it honestly
- Ends by handing the decision back to them — no pressure, no fake urgency

Tone: direct, calm, like a smart friend explaining how it works. Short
sentences. No hype. No guilt-tripping. Do not promise any specific income
or earnings.
```

**What to look for:** Responses that sound like something you'd actually say, not a script. If any answer feels slimy or pushy, cut it. The best responses agree with the concern before reframing it.

## Prompt 9: Write Your Origin Story (Short + Long)

**What it does:** Turns the messy real version of how you got here into a tight story you can use in your bio, your emails, and your sales page.

```
I want to write my origin story — the honest version of why I do this work.

Here are the raw facts (I'll answer, you shape them — don't invent anything):

1. What I was struggling with before I figured this out: [answer]
2. The turning point — the moment something changed: [answer]
3. What I discovered, learned, or built that made the difference: [answer]
4. What that changed for me: [answer]
5. Why I now help other people with this: [answer]

Write my story in three lengths:
- A 2-sentence version (for a bio or the bottom of an email)
- A 100-word version (for an about section)
- A 250-word version (for a sales page or a post)

Keep it true to what I gave you. Plain, direct, human. No exaggeration.
No hero-worship of myself. No invented results or numbers. If something's
missing to make it land, ask me — don't fill the gap with fiction.
```

**What to look for:** A story that sounds like you, not a movie trailer. The turning point should be specific. If the AI adds a result or a detail you didn't give it, delete it — your story only works if it's true.

## Prompt 10: Create a Simple Lead Magnet

**What it does:** Pulls one small, genuinely useful free thing out of your offer — something a prospect can use in 10 minutes that naturally leads to wanting the paid thing.

```
Here's my offer and who it's for:

Offer: [paste your offer or description]
Ideal client: [paste your Ideal Client Profile]
Core pain: [paste your core pain point]

Design one simple lead magnet I can create today. It must:
- Solve ONE small, specific piece of my ideal client's problem
- Be usable in under 10 minutes by the person who gets it
- Be something I can make fast (a checklist, a one-page template, a short
  set of questions, a swipe example — not a 40-page ebook)
- Naturally lead the person toward wanting my paid offer, without being a
  disguised sales pitch

Give me:
1. Three title options for it
2. The exact format (checklist / template / cheat sheet / etc.)
3. The actual content, drafted in full, ready for me to review and use
4. One sentence I'd say to hand it over ("Reply and I'll send you...")

Keep it genuinely useful on its own. No hype. No earnings claims.
```

**What to look for:** Something you'd be happy to give away because it actually helps — and that leaves the person wanting the next step. If it feels like a sales pitch wearing a costume, ask for a version that leads with value.

## Prompt 11: Spin 30 Days of Content From Your Offer

**What it does:** Turns your one offer into a month of content ideas, each tied to a real pain, belief, or objection your buyer holds — so you never stare at a blank screen.

```
Here's everything about my offer:

Offer: [paste your offer document]
Ideal client: [paste your Ideal Client Profile]
Pain points: [paste your pain points from the pain-mapping prompt]
Common objections: [paste a few objections if you have them]

Give me 30 content ideas — one per day for a month — all pulled from this
offer. Spread them across these buckets:

- Reframes (a belief my buyer has, flipped to how it actually works)
- Myths I can bust
- Common mistakes my buyer makes
- Small wins / quick tips they can use today
- Behind-the-scenes of how I work
- Objection-answering posts (one objection each)
- "Here's the truth about [topic]" posts

For each idea give me: a hook line (the first sentence, scroll-stopping),
the core point in one line, and which bucket it's in. Number them 1-30.

Voice: direct, plain, confident, no hype. No income or earnings claims.
No fake urgency.
```

**What to look for:** Hooks that make you want to keep reading. Circle the 5-7 strongest and write those first. If a bunch feel same-y, ask it to make the hooks more specific to your exact buyer.

## Prompt 12: Write a Warm-Audience DM

**What it does:** Writes a short, natural direct message to someone who already knows you — a feedback ask that opens a real conversation instead of a cold pitch.

```
I want to message people in my warm audience — past clients, people who've
asked my advice, engaged followers — about my offer.

My offer one-liner: [paste your one-liner]
Who it's for: [paste short description]

Write me 3 versions of a short DM I can send. Each one should:
- Sound like a real message from a friend, not a broadcast
- Lead with genuine curiosity or a feedback ask, NOT a pitch
- Reference that I value their opinion or that we know each other
- End with an easy, low-pressure question they can actually answer
- Be short enough to read on a phone without scrolling

Make one casual, one slightly more direct, one that asks if they know
someone who'd want this. No hype, no "I'm SO excited," no hard sell.
Do not promise any income or earnings.
```

**What to look for:** A message you'd actually send to someone whose respect you care about. If it reads like a mass blast, it's wrong. The best version feels like a genuine ask — and a "yeah, I'd want that" is a buying signal you follow up on.

## Prompt 13: Sanity-Check Your Pricing

**What it does:** Pressure-tests your price against what you're actually delivering and how clear the offer is — so you're pricing with a reason, not a guess.

```
Here's my offer and the price I'm considering:

Offer: [paste your offer document]
Who it's for: [paste your Ideal Client Profile]
Price I'm thinking: [your price]
What's included / how I deliver it: [list the components and format]

Give me a straight pricing sanity-check. Cover:

1. Does the price match the clarity and specificity of the offer? (Vague
   offers can't hold a high price; specific ones can.)
2. Where in the offer is the value the buyer is really paying for — and is
   it obvious to them, or hidden?
3. What would make this price feel like an easy yes vs. a hesitation?
4. Two alternative price points to consider, and what each one signals.
5. What I could add or clarify to justify a higher price WITHOUT adding
   more work for me.

Be direct. Tell me if the price looks off and why. Do NOT tell me what I'll
earn or make — only reason about the price relative to the offer's clarity
and value.
```

**What to look for:** A clear reason your price makes sense — or an honest flag that it doesn't. If the AI can't point to where the value lives, your buyer won't see it either. Fix the clarity before you touch the number.

## Prompt 14: Stress-Test the Whole Offer (Bonus)

**What it does:** Runs your finished offer through the 4 principles of a Client Ready Offer and tells you exactly where it's still fuzzy.

```
Here's my complete offer:

[paste your full offer document, including price and how it's delivered]

Score my offer against these 4 principles. Be honest and specific — I want
to know what's weak, not a pep talk.

1. Clear, tangible outcome + specific timeframe — Can the buyer picture the
   result and when they'll get it?
2. Clear process — Can they see the steps before they buy?
3. Clear delivery — Do they know exactly what they get and how?
4. Easy to get started — Is the first step obvious and low-friction?

For each principle: give it a rating (Clear / Fuzzy / Missing), quote the
exact part of my offer that earns that rating, and tell me the one change
that would move it up. End with the single most important fix to make first.

Direct feedback only. No hype. No earnings claims.
```

**What to look for:** The "Fuzzy" and "Missing" ratings — that's your to-do list. Fix the single most important thing first, then run it again. When all four come back Clear, your offer is ready to put in front of real people.

## How to Use These

You don't need all 9 at once. Grab the one that matches where you're stuck:

- Offer still feels muddy → Prompt 6, then Prompt 14
- Can't explain what you do in one line → Prompt 7
- People hesitate and you freeze → Prompt 8
- Nobody knows your story → Prompt 9
- Need something to give away → Prompt 10
- Blank on what to post → Prompt 11
- Ready to reach out → Prompt 12
- Unsure what to charge → Prompt 13

Run it, review what comes back, keep what's true, cut what isn't. The AI does the heavy lifting. You make the decisions.

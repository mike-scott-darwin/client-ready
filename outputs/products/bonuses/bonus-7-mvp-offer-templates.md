# Minimum Viable Offer Templates

Here's what most coaches get wrong: they think they need to build something big before they can sell anything. A course. A membership. A 12-week signature program. Months of work before a single dollar comes in.

Wrong.

You can package an offer from what you already have — this week. The trick is picking a *format* that fits your skill and your time, then filling in the blanks.

Below are 8 proven offer formats. Each one is small enough to launch fast and real enough to deliver a result. For each format you get: what it is, when to use it, what you need to launch it, and a fill-in-the-blank one-liner you can drop straight into a page or a DM.

Pick the format that matches what you can deliver *right now*. Not the one you wish you could deliver in six months.

---

## Format 1: The Audit

**What it is:** You review something the client already has — their offer, their page, their profile, their numbers — and hand back a clear, prioritized list of what to fix.

**When to use it:** When your skill is diagnosis. You can look at a mess and instantly see the three things that matter. Great as a low-priced entry offer that opens the door to bigger work.

**What you need to launch it:** A short intake form (send me X), a repeatable checklist you review against, and a simple way to deliver findings (a Loom video or a one-page doc). That's it.

**Fill-in-the-blank one-liner:**
> "I'll review your [thing] and send you the [top 3 / top 5] changes that will [specific result] — in [timeframe]."

*Example:* "I'll review your coaching sales page and send you the top 3 changes that will make it clearer to your buyer — in 48 hours."

---

## Format 2: The Intensive

**What it is:** One focused session (60–120 minutes) where you and the client solve one specific problem together, start to finish.

**When to use it:** When you can create a real outcome in a single working session and the client wants speed. Higher-priced than an audit because you're in the room doing the work with them.

**What you need to launch it:** A booking link, a pre-session questionnaire so you show up prepared, and a clear agenda for the session so it stays on one problem.

**Fill-in-the-blank one-liner:**
> "In one [90-minute] session, we'll [solve one specific problem] together so you leave with [tangible thing they walk away with]."

*Example:* "In one 90-minute session, we'll write your one-sentence offer together so you leave with a clear answer to 'what do you do?'"

---

## Format 3: The Template / Toolkit Pack

**What it is:** A bundle of the assets you already use — swipe files, checklists, scripts, spreadsheets — packaged so someone else can plug them in.

**When to use it:** When you have proven work products sitting on your hard drive. This is the fastest thing to sell because the work is mostly done. Delivery is a download or a portal link.

**What you need to launch it:** The assets themselves (cleaned up and de-personalized), a short "how to use this" guide, and a place to host them.

**Fill-in-the-blank one-liner:**
> "The exact [templates / scripts / checklists] I use to [do the thing] — so you don't have to start from a blank page."

*Example:* "The exact outreach scripts I use to start client conversations — so you don't have to start from a blank page."

---

## Format 4: The Mini-Course

**What it is:** A short, focused set of lessons (3–7 videos or modules) that teaches one skill and gets the buyer to one outcome.

**When to use it:** When the transformation needs teaching, not just tools, but you don't want to build a giant course. Keep it tight — one outcome, no filler.

**What you need to launch it:** A simple outline (each lesson = one step toward the outcome), a way to record (screen share or talking head), and a hosting spot. You can record it in an afternoon.

**Fill-in-the-blank one-liner:**
> "A [short] course that walks you through [the specific skill], step by step, so you can [outcome] without [the usual struggle]."

*Example:* "A short course that walks you through writing your first lead magnet, step by step, so you can grow a list without hiring a copywriter."

---

## Format 5: The Group Cohort

**What it is:** A small group goes through the same process together over a fixed number of weeks, with you leading and a shared start and finish date.

**When to use it:** When the outcome takes a few weeks, peer energy helps, and you want to serve more people per hour of your time than 1:1 allows.

**What you need to launch it:** A week-by-week outline, a call cadence (usually weekly), a place to meet (Zoom + a group chat or community), and a cap on seats so it stays small.

**Fill-in-the-blank one-liner:**
> "A [4-week] group program where [specific people] work through [the process] together and finish with [the outcome]."

*Example:* "A 4-week group program where new coaches work through building their first offer together and finish with a validated one-sentence pitch."

---

## Format 6: The Done-With-You Sprint

**What it is:** A short, hands-on engagement where you build the thing *with* the client — not for them, not just teaching them. You share the work.

**When to use it:** When clients get stuck doing it alone but don't need (or can't afford) a full done-for-you build. This sits between a course and full service, and it's easy to price well.

**What you need to launch it:** A defined scope (exactly what gets built), a start and end date, a working rhythm (calls + async reviews), and a clear "done" so it doesn't drag on.

**Fill-in-the-blank one-liner:**
> "Over [2 weeks], we'll build your [specific asset] together — I guide, you do, we finish with [the deliverable] live."

*Example:* "Over 2 weeks, we'll build your landing page together — I guide, you do, we finish with a page that's live and taking payments."

---

## Format 7: The Challenge

**What it is:** A short, energetic push (usually 5–7 days) where participants take one small action each day toward a single outcome, with you leading the pace.

**When to use it:** When the outcome comes from momentum and doing, not just learning. Great for filling a group, warming up an audience, or leading into a bigger offer.

**What you need to launch it:** A daily prompt or task for each day, a place to post them, a way to keep people moving (email or group posts), and one clear "by the end you'll have X."

**Fill-in-the-blank one-liner:**
> "A [5-day] challenge where you do one small thing each day, so by the end you'll have [the outcome] — even if you're starting from zero."

*Example:* "A 5-day challenge where you do one small thing each day, so by the end you'll have your ideal client written down — even if you're starting from zero."

---

## Format 8: The Office-Hours Retainer

**What it is:** Ongoing access to you on a schedule — a weekly or biweekly call, a Q&A slot, or async questions answered inside a set window — for a flat monthly fee.

**When to use it:** When clients don't need you to build things, they need you *available* when they hit a wall. Low delivery load, recurring revenue, and it deepens relationships that lead to bigger offers.

**What you need to launch it:** A fixed cadence (e.g. one call a week), clear boundaries on what's included, a way to collect questions, and a simple monthly subscription.

**Fill-in-the-blank one-liner:**
> "[Weekly / biweekly] access to me for [specific kind of support], so whenever you get stuck on [the problem area], you're never stuck for long."

*Example:* "Weekly access to me for offer and funnel questions, so whenever you get stuck building, you're never stuck for long."

---

## How to Choose

Don't overthink it. Match the format to your reality:

- **Have proven assets already?** Start with the Template Pack.
- **Great at spotting problems fast?** Start with the Audit.
- **Best in a live conversation?** Start with the Intensive.
- **Need to teach a skill?** Start with the Mini-Course.
- **Want to serve more people at once?** Group Cohort or Challenge.
- **Clients keep getting stuck alone?** Done-With-You Sprint.
- **Want recurring income with light delivery?** Office-Hours Retainer.

You can turn one skill into three or four of these. But you only need one to start.

Pick the format you could launch this week. Fill in the blank. Put it in front of real people. Let's go.

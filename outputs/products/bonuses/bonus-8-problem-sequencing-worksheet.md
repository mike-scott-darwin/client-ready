# Problem Sequencing Worksheet

Here's what most coaches get wrong: they try to build one offer that solves everything. Every problem, every stage, one giant package. It ends up bloated, confusing, and expensive to deliver — and it sells to nobody.

The fix is to see your client's problems as a *chain*.

Nobody has just one problem. They have a first burning problem. Once you solve it, a new problem shows up — one they couldn't even see until the first was gone. And behind all of it sits a bigger problem that's the real reason they came to you.

When you understand the chain, you stop guessing at what to sell. Each problem in the chain becomes an offer. And they buy in order, because each solution naturally reveals the next thing they need.

This worksheet helps you map that chain and line up your offers against it.

---

## The "Strategically Incomplete" Offer (Plainly)

An offer should completely solve one problem. Fully. No holding back the good stuff.

But solving one problem always uncovers the next one. That's not a flaw — that's how progress works. You help someone get their first client, and now they have a new problem: how do I get the second, third, and fourth without burning out? You didn't leave them hanging. You solved what you promised. The next problem appeared on its own.

That's what "strategically incomplete" means: **each offer fully solves one problem and naturally surfaces the next.** You're not tricking anyone. You're not withholding. You're meeting them where they are, solving that, and being ready when the next wall shows up.

The rule from the pros holds here: an offer should never feel *required* to get results from the one before it. The moment a buyer senses "this won't work unless I also buy the next thing," you've broken trust and tanked both. Each offer stands on its own. The next one is there when they're ready — because they hit the next real problem, not because you engineered a dependency.

- **First burning problem** → your front-end offer
- **The problem that appears once it's solved** → your bump / next offer
- **The bigger problem behind it all** → your high-ticket / backend offer

## Part 3: The Sanity Check

Run each offer through these questions. If you answer "no" to any of them, fix that offer before you sell it.

- [ ] Does the front-end offer fully solve the first problem on its own? (No dependency on the next offer.)
- [ ] Does solving the first problem *naturally* create the second? (Or did I invent a problem to sell something?)
- [ ] Could someone buy just the front-end and feel they got their money's worth? (They should.)
- [ ] Does each offer solve exactly one problem — not a bundle of five?
- [ ] Do the offers step up in price as the problems get bigger? (Small problem, small price. Big problem, big price.)
- [ ] Would the next offer feel like a helpful next step, not a bait-and-switch? (Ask a real client.)

## Worked Example

Here's the chain filled in for one specific person.

**Who:** A newly-certified life coach with skills but no paying clients yet.

**First burning problem:** "I don't have a single client and I don't even know what to offer them. When people ask what I do, I ramble."

**Solving it looks like:** One clear offer they can say in a sentence, and the confidence to put it in front of people.

**The next problem (once solved):** "Okay, I know what I sell — but now I have to actually reach out to people, and I have no idea what to say without sounding desperate or pushy."

**Solving that looks like:** Knowing exactly what to say to start real conversations that lead to clients.

**The bigger problem behind it all:** "I want this to be a real business that runs without me hustling every day and second-guessing myself — something built around my life, not the other way around."

**Solving that looks like:** A validated offer, a funnel that brings people in, and the systems and support to grow steadily.

**Offers lined up against the chain:**

| Chain position | Problem | Offer | Promise |
|---|---|---|---|
| Front-end | No clear offer | Offer-clarity mini-course + template | "Get one clear offer you can say in a sentence — in an afternoon." |
| Next offer | Don't know what to say to reach out | Outreach script pack | "Start your first real client conversation this week — without sounding pushy." |
| Backend | Want a real, steady business | Group coaching / done-with-you build | "Build a validated offer and a funnel that brings clients in — with support the whole way." |

Notice: the mini-course fully solves the first problem. A buyer who stops there still got exactly what they paid for. But once they *have* a clear offer, the "now what do I say?" problem is real and obvious — so the script pack is the natural next step, not a cash grab. And once they're having conversations, the "I want this to be a real business" problem is what pulls them toward the backend.

That's the chain. Solve one problem completely. Let it reveal the next. Be ready with the next offer when it does.

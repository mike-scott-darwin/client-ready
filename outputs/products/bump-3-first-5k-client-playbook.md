## Part 1: Why $5K Is Easier Than $500

Most coaches underprice because they're afraid to charge what they're worth. Here's the thing — a $500 offer and a $5K offer require almost the same amount of selling effort. The difference isn't the price. It's the clarity.

**At $500:**
- Buyer thinks: "Is this worth it? There are cheaper options."
- You compete on price with every other coach
- You need 10 clients to make $5K
- Each client expects a lot because they're comparing you to courses

**At $5,000:**
- Buyer thinks: "This person is serious. This is premium."
- You compete on expertise, not price
- You need 1 client to make $5K
- The client is invested and does the work

**The unlock:** A clear offer at $5K is easier to sell than a vague offer at $500 — because the buyer knows exactly what they're getting. If you did the extraction work in the Client Ready Offer, you already have this clarity.

### The Pricing Confidence Framework

Answer these three questions. If you can answer all three clearly, you're ready to charge $5K:

1. **What specific outcome do they walk away with?** (Not "clarity" or "confidence" — what tangible thing?)
2. **What's the timeline?** (When do they get the result?)
3. **What would this outcome be worth to them over the next 12 months?** (If the answer is $50K+, then $5K is a bargain.)

**The rule:** Your price should be 10-20% of the value you create. A $5K offer should create at least $25-50K in value for the client. If it doesn't, you need a better offer — not a lower price.

## Part 2: 5 Conversation Frameworks

These aren't scripts. They're structures. Use your own words.

### Framework 1: The Discovery Conversation

**When to use:** Someone expresses interest but hasn't seen your offer yet.

**Structure:**
1. **Ask about their situation.** "What are you working on right now?" (Let them talk for 2-3 minutes.)
2. **Identify the gap.** "What's the biggest thing standing between you and [their goal]?"
3. **Share your relevant experience.** "I worked with someone in a similar situation. Here's what we did..."
4. **Name the offer.** "I have something that might be exactly what you need. Want me to send you the details?"
5. **Send the offer document.** Don't explain it on the call. Let the document do the work.

**Key:** You're not selling. You're diagnosing. If your offer isn't a fit, say so. That builds more trust than any pitch.

---

### Framework 2: The Offer Walkthrough

**When to use:** They've read your offer document and want to talk.

**Structure:**
1. **Ask what stood out.** "What grabbed you about it?"
2. **Ask what's unclear.** "Any questions about how it works?"
3. **Answer directly.** No hedging. No "it depends." Clear answers.
4. **Ask the decision question.** "Does this feel like what you need right now?"
5. **If yes:** "Here's how to get started." (Send payment link.)
6. **If not yet:** "No problem. What would need to be true for this to be the right time?"

**Key:** Don't re-explain the offer. The document already did that. This conversation is about fit, not features.

---

### Framework 3: The DM Close

**When to use:** You're selling through messages, not calls.

**Structure:**
1. **Value first.** Share something useful related to their problem.
2. **Connect it to your offer.** "This is actually one of the things I cover in [offer name]."
3. **Soft open.** "Would it help if I sent you the details?"
4. **Send offer document.** Let them read it.
5. **Follow up once.** "Any questions after reading through it?"
6. **Direct close.** "Ready to get started? Here's the link."

**Key:** Two messages max after sending the offer. If they don't respond, they're not ready. Move on.

---

### Framework 4: The "I'm Building Something" Close

**When to use:** You're still early and don't have a lot of proof yet.

**Structure:**
1. **Be honest about where you are.** "I'm launching something new and I'm looking for 3-5 founding clients."
2. **Explain the benefit of being early.** "Founding clients get [more access / lower price / direct input]."
3. **Share the offer.** "Here's what I'm building. Would you want to be one of the first?"
4. **Create urgency through scarcity.** "I'm only taking 5 at this price. After that it goes to full rate."

**Key:** Honesty is your superpower when you're early. "I'm building this and want to get it right" is more compelling than pretending you've done it 100 times.

---

### Framework 5: The Referral Ask

**When to use:** After delivering a result for a client, or in any conversation where someone says "this is great."

**Structure:**
1. **Thank them.** Be genuine.
2. **Ask directly.** "Do you know anyone else who's dealing with [specific problem]?"
3. **Make it easy.** "If someone comes to mind, just introduce us over DM. No pressure."
4. **Follow up once.** "Did you think of anyone?"

**Key:** People forget. One follow-up turns a "yeah, let me think" into an actual introduction.

## Part 3: The Warm 50 Activation Plan

You don't need an audience. You need 50 people.

### Who's in your Warm 50?

Go through these categories and list names:

**Category 1: Past colleagues (10-15 people)**
People who've worked with you and know your expertise firsthand.

**Category 2: Industry connections (10-15 people)**
People in your field who could be clients OR could refer clients.

**Category 3: Friends who "get it" (5-10 people)**
People who understand what you do and support your work.

**Category 4: Social media connections (10-15 people)**
People who've engaged with your content, commented, DMed you.

**Category 5: People who've asked for help (5-10 people)**
Anyone who's ever said "can you help me with...?" or "how did you do that?"

### The Activation Sequence

Don't blast all 50 at once. Work through them in waves:

**Week 1 (10 people):** Use Framework 1 (Discovery) or Framework 4 ("I'm Building Something"). Start with the people you're most comfortable with.

**Week 2 (15 people):** Add Framework 3 (DM Close) for social media connections. Send your offer document to anyone who expressed interest in Week 1.

**Week 3 (15 people):** Expand to industry connections. Use Framework 5 (Referral) with anyone who was interested but not a fit.

**Week 4 (10 people):** Clean up. Follow up with everyone who said "let me think about it." Use Framework 2 (Offer Walkthrough) for anyone who read the doc but didn't act.

### Expected Results

From 50 warm conversations:
- **5-10 will say "tell me more"** (10-20% interest rate is normal)
- **2-3 will seriously consider** (conversion from interest to consideration)
- **1-2 will buy** (your first clients)

One $5K client from 50 conversations = $100 per conversation. That's the math.

## Part 4: The Objection Playbook

Every objection is a question in disguise. Here's what they're really asking and how to respond.

### "It's too expensive"

**What they mean:** "I don't see how the value justifies the price."

**Response:** "I get it. Let me ask you this — what would solving [their specific problem] be worth to you over the next 12 months? If the answer is more than $5K, then this is actually the cheapest option."

**If they push back:** "No pressure. If the timing isn't right, it isn't right. But the offer stands if things change."

---

### "I need to think about it"

**What they mean:** "I'm interested but something isn't clear, or I'm not confident enough to decide."

**Response:** "Totally fair. What specific thing do you need to think through? Maybe I can help clarify."

**If they can't name it:** "Sometimes 'I need to think about it' means 'I'm not sure this is for me.' If that's the case, just tell me straight — I'd rather know now than waste your time."

---

### "What exactly do I get?"

**What they mean:** "Your offer document wasn't clear enough, or I didn't read it carefully."

**Response:** Walk them through the deliverables briefly. Then: "But honestly, the deliverables aren't the point. The point is [the outcome]. Everything I listed is how we get you there."

---

### "I've tried coaching before and it didn't work"

**What they mean:** "I'm protecting myself from another bad investment."

**Response:** "What did you try? And what specifically didn't work about it?" (Listen.) "Here's how this is different: [one specific thing about your approach that addresses their past failure]."

---

### "Can I see some results / testimonials?"

**What they mean:** "I need proof before I trust you."

**Response (if you have proof):** Share it. Keep it specific and relevant to their situation.

**Response (if you're early):** "I'm building my case studies right now — that's why I'm offering founding client pricing. You'd be one of the first, which means more direct access to me and a lower price. The tradeoff is I don't have 50 testimonials yet."

### Conversation 2: The Objection Handler

**Context:** Business coach prospect. Referred by a friend. Hesitant about price.

> **Prospect:** "This sounds great but $5K is a lot. I'm not making much right now."
>
> *[NOTE: Don't discount. Don't get defensive. Ask a question.]*
>
> **Michael:** "I hear you. Let me ask — if we got your offer right and you landed even 2 clients at your target price, what would that be worth?"
>
> **Prospect:** "My target is $3K per client. So... $6K."
>
> **Michael:** "So you'd make back the investment plus $1K from just 2 clients. And that's recurring — once the offer is right, it keeps working. Does that math work for you?"
>
> **Prospect:** "When you put it that way... yeah. Let's do it."

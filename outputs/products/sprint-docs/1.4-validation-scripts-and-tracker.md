# Validation Conversation Scripts and Tracker

**Sprint Module 1.4 — Estimated time: 30 minutes prep + 5 conversations over the week**

---

## How to Use This

Five conversations will tell you more than five weeks of overthinking.

You're not selling. You're testing. The goal is honest feedback from real people — not validation of what you want to hear.

**Minimum:** 5 conversations. More is better. The patterns emerge around conversation 3-4.

---

## The Reach-Out Scripts

Three ways to start the conversation. Pick the one that fits your relationship with the person.

### Script 1: The Direct Ask

Best for: Close friends, family members, people who already know what you're working on.

> "Hey [name] — I'm building something and I need honest feedback from someone I trust. Can I get 15 minutes of your time this week to run something by you? No selling, I promise — I just need a real opinion."

### Script 2: The "I'm Building Something" Ask

Best for: Colleagues, acquaintances, people in your industry.

> "Hey [name] — I'm working on something for [type of person] who struggle with [problem]. You came to mind because [reason they're relevant]. Would you be open to a quick chat? I'm in research mode and your perspective would be really helpful."

### Script 3: The Casual Mention

Best for: People you run into naturally — at work, events, online communities.

> "I've been thinking a lot about [problem]. I'm putting together [offer description in plain language]. What's your take — does that sound like something [people like you/them] would actually use?"

---

## The 5-Question Framework

Once you're in the conversation, ask these five questions. In this order.

**Question 1: "What's the biggest challenge you're facing with [topic]?"**

Why: You want to hear the problem in THEIR words, not yours. Listen for emotion, not just facts.

**Question 2: "What have you tried so far?"**

Why: This tells you what solutions already exist and where they failed. If they haven't tried anything, the problem might not be painful enough.

**Question 3: "What would it look like if this was solved?"**

Why: This is the transformation. If they can't articulate it, the problem might be too vague. If they get specific and excited, you're onto something.

**Question 4: "If something existed that [your offer promise], would you be interested?"**

Why: Direct interest check. Watch their reaction, not just their words. Polite "sure" is different from leaning in and asking "how would that work?"

**Question 5: "What would make you hesitate?"**

Why: This surfaces objections. Price, time, trust, "I've been burned before." Every objection is useful data.

---

## How to Interpret Responses

**Strong signal (move forward):**
- They describe the problem with emotion or frustration
- They've already tried to solve it (proof the pain is real)
- They lean in at Question 4 and ask follow-up questions
- They say some version of "when can I buy this?"

**Moderate signal (refine and keep testing):**
- They recognize the problem but aren't urgent about it
- They say "that sounds cool" but don't ask follow-up questions
- Their hesitations are about logistics (price, timing) not about whether they need it

**Weak signal (rethink):**
- They can't describe the problem clearly
- They haven't tried to solve it (not painful enough)
- Polite interest with no energy behind it
- Their version of the problem doesn't match yours

**Silence (data, not failure):**
- If they ghost your reach-out, they're not your audience
- If they take the call but seem uninterested, the problem isn't painful enough for them
- 3+ people ghosting = wrong audience, not wrong offer

---

## The Follow-Up

After the conversation, send a quick thank-you:

> "Thanks for the honest feedback — really helpful. I'll keep you posted on what I build. And if you think of anyone else who deals with [problem], I'd love to talk to them too."

This does two things: shows gratitude and opens a referral door.

## The "Validated" Checklist

After your conversations, check what applies:

- [ ] At least 3 out of 5 people expressed genuine interest (level 4-5)
- [ ] You heard the same pain point repeated across multiple conversations
- [ ] At least one person asked "when can I buy this?" or "how do I sign up?"
- [ ] The objections were about logistics (price, timing) not about whether they need it
- [ ] You can now describe the problem better than you could before the conversations

**If you checked 4-5:** Your offer is validated. Move to Week 2 and build.

**If you checked 2-3:** Close but not there. Refine and do 5 more conversations.

**If you checked 0-1:** Something needs to change. See below.

---

## What to Change If Validation Fails

Don't panic. Failed validation is data, not a dead end.

**Change the problem.** Maybe you're solving the wrong pain point. Go back to your validation conversations — what problem did THEY bring up? Use their words, not yours.

**Change the audience.** The problem might be real but you're talking to the wrong people. Who else has this problem? Who has it worse?

**Change the delivery.** The problem and audience might be right, but how you're solving it doesn't resonate. Try a different format, timeline, or price point.

**The test:** After adjusting, do 5 more conversations. If the signals improve, keep going. If they don't, go back to the Zone of Genius Worksheet and look for a different intersection.

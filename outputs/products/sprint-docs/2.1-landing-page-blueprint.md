## How to Use This

Print this out (or keep it open on a second screen) and work through it section by section. Each section has a purpose, copy prompts, and an example. Your landing page is just these 7 sections in order.

---

## The 7-Section Framework

### Section 1: Headline + Subheadline

**Purpose:** Stop the scroll. State the promise. The headline is the most important line on the page — if it doesn't work, nothing else matters.

**Headline formula:** [Outcome] + [Timeframe] + [Without Pain Point]

**Example:** "Validate Your High-Ticket Offer Before You Build Anything — In One Afternoon"

**Alternative formulas:**
- "How to [result] in [timeframe] without [pain point]"
- "[Who]: Stop [frustrating activity]. Start [desired activity]."
- "The [number]-step system for [result] — even if [common objection]"

**Subheadline:** One sentence that expands on the headline. Adds specificity or addresses the "how."

**Example:** "5 AI prompts that extract your zone of genius, build your offer document, and tell you if it's worth building — before you spend a dollar on a funnel."

### Section 2: The Problem

**Purpose:** Make them feel understood. Describe their situation so accurately they think you're reading their mind.

**Copy prompt:** "You've been [doing what] for [how long]. You've tried [what]. Nothing's worked because [real reason]. And the worst part? [The emotional cost]."

**Example:**
"You've been trying to turn your expertise into a coaching business for over a year. You've watched the YouTube videos. Maybe bought a course. You've started building a website, wrote some social media posts, even had a few conversations that went nowhere. And the worst part isn't that it hasn't worked — it's that you're starting to wonder if the problem is you."

**Length guide:** 3-5 short paragraphs. Lead with their situation, then twist the knife on the emotional pain.

### Section 3: The Solution

**Purpose:** Bridge from problem to your mechanism. This is where you introduce what you offer — but focus on the approach, not the features.

**Copy prompt:** "What if you could [result] by [mechanism] in [timeframe]? Not by [common approach they've tried], but by [your different approach]."

**Example:**
"What if you could validate a high-ticket offer in one afternoon — before spending a dollar on a funnel? Not by guessing what people want, but by using a proven extraction process that pulls the offer out of what you already know."

**Length guide:** 2-3 paragraphs. Keep it about THEM, not you.

### Section 4: Social Proof

**Purpose:** Build trust. Show that this works for real people.

**If you have testimonials:**
- Use 2-3 short quotes (1-2 sentences each)
- Include the person's name and context (role, situation)
- Specific results beat vague praise

**If you don't have testimonials yet:**
- Use your own credentials and experience
- Reference your validation conversations ("When I tested this with [type of person], they said...")
- Use a mini case study from your Experience Inventory

**Copy prompt for credentials:** "[Number] years of [expertise]. [Number] people helped with [problem]. Here's what I've learned: [key insight]."

### Section 7: Final CTA

**Purpose:** One more push. Restate the promise and give them a clear action.

**Copy prompt:** "You've read this far because [reason]. [Restate the problem one more time]. [Restate the promise]. [Tell them exactly what to do]."

**Example:**
"You've read this far because you know your expertise is worth more than your 9-to-5 pays you. The question isn't whether you can do this — it's whether you'll keep waiting or start today. Get instant access now. Your offer validated in one afternoon."

**Button text:** Use action words. "Get Instant Access" beats "Submit." "Start Now" beats "Buy."

## Pre-Launch Checklist

Before your page goes live, verify:

- [ ] Headline is clear and specific (not clever or vague)
- [ ] Problem section uses their words, not yours
- [ ] At least one form of social proof
- [ ] Every component in the offer stack has a "what it does for you" line
- [ ] Guarantee is stated clearly
- [ ] Price is visible without scrolling past the offer stack
- [ ] CTA button text is action-oriented
- [ ] FAQ addresses the top 5 objections
- [ ] Page loads on mobile
- [ ] One single action (no competing links or navigation)

## Pre-Launch Checklist

Go through every item before you publish your campaign. One missed step can waste your entire budget.

- [ ] 1. Meta Business Suite account set up
- [ ] 2. Ad account created and payment method added
- [ ] 3. Meta pixel created in Events Manager
- [ ] 4. Pixel installed on landing page (verify: visit page, check Events Manager for "PageView")
- [ ] 5. Pixel installed on thank you page (verify: same method)
- [ ] 6. Purchase event configured (fires when someone completes checkout)
- [ ] 7. Test purchase completed (Stripe test mode — full flow from ad click to thank you page)
- [ ] 8. Landing page loads correctly on mobile
- [ ] 9. Landing page loads correctly on desktop
- [ ] 10. Checkout page working (price correct, bumps displaying)
- [ ] 11. Email automation triggers on purchase
- [ ] 12. Bump offers displaying correctly on checkout
- [ ] 13. Thank you page shows after purchase
- [ ] 14. Ad copy written (3 variations from Module 3.2)
- [ ] 15. Ad creative ready (images or video from Module 3.3)

**All 15 checked?** You're ready to publish.

## First 48 Hours: What to Expect

### The Learning Phase

When you publish a new campaign, Meta enters a "learning phase." This means:

- Meta is testing who to show your ads to
- Performance will be inconsistent (some hours great, some terrible)
- This is normal. Don't panic.
- The learning phase typically lasts 3-7 days or until you get ~50 conversions

### The Rules for the First 48 Hours

**Do NOT:**
- Turn off ads
- Change the budget
- Edit the ad copy or creative
- Panic about numbers

**DO:**
- Check once in the morning, once at night
- Note the numbers in your tracker (below)
- Watch for: are impressions being delivered? Are people clicking?

---

## The 3 Metrics That Matter at Launch

| Metric | What It Means | Healthy Range | Where to Find It |
|--------|-------------|---------------|------------------|
| CTR (click-through rate) | Are people clicking your ad? | Above 1% | Ads Manager → Campaigns |
| CPC (cost per click) | How much does each click cost? | Under 3 for coaching | Ads Manager → Campaigns |
| LP Conversion Rate | Are clicks turning into buyers? | 2-5% for cold traffic | GHL or Stripe (sales / page views) |

**After 48 hours, you care about:**

| Metric | What It Means | Target |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| CPA (cost per acquisition) | How much per sale? | Under 100 |
| AOV (average order value) | Revenue per buyer? | 100+ (with bumps) |
| ROAS (return on ad spend) | Revenue / spend? | Above 1.0 to break even |

## Benchmark Ranges

Use these to judge your numbers. Don't compare to day 1 — compare to day 7+.

| Metric | Below Average | Average | Good | Great |
|--------|-------------|---------|------|-------|
| CTR | Under 0.5% | 0.5-1% | 1-2% | Above 2% |
| CPC | Above 5 | 3-5 | 1-3 | Under 1 |
| LP Conversion | Under 1% | 1-2% | 2-5% | Above 5% |
| CPA | Above 150 | 100-150 | 50-100 | Under 50 |
| AOV | Under 40 | 40-80 | 80-120 | Above 120 |

## "When to Worry" Decision Guide

**After 48 hours:**
- Check CTR. Below 0.5% across all ads? Your hooks aren't working. Write new hooks (don't change the rest of the ad — just the first line).

**After 100 clicks, 0 sales:**
- Your landing page has a problem. Review: Is the headline clear? Is there social proof? Is the offer stack compelling? Is the CTA obvious?

**After spending 300 with no sales:**
- The 300 Rule: Change the offer, not the ad. If 300 worth of clicks can't produce a sale, the landing page or the offer itself needs work.

**After 7 days of profitable data:**
- Scale: increase budget by 20%. Wait 48 hours. If still profitable, increase another 20%. Never more than 20% at a time.

**One ad performing, two aren't:**
- Kill the losers. Move their budget to the winner. This is normal — you're not failing, you're learning.

# Optimization Decision Framework

**Sprint Module 4.2 — Estimated time: 30-45 minutes**

---

## How to Use This

Use this after you have at least 7 days of data and 100+ total clicks. Before that, you don't have enough information to make good decisions.

---

## The Kill / Keep / Scale Framework

Three options for every ad:

**Kill** = Turn it off. It's not working and more money won't change that.

**Keep** = Leave it running. Either not enough data yet, or it's borderline and worth monitoring.

**Scale** = Increase budget. It's working. Give it more fuel.

---

## Minimum Data Thresholds

Don't decide too early. Bad data leads to bad decisions.

| Decision | Minimum Data Needed |
|----------|-------------------|
| Kill an ad | 500+ impressions with 0 clicks = definitely kill. OR: Spent 2x your target CPA with 0 sales. |
| Keep an ad | Less than 500 impressions, or less than 100 clicks. Not enough data yet. Wait. |
| Scale an ad | 3+ sales at a CPA below your target. Consistent performance over 3+ days (not just one lucky day). |

---

## The Decision Flowchart

For each ad, work through this:

```
START: Look at this ad's numbers
|
Has it spent 2x your target CPA with 0 sales?
|
+-- YES --> KILL IT (it's had enough budget to prove itself and didn't)
|
+-- NO
    |
    Does it have 3+ sales?
    |
    +-- NO --> KEEP IT (not enough data to decide — let it run)
    |
    +-- YES
        |
        Is the CPA below your target?
        |
        +-- NO --> KEEP IT (it's converting but expensive — monitor for 3 more days)
        |
        +-- YES --> SCALE IT (increase budget 20%, wait 48 hours, reassess)
```

---

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: Kill

**Ad A** spent 160 with 0 sales. Target CPA is 80.
- 160 = 2x the target CPA
- Zero sales
- **Decision: KILL.** It had its chance.

### Example 2: Keep

**Ad B** spent 60 with 1 sale. Target CPA is 80.
- Only 1 sale — not enough data for a pattern
- CPA is 60, which is below target — promising
- **Decision: KEEP.** Let it run another week. If it gets to 3+ sales at similar CPA, scale it.

### Example 3: Scale

**Ad C** spent 200 with 4 sales. Target CPA is 80.
- 4 sales = pattern, not luck
- CPA is 50 (200 / 4) — well below target
- Consistent over 5 days
- **Decision: SCALE.** Increase budget 20%, wait 48 hours, check again.

### Example 4: Keep (borderline)

**Ad D** spent 120 with 2 sales. Target CPA is 80.
- CPA is 60 — looks good
- But only 2 sales — could be luck
- **Decision: KEEP.** Need one more week of data. If it holds, scale.

## Budget Reallocation Rules

When you kill a loser, move its budget to a winner:

1. **Kill a loser** — take its daily budget
2. **Add to the winner** — increase the winning ad's budget by 20% (not more)
3. **Wait 48 hours** — let Meta readjust
4. **Reassess** — still profitable? Increase another 20%

**Never increase budget by more than 20% at a time.** Big jumps reset Meta's learning phase and can tank performance.

---

## The One Change Rule

When something isn't working, change ONE thing at a time.

If you change the ad hook AND the landing page headline AND the price all at once — and results improve — you won't know which change worked. You'll have no idea what to do next time.

**Priority order for changes:**

| Priority | What to Change | When |
|----------|---------------|------|
| 1 | Offer or price | 300+ spent, 0 sales (the 300 Rule) |
| 2 | Landing page headline | Clicks but no conversions |
| 3 | Ad hook (first line) | Low CTR (under 1%) |
| 4 | Ad creative (image/video) | CTR plateaus after testing hooks |
| 5 | Targeting | High CPC with good creative |

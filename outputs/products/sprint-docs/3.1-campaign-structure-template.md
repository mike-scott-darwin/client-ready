# Campaign Structure Template + Budget Calculator

**Sprint Module 3.1 — Estimated time: 1-2 hours to set up**

---

## How to Use This

This template shows you exactly how to structure your first Meta ad campaign. One campaign. One ad set. Three ads. That's it. Don't overcomplicate this.

---

## Campaign Structure

**CAMPAIGN (CBO — Purchase objective)**

**Ad Set (Broad targeting)**

- **Ad 1:** Problem Agitation angle
- **Ad 2:** Transformation angle
- **Ad 3:** Identity Callout angle

### What Each Level Does

**Campaign** = The budget container. You set your daily budget here and tell Meta what you're optimizing for (purchases). Meta distributes your budget across the ads automatically.

**Ad Set** = Who sees your ads. This is where you set targeting (age, location, interests). Start broad — Meta's algorithm is smarter than manual targeting for most coaching offers.

**Ad** = The actual creative people see. Your copy, image/video, headline, and call-to-action button. You're running 3 ads so you have data to compare.

---

## Targeting Guide

### Start Broad (Recommended for First Campaign)

- **Age:** 25-55
- **Gender:** All (unless your offer is specifically gendered)
- **Location:** Your country
- **Detailed targeting:** Leave blank (broad)
- **Placements:** Automatic (let Meta decide)

### Alternative: Interest-Based (If Broad Doesn't Work After 7 Days)

Pick 1-2 interests related to your audience:

- **Coaches/consultants:** Business coaching, Life coaching, Tony Robbins, Entrepreneurship
- **Fitness professionals:** Personal training, Fitness coaching, NASM, ACE Fitness
- **Creative freelancers:** Freelancing, Upwork, Fiverr, Creative entrepreneurship
- **Your niche:**

_______________

**Why broad first:** Meta's algorithm learns faster with a bigger pool. Narrow targeting limits its options. Most coaching offers perform better broad.

## Budget Calculator

- **20/day:** 140/week, 280 total — Basic signal — are people clicking?
- **50/day:** 350/week, 700 total — Solid data — which ad works best
- **100/day:** 700/week, 1,400 total — Fast learning — enough for real decisions

**Recommendation for first-time advertisers:** Start at 20-30 per day. You can always increase once you see what works. Don't blow your budget in the first 3 days.

**The expectation:** Plan to spend at least 300-500 before making any big decisions. The learning phase is real — Meta needs data before it can optimize.

## Campaign Naming Convention

Name things so you can find them later:

**Campaign:** [Date]-[Offer]-[Type]
- Example: 0214-ClientReady-CBO

**Ad Set:** [Targeting]-[Date]
- Example: Broad-0214

**Ad:** [Angle]-[Creative Type]-[Number]
- Example: ProblemAgitation-Image-01

## Setup Checklist

Before you create the campaign, verify:

- [ ] Meta Business Suite account created
- [ ] Ad account set up inside Business Suite
- [ ] Payment method added to ad account
- [ ] Meta pixel created
- [ ] Pixel installed on your landing page
- [ ] Pixel installed on your thank you page (for tracking purchases)
- [ ] Custom conversion set up for "Purchase" event
- [ ] Test: visit your landing page and check Events Manager — does the pixel fire?
- [ ] Ad copy written for 3 ads (Module 3.2)
- [ ] Ad creative ready — images or video (Module 3.3)

## Creating the Campaign (Step by Step)

1. Go to **Ads Manager** (not "Boost Post" — never boost)
2. Click **Create**
3. Choose objective: **Sales** (or "Conversions" depending on your interface)
4. Campaign name: [your naming convention]
5. Turn on **Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO)**
6. Set daily budget
7. Create one ad set:
   - Name it
   - Set your targeting (broad recommended)
   - Placements: Automatic
   - Optimization: Purchase
8. Create 3 ads within that ad set:
   - Ad 1: Problem Agitation angle
   - Ad 2: Transformation angle
   - Ad 3: Identity Callout angle
9. For each ad: upload creative, paste copy, set headline, choose CTA button ("Learn More" for cold traffic)
10. Review and publish

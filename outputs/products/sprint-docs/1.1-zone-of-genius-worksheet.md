# Zone of Genius Extraction Worksheet

**Sprint Module 1.1 — Estimated time: 45-60 minutes**

---

## How to Use This

This worksheet walks you through finding your expertise intersection — where what you know, what energizes you, and who you've helped all overlap. That intersection is your zone of genius.

**Do this in one sitting.** Don't overthink it. Write fast, edit later.

**The rule:** If you catch yourself writing what sounds good instead of what's true, stop and rewrite it.

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: The HR Manager

**Career:** 12 years in HR, certified mediator, trained 200+ managers on conflict resolution.
**Energy:** Researches organizational psychology on weekends. Lights up when someone mentions a toxic boss.
**Experience:** Helped a startup founder handle a co-founder conflict. Coached three colleagues through managing underperformers.
**Intersection:** Conflict resolution + coaching through hard conversations + managers who avoid confrontation.
**ONE Problem:** Mid-level managers who avoid difficult conversations and let small issues become team-destroying conflicts.
**One sentence:** "I help managers who avoid confrontation have the hard conversations before small problems become crises."

### Example 2: The Fitness Trainer

**Career:** 8 years certified PT, nutrition cert, 300+ clients, specializes in strength training.
**Energy:** Reads studies on exercise efficiency. Gets fired up helping someone see that 30 minutes is enough.
**Experience:** Best results came with busy professionals. Helped a lawyer lose 25 pounds training 3x/week for 30 minutes.
**Intersection:** Efficient training + helping people who think they need more time + busy professionals.
**ONE Problem:** Busy professionals who believe getting fit requires hours they don't have, so they do nothing.
**One sentence:** "I help busy professionals get in the best shape of their life with 30 minutes, 3 times a week."

### Example 3: The Project Manager

**Career:** 10 years PM, PMP certified, managed teams of 5-50, built delivery frameworks for 3 companies.
**Energy:** Reads about productivity systems for fun. Loves building templates.
**Experience:** Helped a freelance designer double her capacity. Built a system for a small agency that cut overruns by 70%.
**Intersection:** Delivery systems + building workflows + freelancers drowning in chaos.
**ONE Problem:** Freelancers who spend more time managing projects than doing the work.
**One sentence:** "I help freelancers stop losing money on projects by installing a simple delivery system."

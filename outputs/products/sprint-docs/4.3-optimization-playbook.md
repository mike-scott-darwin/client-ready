# Optimization Playbook

**Sprint Module 4.3 — Estimated time: 1-2 hours**

---

## How to Use This

You've identified your bottleneck (Module 4.1) and made your kill/keep/scale decisions (Module 4.2). Now make the specific changes.

Work through the priority that matches your bottleneck. Don't try to optimize everything at once.

---

## Priority Matrix

- **1 — Ad hooks (first line):** High impact, Low effort — Do it when CTR is below 1%
- **2 — Landing page headline:** High impact, Low effort — Do it when you have clicks but no conversions
- **3 — Offer or price:** High impact, Medium effort — Do it when 300+ spent, 0 sales
- **4 — Bump descriptions:** Medium impact, Low effort — Do it when you have sales but low AOV
- **5 — Email subject lines:** Medium impact, Low effort — Do it when you have low open rates
- **6 — Ad creative:** Medium impact, Medium effort — Do it when CTR plateaus
- **7 — Landing page body copy:** Medium impact, High effort — Do it when conversion is below 1%
- **8 — Targeting:** Low impact, Low effort — Do it when CPC is high
- **9 — Checkout page:** Low impact, Medium effort — Do it when you see cart abandonment
- **10 — Email body copy:** Low impact, High effort — Do it when you have low click rates

**Rule:** Fix the bottleneck. Only the bottleneck. Don't redesign your page when you need better hooks. Don't write more emails when nobody's opening the first ones.

## Priority 1: Hook Optimization

**When:** CTR is below 1%

Your ad hook (first line) is the most important line in your entire funnel. If nobody clicks, nothing else matters.

**Write 3 new hooks using different formulas:**

**Question Hook:**
"[Pain point they experience daily]?"
- Example: "Still explaining what you do to everyone you meet?"
- Yours: _______________

**Statement Hook:**
"[Contrarian claim]. Here's why."
- Example: "Your content strategy is the reason you don't have clients. Here's why."
- Yours: _______________

**Story Hook:**
"[Time period] ago, I [made a mistake]. [Bad result]."
- Example: "6 months ago, I scrapped my entire funnel. Best decision I ever made."
- Yours: _______________

**Number Hook:**
"[Number] [things] that [result]. Number [X] is the one nobody talks about."
- Example: "5 reasons your coaching offer doesn't convert. Number 3 is the one nobody talks about."
- Yours: _______________

**Contrarian Hook:**
"Everyone says [common advice]. They're wrong."
- Example: "Everyone says you need more content. They're wrong."
- Yours: _______________

**How to test:** Create new ads with the same body copy and creative, but different hooks. Run them alongside your current ads.

---

## Priority 2: Headline Optimization

**When:** People click but don't buy (LP conversion below 2%)

Your landing page headline is the first thing visitors see. If it doesn't match what the ad promised, they bounce.

**Write 3 new headlines:**

**Outcome + Timeframe:**
"[Result] in [timeframe]"
- Example: "A validated offer in one afternoon"
- Yours: _______________

**Pain Point + Solution:**
"Stop [frustrating activity]. Start [desired result]."
- Example: "Stop guessing what to sell. Start validating what works."
- Yours: _______________

**"How to" + Specific Result:**
"How to [specific result] — even if [common objection]"
- Example: "How to land your first high-ticket client — even if you have no audience"
- Yours: _______________

**How to test:** Duplicate your landing page. Change ONLY the headline. Split your traffic 50/50 between the two pages. Run for 7 days minimum.

---

## Priority 3: Offer / Price Adjustment

**When:** 300+ spent with 0 sales (the 300 Rule)

If your hooks work (people click) and your page is decent (people read it) but nobody buys — the problem is usually the offer itself.

**Three questions to diagnose:**

1. **Is the problem specific enough?** "I help people with business" won't sell. "I help freelance designers stop losing money on projects" might.

2. **Is the promise clear?** Can someone read your headline and know exactly what they'll get? If not, simplify.

3. **Is the price wrong?** Too low can actually hurt — it signals low value. Too high creates friction. Test moving the price UP before moving it down.

**The 3 offer fixes:**
- Narrow the problem (more specific = more compelling)
- Strengthen the guarantee (reduce their risk)
- Adjust the price (test 50% higher before testing lower)

## Priority 4: Bump Description Optimization

**When:** Sales are coming but AOV is below target

People are buying the main offer but skipping the bumps. The bump descriptions need work.

**The formula:** [Result] + [How] + [Timeframe] — under 50 words.

**Rewrite your bump descriptions:**

Bump 1 current description:
_______________

Bump 1 new version:
_______________

Bump 2 current description:
_______________

Bump 2 new version:
_______________

**Tips:**
- Lead with the result, not the feature
- Create urgency around the moment ("use this today")
- Connect it to the main product ("now that you have X, you need Y")

---

## Priority 5: Email Subject Line Optimization

**When:** Open rates below 20%

**10 subject line formulas to test:**

1. "Quick question about [topic]"
2. "I was wrong about [thing]"
3. "The [topic] shortcut"
4. "Don't do this (I did)"
5. "[Number] minutes to [result]"
6. "Real talk about [topic]"
7. "What nobody tells you about [topic]"
8. "This changed everything"
9. "[First name], quick thought"
10. "Read this before you [action]"

**How to test:** Send the same email with different subject lines on different days. Track open rates in GHL.

---

## What NOT to Optimize

- Don't redesign your landing page when you need more traffic
- Don't write more emails when nobody's opening the first ones
- Don't change targeting when your creative is the problem
- Don't optimize ads with less than 100 clicks of data
- Don't change multiple things at once

**Fix the bottleneck. Only the bottleneck.**

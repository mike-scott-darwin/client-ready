# UGC Creative Shot List

**Why UGC:** AI-generated images feel inauthentic for an anti-guru brand. Real photos, screen recordings, and product screenshots carry more trust — especially for cold traffic selling to coaches burned by polished marketing.

**Format:** All 9:16 vertical. Safe zone: 1:1 center square (for feed crop).

---

## Replacing AI Images with Real Content

Each slot maps to the original image ID from batches 003/004. Same pairing rules apply — just swap AI images for real ones.

---

## SLOT 1: Authority/Framework (003_01)
**Pairs with:** Before the Funnel P1 (Deep), P3 (Direct Response)

### Option A: Screen Recording Still
Screenshot of the AI prompt interface mid-conversation — the moment the offer gets extracted. Crop to show the "aha" output, not the full screen. Black out or blur anything sensitive.

### Option B: Product Screenshot
The actual Client Ready Offer Document — the deliverable. Show it on screen (laptop or phone). Not the whole thing — just enough to see it's real, structured, and professional.

### Option C: Whiteboard/Notebook
Hand-drawn "Confused → Clear → Converting" framework on a whiteboard or in a notebook. Real handwriting. Imperfect. Shows the thinking, not the polish.

**Shoot notes:** Natural light. No ring light. If using laptop screen, slight angle (not flat-on). Coffee mug or desk clutter in periphery is fine — it signals "real workspace."

---

## SLOT 2: Lo-fi/Authentic (003_02)
**Pairs with:** Before the Funnel P2 (UGC), P5 (Testimonial)

### Option A: Selfie at Desk
Michael at his actual workspace. Not posed — mid-thought, looking at screen, or looking at camera like you're FaceTiming a friend. Phone camera quality.

### Option B: Over-the-Shoulder Screen Shot
Someone (you) looking at a screen showing the offer system output. Shot from behind/side. Focus on the screen, person slightly blurred.

### Option C: Real Desk Overhead
Your actual desk from above. Laptop open, notebook with notes, coffee. The "this is where the work happens" shot. Messy is better than staged.

**Shoot notes:** iPhone camera. No editing beyond basic crop. Warm/natural tones. The point is this looks like it was taken in 5 seconds, not 5 minutes.

## SLOT 3: Pattern Interrupt (003_03)
**Pairs with:** Before the Funnel P4 (Pattern Interrupt)

### Option A: Bold Text Card (Canva)
Dark background (#1A1A1A). One line of text in white or amber (#D4933A). Example lines:
- "The offer is already in you."
- "Everyone sells funnels. Nobody asks the obvious question first."
- "What if the problem was never your marketing?"

### Option B: Contrast Split
Left side: screenshot of a cluttered social feed (blurred). Right side: clean offer document on white background. Visual "noise vs. clarity."

### Option C: Single Object
One clean printed page on a dark surface. Nothing else. The offer document as a physical object. Minimal, stark, pattern-breaking in a feed full of faces and graphics.

**Shoot notes:** For text cards, use Canva — 1080x1920, dark background, one bold line. For contrast splits, screenshot your own feed for the "noise" side. For single object, print your offer doc and photograph it on a dark table.

## SLOT 4: Authority/Framework (003_04)
**Pairs with:** Clarity Unlock P1 (Deep), P3 (Direct Response)

### Option A: Before/After Screenshot
Side-by-side of a messy "offer brainstorm" (sticky notes, crossed out ideas, confusion) next to the clean final offer document. Real artifacts, not staged.

### Option B: Testimonial Card (Canva)
Clean card with real quote from Renee, Starr, or Robin. Format:
- Quote in large text
- Name + context below
- Disclaimer: "worked directly with Michael through coaching"
- Brand colors (teal border or amber accent)

### Option C: System Walkthrough Still
Screenshot of the Client Ready system in action — could be the AI prompt flow, the offer template, or the validation checklist. Shows "this is a real system, not a PDF."

**Shoot notes:** Testimonial cards should be clean and professional — this is the one place polish is appropriate because it signals credibility. Use Canva templates.

## SLOT 5: Lo-fi/Authentic (003_05)
**Pairs with:** Clarity Unlock P2 (UGC), P5 (Testimonial)

### Option A: Phone Notification Mockup
Your phone showing a real DM or message (blur name if needed) that says something like "this is exactly what I needed" or "I finally know what I'm selling." Real phone, real notification.

### Option B: Video Call Screenshot
A still from a coaching call (with permission) showing the moment someone gets clarity. Zoom/Google Meet interface visible. Candid, not posed.

### Option C: Handwritten Note
A sticky note or index card with "My offer: [one sentence]" written by hand. The physical artifact of clarity. Simple, memorable, thumb-stopping because it's so different from everything else in the feed.

**Shoot notes:** Phone notification — take a screenshot of your actual phone, or recreate the notification in a messaging app. Blur personal info. The phone frame makes it feel real.

## SLOT 6: Pattern Interrupt (003_06)
**Pairs with:** Clarity Unlock P4 (Pattern Interrupt)

### Option A: Bold Text Card (Canva)
Same format as 003_03 but for Clarity Unlock angle. Example lines:
- "You don't need more followers. You need a clearer offer."
- "Clarity isn't a feeling. It's a document."
- "One afternoon. One offer. One system that sells itself."

### Option B: Stark Contrast Photo
A single light in a dark room. Or a single clear window in a wall. Visual metaphor for "clarity" without being cheesy. Can be a stock photo or something you shoot.

### Option C: Counter-Intuitive Stat Card (Canva)
Card showing a surprising data point. Example:
- "Average time to extract a validated offer: 90 minutes"
- "5 AI prompts. One clear offer document."
Clean design, teal/amber brand colors.

**Shoot notes:** Text cards and stat cards are the easiest pattern interrupts to produce. Make 3-5 variations and test.

## SLOT 7: Authority/Framework (004_01)
**Pairs with:** Content Merry-Go-Round P1 (Deep), P3 (Direct Response)

### Option A: Screen Time Screenshot
Your actual iPhone screen time report showing social media hours. Or a screenshot of a social analytics dashboard showing effort vs. results. Real data, real frustration.

### Option B: Content Calendar Chaos
Screenshot of a packed content calendar (Google Calendar, Notion, or similar) showing the grind of posting daily. The visual argument for "there's a better way."

### Option C: Stripe/Analytics Screenshot
A dashboard showing results (blur actual numbers if needed) — the "what happens when you stop posting and start running ads" visual. Shows the system working.

**Shoot notes:** Screen time screenshots are powerful because everyone has them and everyone feels guilty about them. The shame is the scroll-stop.

## SLOT 8: Lo-fi/Authentic (004_02)
**Pairs with:** Content Merry-Go-Round P2 (UGC), P5 (Testimonial)

### Option A: Exhausted at Desk
You looking tired/frustrated at a laptop. The "I've been posting for 3 hours and got 12 likes" energy. Can be slightly dramatized but should feel real.

### Option B: Instagram Grid Screenshot
Screenshot of an Instagram profile with consistent posting but low engagement. Could be a generic example or your own. Shows the "doing everything right but nothing's working" feeling.

### Option C: Sticky Note Stack
A stack of sticky notes with content ideas, each crossed out or marked "posted — 3 likes." The physical evidence of the content treadmill.

**Shoot notes:** This is the "before" energy. Don't try to look good. Look how your audience feels right now — overwhelmed, underappreciated, grinding.

## SLOT 9: Pattern Interrupt (004_03)
**Pairs with:** Content Merry-Go-Round P4 (Pattern Interrupt)

### Option A: Bold Text Card (Canva)
Content Merry-Go-Round angle. Example lines:
- "Posting 3x a day isn't a strategy. It's a coping mechanism."
- "The algorithm doesn't owe you customers. Your offer does."
- "Stop creating content. Start creating clarity."

### Option B: Side-by-Side Hours
Simple graphic: "Hours spent on content this week: 14. Clients from content: 0." vs. "Hours spent on offer clarity: 2. Clients from ads: 3." Clean, stark comparison.

### Option C: Meme-Adjacent
A "this vs. that" format using real screenshots. Left: your social analytics (low engagement). Right: your ad results or offer doc. Not a meme — but uses the format people recognize.

**Shoot notes:** The Content Merry-Go-Round angle is Miles Stutz's #1 performing category (~35% of his ads). Your version is more grounded — no income claims, just the contrast between grinding on content and having a clear offer.

## Priority Order (What to Shoot First)

1. **Text cards in Canva** (003_03, 003_06, 004_03) — 15 minutes, zero equipment needed, test immediately
2. **Selfie at desk** (003_02) — 2 minutes with iPhone
3. **Product screenshot** (003_01) — screenshot your offer doc or AI prompt interface
4. **Testimonial cards in Canva** (003_04) — 20 minutes, use existing testimonials from Renee/Starr/Robin
5. **Phone notification mockup** (003_05) — screenshot a real DM or recreate one
6. **Screen recording stills** (004_01, 004_02) — screenshot your analytics/screen time
7. **Over-the-shoulder/desk shots** (remaining slots) — 5 minutes with iPhone

**Total time estimate for minimum viable creative set:** ~1 hour

## Ultra-Short Video (4-8 seconds)

The 5 USV scripts from batch 003 work best as:
- **Selfie video:** Look at camera, say the line, stop. No intro, no outro.
- **Text animation:** Line appears word-by-word over dark background (Canva animate)
- **Screen recording:** Show the AI extracting an offer while the line appears as text overlay

These replace the need for any AI-generated animated clips.

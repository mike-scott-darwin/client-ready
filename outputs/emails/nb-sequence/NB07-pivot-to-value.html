Just something I've noticed working with coaches:

The ones who struggle with offers usually have the same root problem:

They describe their work in terms of PROCESS, not OUTCOME.

"I help you get clarity on your values."

"I guide you through a transformational journey."

"I support you in stepping into your power."

Process. Process. Process.

Here's what buyers actually want:

"I help you land 3 clients in 30 days."

"I help you lose 20 pounds without giving up carbs."

"I help you have hard conversations without blowing up your relationships."

Outcome. Outcome. Outcome.

Specific. Measurable. Desirable.

If your offer describes a process, flip it. What happens AFTER the process? What result does someone walk away with?

That's your offer.

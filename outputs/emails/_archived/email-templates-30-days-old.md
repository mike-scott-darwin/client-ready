## Key Templates (Detailed)

### 1. The Soft Teach Email
**Use:** Demonstrate expertise while leaving them wanting more. Show WHAT to do, not HOW.

**Structure:**
1. Tell them what you're about to teach
2. Tell them why it's important
3. Tell a story that illustrates the technique
4. Recap the takeaway lesson
5. Share tweaks for their situation
6. Say there's much more to the story
7. Direct them to your product

**Subject Line Formula:**
> How could the story of [X] help you [desired goal]?

---

### 2. The Classic Evergreen Email
**Use:** Simple, flexible daily email. Works for anything.

**Structure:**
1. Hook into message (curiosity/attention)
2. Tell a short story or insight
3. Transition: "Here's what I've got... Here's what it does for you... Here's how to get it"
4. Restate the lesson
5. PS drives back to meat or restates offer

**Subject Line Formula:**
> Did you know that [fill in the blank]?

---

### 3. The Future You Email
**Use:** Future pacing + identity switching. Powerful for transformation offers.

**Structure:**
1. Show how you "switched identities"
2. Tell them their life is about to change
3. Give a glimpse into YOUR future because of what you discovered
4. Say it was all the result of a DECISION
5. Tell them that decision is waiting for them
6. Give them a way to act
7. Acknowledge fears/doubts
8. PS re-emphasizes the point

**Subject Line:**
> Personal Note from [subscriber's first name]

---

### 4. The Wrong, Wrong, Wrong Email
**Use:** Show a common mistake they think is good advice. Agitation + solution.

**Structure:**
1. Start with popular behavior people think is right
2. Point out it's wrong
3. Explain WHY it's wrong using your mechanism
4. Describe what happens when done right
5. Link to solution
6. Soft teaching to prove credibility
7. Show proof
8. Final CTA

**Subject Lines:**
- Is it good or bad to [X]?
- This common advice is killing your [X]

---

### 5. The Best Intentions Email
**Use:** They've tried but aren't getting results. Show what's missing.

**Structure:**
1. Paint picture of where they are in their journey
2. They've made progress but it's not working
3. Show the frustration: "Where did I go wrong?"
4. Point out this is common
5. Good news: there's another way
6. Send to solution
7. Reinforce why they should watch/buy
8. Social proof
9. Final CTA

**Subject Line:**
> Picture this with me...

---

### 6. The FAQ Email
**Use:** Overcome objections through Q&A format. Great for showing ease/speed.

**Structure:**
1. Say you've been receiving questions (be specific about HOW)
2. Tell them why the questions matter
3. Relate to your product
4. Present questions and answer them
5. Each answer leads back to offer

**Subject Line:**
> FAQ about [topic]

---

### 7. The Current Events Email
**Use:** Newsjacking. Tie current events to your product.

**Finding hooks:**
- Google News + your keywords
- "On this date" / "anniversary" searches
- Sports, pop culture, anything relevant

**Subject Lines:**
- Can you remember when you first heard THIS?
- What [celebrity/event] taught me about [topic]

---

### 8. The Emotional Story Email
**Use:** Deep personal story. Not for direct sale - builds relationship.

**Goals:**
- Reveal who you are as a person
- Explain your passion
- Bond reader to your mission
- Deepen relationship
- Show what makes you tick

**Note:** Can combine with soft CTA, but be careful not to seem manipulative.

---

### 9. The Everyone Says Email
**Use:** Debunk conventional wisdom. Position yourself as the smarter solution.

**Structure:**
1. Quote the common misguided advice
2. Render verdict: it's wrong
3. Show reasonableness - kernel of truth exists
4. Explain why it's still wrong and how your solution fixes it
5. Show proof
6. CTA

**Subject Lines:**
- Are these [X] really dangerous?
- Everyone says this. But is it true?
- I'm calling B.S. on this right NOW...

---

### 10. The You Can't Go It Alone Email
**Use:** Show they need help. Free resources aren't enough.

**Structure:**
1. State first rule: be willing to invest in yourself
2. Share your struggle with investing in yourself
3. Acknowledge free resources exist
4. Point out the hidden cost: TIME
5. Mention overwhelm from bad info
6. Position your reputation and results
7. Tell them they deserve to start now
8. CTA

**Subject Line:**
> I struggled with this too

---

### 11. The Irrefutable Analogy Email
**Use:** Make complex concepts click through perfect analogy.

**Tips:**
- Analogies make ideas come alive
- Find ones that apply to your mechanisms
- Visual analogies work especially well

**Examples:**
- Owl in knothole = natural fit for story-based selling
- Escape velocity = sustainable results vs willpower

## Subject Line Formulas

### Curiosity
- Did you know that [X]?
- How could [story] help you [goal]?
- Have you ever wondered why [X]?

### Provocation
- Wrong, wrong, wrong
- I'm calling B.S. on this
- Everyone says this. But is it true?

### Personal
- I struggled with this too
- Personal note from [name]
- A story like no other (because it's mine)

### Urgency
- [X] spots left
- Read this ASAP
- Don't be left behind

### Direct Benefit
- How to [big promise]
- What to do if [situation]
- The [X] mistake killing your [Y]

---

## Email Structure Rules

1. **Short paragraphs** - 1-3 sentences max
2. **One clear CTA** - Don't confuse them
3. **Always use PS** - Many read it first
4. **Story > lecture** - Teach through narrative
5. **Prove, don't claim** - Show results, testimonials
6. **Acknowledge objections** - Then overcome them
7. **Be conversational** - Un-template-y

---

## PS Formulas

**Restate offer:**
> PS. In case you skipped down here first, here's the deal: [one-sentence offer summary + link]

**Add urgency:**
> PS. This discount expires [date]. Here's the link again: [link]

**Add proof:**
> PS. Want to see what [Name] said about this? [testimonial or link]

**Tease content:**
> PS. Some people read the PS first. If that's you, go back and read the email - the good stuff is in there.

## Applying to Client Ready

### For $47 Front-End (Client Ready Offer Guide)
- **Soft Teach:** Show WHAT a clear offer looks like, not HOW to build one
- **Wrong Wrong Wrong:** Attack vague offers, transformation-speak

### For $97 OTO (Client Ready System)
- **Future You:** Paint picture of life with consistent clients

- **FAQ:** Address price, time, "will this work for me" objections

### For $5K+ Backend
- **Emotional Story:** Your journey, why you do this
- **Irrefutable Analogy:** DIY vs done-with-you comparison

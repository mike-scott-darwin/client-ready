The 3-Bump Checkout Stack

This month's play: stacking three order bumps on the $27 front-end checkout instead of running one. Here's the whole thing — the build, the numbers, and the copy you can paste into your own checkout today.

1. The Breakdown

The play: three sequential order bumps at checkout — not one.

Why I ran it: a $27 front-end doesn't pay for itself. The buyer is worth more than the entry. The question was how much of that value you can capture in the 30 seconds they already have their card out — before a single follow-up email.

The build: Bump 1 at $37 (the natural next step). Bump 2 at $67 (the "do it faster" tool). Bump 3 at $97 (the "close the bigger deal" asset). Each one is a yes/no checkbox, ordered small → large, each with a one-line reason it matters right now, at this step.

The lesson: the moment of highest intent is the checkout, not the inbox. Every bump you don't offer there, you're paying to recover later with email. Stack the offer where the intent already is.

2. Steal This

The bump-copy formula. Fill in the blanks and paste it at your checkout:

Yes! Add [PRODUCT] for just $[PRICE].
You just got [what they bought]. This is the [next thing] that makes it [work faster / go further]. Add it now — you won't see this price again.

Order them small to large. One reason each. Never explain more than one sentence — a bump is a reflex, not a decision.

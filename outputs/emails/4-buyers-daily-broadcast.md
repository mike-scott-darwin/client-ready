# Daily Broadcast Templates

**The compound advantage most coaches ignore: daily emails to your customer list.**

---

## Why Daily

From Miles Stutz:
> "Your customer list is your ATM. You write an email, you make money. But only if you show up daily."

**The math:**
- Weekly emailers: 52 touchpoints/year
- Daily emailers: 365 touchpoints/year
- 7x more opportunities to ascend buyers

**The psychology:**
- Buyers forget you within 72 hours
- Daily = always top of mind when ready to buy
- Story-first means you're not "that pitch person"

---

## The Framework

Every email follows the same structure:

### 1. Story (60% of email)
- Personal experience
- Client story
- Lesson learned
- Something that happened today/this week

**Not:** Generic advice, listicles, "5 tips for..."

### 2. Offer (30% of email)
- One product from your ladder
- Rotate through offers across the week
- Match story to offer when natural

### 3. CTA (10% of email)
- Single, clear action
- Link or reply instruction
- No multiple options

## Writing Process (15-20 min)

1. **Pick your story** (2 min) — What happened recently that illustrates a point?
2. **Write the story** (8 min) — 150-200 words, conversational
3. **Connect to offer** (3 min) — Natural transition, not forced
4. **Add CTA** (1 min) — One link or one reply instruction
5. **Write subject line** (1 min) — Curiosity or specificity

**Send time:** Same time every day (builds reader habit)

## Common Mistakes

1. **Emailing prospects, not buyers** — Daily rhythm is for CUSTOMERS only
2. **Pitching without story** — Every email needs the story first
3. **Multiple CTAs** — One email, one action
4. **Skipping days** — Consistency > perfection
5. **Over-producing** — 200 words > 2000 words
6. **Being generic** — Specificity beats broad advice

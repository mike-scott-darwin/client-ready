**Header**

> Seven years. Since 2018.

> 20 calls a week. Maybe 2 clients. **Something was wrong. And it wasn't my sales skills.**

**Part 2 — The Realization**
> The problem wasn't the calls. **It was the offer.**

> "I help coaches grow their business." What does that even mean? It could mean 50 things — so people needed a call just to figure out what they'd be buying. Every coach I looked at had the same problem: offers that promised everything, so vague they *required* a conversation to sell.

> That's not a sales problem. That's a design problem.

**Part 3 — What Changed Everything**
> I rebuilt my offer around one rule: **if someone can't understand what they're getting in 60 seconds, it isn't clear enough.**

> Instead of "I help coaches grow their business," I got specific: *"I help coaches build a $5K+ offer so clear it sells without a sales call — in one afternoon."*

> ✅ Clear outcome + timeframe — "build your offer in one afternoon"

> ✅ Clear delivery — you know exactly what you get
> ✅ Easy to start — instant access, low friction

> When I applied those four, everything changed. No more vague pitches. No more different answer every time. People saw the offer, understood it, and bought — or didn't. Either way, **the offer did the selling.**

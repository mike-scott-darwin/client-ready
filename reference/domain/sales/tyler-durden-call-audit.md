# Tyler Durden Call Audit

**Purpose:** Post-sales-call debrief framework. Captures customer data, identifies conversion blockers, and creates re-entry paths for maybes/nos.

---

## Section 1: Basics (From Application)

1. What was their specific, stated goal / problem?
2. In short, why does that matter to them?
3. What are they currently doing (or have tried to do) to achieve or solve it?
4. Why hasn't that been working?
5. Why is now the right time to solve it?
6. What will happen if they don't? How might that consequence manifest 3 days from now? 3 weeks? 3 months? (Risk Mitigator)
7. What specifically happened that motivated them to get on the call today? (MOHT — Moment of Highest Tension)

---

## Section 2: If a YES

**Vital customer data to capture and transfer into marketing:**

### What was true about them tangibly?
- What they've tried before
- How deep / far along in the process
- What they're currently doing
- Accomplishments / Abilities / Points of Pride (EE)
- What tipping point pains made them say "no more"

### What was true about them mentally/emotionally?
- Beliefs about themselves
- Beliefs about the problem / goal
- Beliefs about what it takes to achieve it
- How empowered they feel to solve it
- What breakthroughs / shifts did they experience on the call that LED to the yes?

---

## Section 3: If a Clear NO

### Reason for the "no"
- Not a good offer fit? (why or why not?)
- Not a good personality fit? (why or why not?)
- What could we do better in qualification to save time?

### If good fit but said "no"
What can we do to optimize the offer OR presentation of it?

### Follow-up triggers
- Different product/offer more appropriate for their level?
- Similar offer at a lower price point (course instead of coaching)?

---

## Section 4: If a "Maybe" / "Not Right Now"

**Most important:** Accurately log what they'll be doing instead.

### The Confirmation Script

**Weight loss example:**
> "OK, so what I'm hearing is that now isn't the right time for you because you're confident that you can commit yourself to working out 4 times per week using free videos on YouTube, and that you expect you'll lose 13 pounds and have more energy and confidence within 90 days?"

**If they say YES and no danger in going solo:**
> "Awesome. I appreciate your confidence and commitment in yourself. As you know, it's a challenging path that has a fair amount of risk — many people in your situation will get bogged down from a lack of clear direction and wondering if what they're doing is even working — which tends to hurt motivation. But it sounds like you're ready to give it an honest shot and see what you can do. I commend you for that. Is it cool if I follow up in about a month from now to make sure you're on track?"

**If you disagree with their path (health example):**
> "I respect your choice. But can I be direct? In all honesty, I'd be acting unethically if I didn't at least give you a gentle warning that I have a strong conviction that there are underlying causes that can't be addressed with a simple self-care ritual on its own. Would you be open to me checking in in two weeks from now to see how you're doing?"

### What are they doing instead?
- Finishing a different program
- Working on it solo doing X, Y, Z
- Nothing (out of resistance, convinced themselves they don't have a problem)

## Section 5: Re-Entry Path

### Template 1: Doing Something Else Instead
> "Hey [name], it was awesome connecting with you about 12 days ago around helping you [achieve goal / overcome problem] through Z program.
>
> At the time, you mentioned that you wanted to [what they're doing instead].
>
> Had you on my mind this morning and wanted to check-in to see how that's going so far?"

### Template 2: Needed More Time to Think
> "Hey [name], it was a pleasure connecting with you a few weeks ago. It really struck me when you said [motivation for solving problem].
>
> I wanted to check in to see how things have been since our call?"

### Template 3: Price Was the Barrier
> "Hey [name], it was awesome connecting with you a few weeks back. Was really inspired by [your goal / motivation], and felt a bit bad that your financial situation ultimately got in the way of us partnering together to [achieve Y or overcome Z].
>
> We've had a few people in your situation over the last few weeks, and were thinking of putting together a new package with a more accessible investment. Would you be interested in hearing more about it — or revisiting the initial one?"

### If No Reply to Email
1. Text or call them
2. Have team member "bump it" (F.U. 2)
3. "Haven't heard back. Sounds like this is a 'no'? All good, but we'd both do well with the clarity. Let me know." (F.U. 3)

## Section 6: Airport Moment (Go for Broke)

**When to use:** Within 48 hours, if you truly feel they're making a big mistake saying no.

**More appropriate in:** B2C (fitness, relationship, spiritual, mindset)
**Less appropriate in:** B2B (marketing, consulting)

### The Framework
1. Mirror back the goal
2. Mirror back the objection/reason for no
3. Put your cards on the table ("I want to work with you")
4. Suggest a call on "making it work"

### Example: Weight Loss Coach
> "Hey Emma, thx again for taking the time to connect last week; and for sharing so bravely.
>
> I'll be honest. You've been on my mind a lot these past few days. I have a ton of admiration and appreciation for your goal of losing 15 lbs this month and reclaiming your energy/power/confidence.
>
> I understand the finances were a bit of a challenge…
>
> But when it comes to your goal, I know it can be done.
>
> I personally thought we had a great vibe. You're someone I'd love to work with, and with your energy, enthusiasm and fierce commitment — I think we can be celebrating your new target weight together by this time next month.
>
> If you feel similarly, would you be open to a 20 minute chat tomorrow to see what we can do to make it work?"

## Application to Client Ready

**Primary use case:** Accelerator ($5K) follow-ups if someone doesn't immediately buy.

**Sections most relevant:**
- Section 4 (Maybe/Not Right Now) — for Sprint or Accelerator maybes
- Section 5 (Re-Entry Path) — templates adapt to any price point
- Section 6 (Airport Moment) — for high-ticket only

**Less relevant:** Sections 1-3 focus on sales calls, which Client Ready doesn't use for front-end.

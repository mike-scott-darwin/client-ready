# Content Ideas Library

## Offer Creation Pillar

### 1. "The $100 Rule" (LinkedIn Carousel)
**Idea:** 6-slide carousel explaining the $100 Rule — if your offer can't generate $100 from strangers in 7 days, pivot the messaging, not the offer.
**Data:** Gemini offer validation research — average validation period dropped from 30 days to 7-10 days. Long validation (30+ days) decreases success rates.
**Alignment:** Maps to offer.md: "Validate before you build" + "Good enough to launch, then refine." Maps to audience stuck pattern #5 (perfection as procrastination).
**CTA:** "Comment VALIDATE and I'll send you the 5 prompts that build your offer in one afternoon."

### 2. "Stop Surveying, Start Selling" (LinkedIn Text Post)
**Idea:** Short post on why surveys and focus groups show negative correlation with market success. The only validation that matters is someone paying you.
**Data:** Gemini offer validation — "Survey data and focus groups are showing negative correlation with actual market success."
**Alignment:** Maps to voice: "Test, validate, build." Maps to offer.md: "The only way to know if your offer works is to put it in front of real people."
**CTA:** Link to $47 offer.

### 3. "50 People Is Enough" (X Thread)
**Idea:** Thread on why you don't need a big audience to validate. Small, targeted tests (50-100 people) provide equally reliable signals as large launches.
**Data:** Gemini offer validation — "Larger audience size during validation doesn't correlate with better long-term success."
**Alignment:** Maps to audience.md pain point #2 (content merry-go-round) and offer.md: "Warm audience first." Directly addresses stuck pattern #4 (confusing silence with stagnation).

### 4. "Your Free Lead Magnet Is Lying to You" (LinkedIn Text Post)
**Idea:** Free signups don't predict buyers. SLOs have 15-25% higher validation accuracy than free lead magnets. The $47 test tells you more than 1,000 free downloads.
**Data:** Gemini offer validation — "Free lead magnets are proving to be poor predictors of actual buyer behavior."
**Alignment:** Maps to offer.md: "Self-liquidating checkout" philosophy. Maps to voice Pattern 2 ("Wrong" hook): "Build a free lead magnet first, then sell. Wrong."

### 5. "Minimum Viable Offer, Not Minimum Viable Product" (LinkedIn Carousel)
**Idea:** 8-slide carousel. The MVP approach is being replaced by "minimum viable offer" — test messaging and positioning BEFORE building anything. You don't need a course, a portal, or a funnel to validate.
**Data:** Gemini offer validation — "The traditional MVP approach is being replaced by 'minimum viable offer' testing."
**Alignment:** Maps directly to the $47 front-end (PDF + AI prompts, not a course). Maps to offer.md: "Extract, Validate, Build" — validate comes before build.

## Funnel Strategy Pillar

### 6. "82% Open Rates — The Email Nobody Optimizes" (LinkedIn Carousel)
**Idea:** Carousel on why your welcome sequence is the highest-leverage asset in your entire funnel. 82% open rates. Most coaches spend weeks on ads and 10 minutes on welcome emails.
**Data:** Gemini content pipeline — "Welcome email sequences achieve 82% open rates."
**Alignment:** Maps to offer.md: 10-Day Welcome Sequence (Days 1-10). Maps to email-rhythm.md. The welcome sequence is already built — this content positions it as THE priority.
**CTA:** "Comment WELCOME and I'll send you the 10-email framework."

### 7. "Your Email List Converts 2x Better Than Your Ad Traffic" (LinkedIn Text Post)
**Idea:** Organic newsletter subscribers convert to buyers at 2.6% vs 1.5% for paid acquisition. The cheapest sale you'll ever make is to someone who already reads your emails.
**Data:** Gemini content pipeline — "Newsletter subscribers from organic sources convert to buyers at 2.6% vs 1.5% for paid acquisition."
**Alignment:** Maps to content-strategy.md: "Email is the revenue engine; social is discovery." Maps to offer.md: daily email rhythm as the ascension mechanism.

### 8. "The 14-Day Window" (X Thread)
**Idea:** Thread on the newsletter-to-buyer conversion timeline. Average is 14-21 days. If someone subscribes and doesn't buy in 3 weeks, they probably won't. That's why the welcome sequence matters more than the broadcast.
**Data:** Gemini content pipeline — "Average newsletter-to-buyer conversion timeline: 14-21 days."
**Alignment:** Maps to offer.md Iron Strike Principle: "Ascension probability peaks in the first 7-21 days after purchase."

### 9. "The Bump Nobody Talks About" (LinkedIn Text Post)
**Idea:** Why higher-priced bumps convert better than cheap ones. Your $97 bump might outperform your $37 bump — because sophisticated buyers trust higher prices more.
**Data:** Offer.md: "Cat Howell split test: $33/$44/$55 bumps at 4.0% conversion vs $17/$33/$55 at 2.3%."
**Alignment:** Maps to funnel strategy pillar directly. Voice pattern: counterintuitive claim.

## Alignment Pillar

### 10. "The 3 Questions That Tell You If You Have the Wrong Offer" (LinkedIn Carousel)
**Idea:** The alignment diagnostic as a carousel. 8 slides: hook → 3 questions → what each answer reveals → the insight → CTA. This is the most original idea in the brand and it's buried in audience.md. Make it headline-level.
**Data:** Audience.md alignment diagnostic — "What do you research when no one's watching?" / "What intersections excite you?" / "What decisions feel like discovery vs obligation?"
**Alignment:** This IS the alignment engineer positioning. Maps to the new Angle 4. Maps to stuck pattern #6 (identity mismatch) and #11 (safety as slow death).
**CTA:** "If you answered and something felt off — that's the signal. Comment ALIGN."

### 11. "The Offer You'll Burn Down at $8K" (LinkedIn Text Post)
**Idea:** Short, punchy post. "The offer you'll burn down at $8K isn't better than the one you'll show up for at $3K. Alignment beats scale. Every time." One-paragraph expansion on why coaches who scale misaligned offers eventually quit.
**Data:** Voice.md alignment hooks + audience.md stuck pattern #11 (safety as slow death).
**Alignment:** Core brand phrase: "You can't grow into pain." New alignment engineer positioning.

### 12. "I Didn't Quit My 9-to-5 to Build Another Grind" (LinkedIn Text Post)
**Idea:** Personal story post. The moment you realized the coaching business you were building felt exactly like the job you left. What changed when you started building for alignment instead of scale.
**Data:** Gemini creator positioning — "Hybrid approaches (system + personality) work best." This is the personality injection the research calls for.
**Alignment:** Maps to audience.md: "Want freedom, not just income — don't want to trade one grind for another." Maps to stuck pattern #3 (the grind trap). Voice: Story → Lesson pattern.

### 13. "Build Something Worth Quitting For" (X Thread)
**Idea:** 5-tweet thread. Why "quit your 9-to-5" is bad advice, but "build something worth quitting for" changes everything. The difference is alignment — building from who you are, not who the market says you should be.
**Data:** Alignment hooks: "I'm not going to tell you to quit your 9-to-5. I'm going to help you build something worth quitting for."
**Alignment:** Signature phrase. Maps to audience.md: "Still have a 9-to-5 (that's okay)." The 9-to-5 angle is a primary differentiator.

### 14. "Engineer Your Own Playbook" (LinkedIn Carousel)
**Idea:** Carousel comparing "following someone else's playbook" vs "engineering your own." 6 slides showing the symptoms of each: burnout vs energy, generic vs specific, copying vs extracting.
**Data:** Gemini creator positioning — "Systems-based approaches rather than relying on personal brand equity." Audience stuck pattern #6: "They're forcing someone else's playbook."
**Alignment:** New alignment engineer identity. Maps to offer.md: "Extraction over templates — Client Ready pulls out what you already know."

## Behind the Scenes Pillar

### 15. "Why I Moved LinkedIn to Primary" (LinkedIn Text Post)
**Idea:** Behind the scenes: the data that made you flip LinkedIn to primary and X to secondary. Share the actual benchmarks. Show the decision-making process. This IS the builder identity — showing how you think, not just what you think.
**Data:** Gemini content pipeline — LinkedIn 5.2% vs X 0.5-1%. Diminishing returns after 5 posts/week.
**Alignment:** Voice: concrete, shows the math. Content pillar: behind the scenes. Also demonstrates the alignment engineer identity — "I looked at the data and changed my approach."

### 16. "What 72% of Coaches Get Wrong About Validation" (LinkedIn Text Post)
**Idea:** 72% of successful validations use paid traffic, not organic. If you're trying to validate your offer by posting and hoping, you're using the wrong signal. Paid traffic tells you in 7 days what organic takes 3 months to reveal.
**Data:** Gemini offer validation — "72% of successful offer validations in 2026 use paid traffic."
**Alignment:** Maps to offer.md: "Paid traffic over content merry-go-round." Maps to audience pain point #2 (content merry-go-round).

### 17. "My Content Costs: $0/Month" (LinkedIn Text Post)
**Idea:** Behind the scenes of the newsletter-first, AI-assisted content system. Write once (newsletter), deconstruct into LinkedIn + X. 2-3 hours per week. No team. No agency. Just a system.
**Data:** Content-strategy.md: Phase 0 workflow. "Write once, repurpose everywhere."
**Alignment:** Offer.md: "AI does 90% of the heavy lifting." Voice: shows the system, not just the result.

### 18. "Small Accounts Win" (X Thread)
**Idea:** Thread on why small accounts (<5K followers) see 2-5x higher engagement than large accounts. Your 500 followers are more valuable than someone's 50K if they're the right people. Stop chasing follower count.
**Data:** Gemini content pipeline — "Small accounts (<5,000 followers) see 2-5x higher engagement than large accounts."
**Alignment:** Maps to audience.md: "Skeptical of 'scale to $50K months' promises." Maps to offer.md: "Warm audience first." Alignment over scale.

## Alignment Check

Every idea in this file was validated against:
- **offer.md** — Does it support the value ladder or a specific product?
- **audience.md** — Does it address a real pain point or stuck pattern?
- **voice.md** — Does it sound like Michael? Builder, not critic? Direct, not preachy?
- **main-angles.md** — Does it map to a proven angle?
- **content-strategy.md** — Does it fit a pillar? Is the format right for the platform?

If an idea doesn't pass all five, it doesn't go in.

# Product Ladder

## Brand Thesis

Michael Scott helps business owners build businesses they won't abandon — through alignment, codification, and systems that compound. Two offers serve this mission at different levels.

---

## Offers

### Client Ready (Original)

- **Type:** Low-ticket → High-ticket funnel
- **Price Range:** $47 front-end → $5,000 accelerator
- **Purpose in ladder:** Entry point for solopreneurs and coaches who need offer clarity
- **Target:** People in transition — have expertise, need a clear offer and funnel
- **Mechanism:** Extract → Validate → Build → Automate
- **Feeds into:** Codification (once their business is running, they need AI to scale it)
- **Status:** Active, proven, 322+ commits of reference data

### Codification (New)

- **Type:** Workshop → Sprint → Licensed architecture (recurring)
- **Price Range:** $197 workshop → $2,497/month enterprise license
- **Purpose in ladder:** Mid/high-ticket for established businesses that need AI to actually work
- **Target:** AI-literate business owners ($100K-$500K+ revenue) who've tried AI and it underdelivered
- **Mechanism:** Extract → Codify → Generate → Compound
- **Feeds from:** Client Ready (graduates who need AI systems) + direct acquisition (businesses that already have offers but lack codification)
- **Status:** Building — Week 1

```
    ↓                                     ↓

    ↓                                     ↓
    └──────── BRIDGE ─────────────────────┘

Client Ready graduates who want AI
to run their business → Codification
```

### The Bridge

Client Ready gets them a business. Codification gets them a system.

A Client Ready Accelerator graduate has:
- A validated offer
- A running funnel
- Active clients

What they DON'T have:
- Structured business knowledge that AI can use
- A system that compounds without increasing hours
- AI outputs that sound like them from the first draft

The codification offer picks up exactly where Client Ready leaves off. But it also serves people who ALREADY have businesses and never needed Client Ready.

## Content Strategy Implications

Both offers share:
- Core beliefs (soul.md — "you can't grow into pain")
- Voice (direct, no-BS, framework-driven)
- Newsletter platform (Beehiiv)

But content splits by angle:
- **Client Ready content:** Offer clarity, validation, funnel building, paid traffic
- **Codification content:** Context > prompts, AI architecture, knowledge management, before/after demos

The codification angle has broader appeal and higher ceiling. Client Ready content serves the existing funnel. Both compound the personal brand.

---

## Why One Repo, Not Two

These offers share `soul.md` and `voice.md`. Same person. Same beliefs. Same communication style. Different products serving different stages of the same journey.

If the codification business eventually develops its own distinct voice or soul, it moves to a separate repo. For now, the shared foundation is genuine — both offers exist because Michael believes "you can't grow into pain" and systems should compound.

Client Ready IS the case study that proves codification works. Separating them would break the proof chain.

# Voice Dump Process

Your thinking process for staying connected to Client Ready. Adapted from Devon's walk-to-research cycle, compressed for a 9-to-5 schedule.

**The principle:** Ideas don't come at the keyboard. They come in motion. The voice dump captures what the walk reveals. /think turns it into research, decisions, and reference updates.

---

## The Cycle

**Weekday (2-3x per week, 45 min total):**

1. Walk and Record -- 15-30 min
2. Transcribe -- 5 min
3. Paste into /think -- 5 min
4. Read synthesis -- 10 min
5. Decide and codify -- 10 min

**Weekend (1x per week, 90 min total):**

1. Walk and Record -- 60 min
2. Transcribe -- 5 min
3. Paste into /think (full research wave) -- 5 min
4. Read synthesis -- 10 min
5. Multiple decisions and codify -- 15 min

---

## Step 1: Walk and Record

Leave your computer. Walk, drive, sit outside -- whatever gets you away from the screen. Open your phone's voice recorder and just talk.

**No structure needed. Don't filter. Don't organize. Just talk.**

### Prompts If Stuck

- What's bothering me about Client Ready right now?
- Who bought recently and why?
- Who didn't buy and what does that tell me?
- What would make me excited to show up tomorrow?
- If I could only fix one thing in the business, what is it?
- What am I avoiding?
- What's the gap between what I'm selling and what I actually want to build?
- What did I learn this week that changes how I think about my audience?

---

## Step 2: Transcribe

Use your phone's built-in transcription, a free service, or whisper if set up. Doesn't need to be perfect -- /think works with rough transcripts.

---

## Step 3: Paste Into /think

Open a session. Paste the transcript. Say something like:

"Here's a voice dump from my walk. Seed research from whatever threads you find."

/think will identify threads of interest and spawn research agents on each one. It checks your reference files against what it finds in the market.

---

## Step 4: Read the Synthesis

Research agents return summaries. Read them. Some confirm your instincts. Some challenge them. The interesting ones are the challenges.

**Optional:** Get a second opinion. Paste the synthesis into Gemini or another tool. Different AI tools think differently -- Devon calls these "different flavor profiles."

---

## Step 5: Decide and Codify

When something clicks -- a pricing insight, an audience shift, a new angle -- say "let's decide on this." That becomes a decision file. Then say "codify" and the changes get applied to your reference files.

Your offer.md, audience.md, voice.md, and content-strategy.md get sharper every cycle.

---

## What This Replaces

Sitting at the computer trying to "figure out" the next move. The ideas don't come at the keyboard. Devon's entire business runs on this insight.

---

## Why This Works

From soul.md: "Reference files aren't just documentation. They're how you stay connected to why you do this."

The voice dump is the reconnection mechanism. When you record yourself talking about Client Ready, you're forced to articulate what matters. That's identity work. The research and decisions are just the structure that captures what the walk reveals.

---

## The Compound Effect

- Week 1: Feels awkward. Mostly rambling.
- Week 2-3: Threads start emerging. You hear yourself repeat certain frustrations or ideas.
- Month 1: Reference files noticeably sharper. Content ideas flow from the dumps.
- Month 2+: You stop guessing what to work on. The voice dump tells you.

Devon went from 5 research files per day to 20, with 5-7 decisions each. You won't hit that pace on a 9-to-5 schedule -- and you don't need to. One good decision per week compounds into a fundamentally different business over a quarter.

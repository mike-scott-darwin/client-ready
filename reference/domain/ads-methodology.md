# Low-Ticket Ads Methodology

Unified framework synthesizing Miles Stutz (copy/messaging) and Cat Howell (campaign structure/scaling).

---

## Philosophy

**Miles:** Headlines tied to payoff. Sell to their standard, not yours.

**Cat:** Positioning over everything. Your job is to feed Facebook creative across the awareness scale.

**Both:** Test the offer first. Don't trust early data. Static ads often beat video.

---

## The Hierarchy: What to Get Right First

| Priority | Element | Why |
|----------|---------|-----|
| 1 | **Positioning** | Everything downstream depends on this |
| 2 | **Offer** | Can't sell what nobody wants |
| 3 | **Headline/Hook** | Stops the scroll, sets the promise |
| 4 | **Copy** | Delivers the argument |
| 5 | **Creative** | Gets attention, supports the message |
| 6 | **Campaign Structure** | Gets the message to the right people |

## Part 1: Copy & Messaging (Miles Stutz)

### The Headline Formula

```
[Action Verb] + [Unique Mechanism] + [Payoff They Want]
```

**Examples:**
- "Integrate the cashflow mindset and never struggle to sign 5-20K clients"
- "Instantly eradicate a batting slump with this 30-second reset"
- "Use the calendar clear to create 2 hours of white space every day"

### The Payoff Rule

> "The payoff you've got to tie all of your headlines to is money."

For coaches/consultants: tie EVERYTHING to making more money. You're in the money-making industry now. The vehicle is different (mindset, systems, frameworks), but the payoff is the same.

### Sell to Their Standard

> "You've got to speak to their standards because if you don't, then you first have to educate them about your standards."

Don't try to change their benchmark. Writing copy that shifts their standard requires too much effort — you'll lose the lead. Meet them where they are.

**Example:** Cricket batters hold themselves to 50+ runs. Don't sell "better mindset" — sell "hit 50s consistently, even after bad scores."

### Product Naming

If your product name requires explanation, you lose people in the first few seconds.

| Bad (Requires Explanation) | Good (Instantly Clear) |
|----------------------------|------------------------|
| "Digital Snacks" | "The Free Client System" |
| "Parent Business Week" | "The Daily Client Funnel" |
| "The Reset Protocol" | "The Cashflow Mindset" |

### Proof Framing (Avoiding Income Claims)

Don't promise THEY will make money. Share YOUR experience.

> "I closed a $15,000 client yesterday. Here's why mindset was the key..."

This tells your story without making income claims about them.

## Part 2: Campaign Structure (Cat Howell 2026)

### Old Way (Don't Do This)
- Separate ad sets for TOF/MOF/BOF
- Interest-based targeting
- Lookalike audiences
- Ad set budgets (ABO for scaling)
- Retargeting campaigns
- Audience exclusions

### New Way
- **One ad set** with Advantage Plus targeting
- **Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO)** only
- **All creative in one ad set**
- **Target only by country/region** — no interests, no lookalikes, no exclusions
- Let the algorithm find your audience through your creative

### Campaign Setup Steps

1. Click Create → **Sales objective**
2. Enable **Campaign Budget Optimization**
3. Set daily budget (see Budget Table below)
4. At ad set level:
   - Website only (NOT "Website and Calls")
   - Select your pixel
   - Purchase as conversion event
   - Advantage Plus targeting
   - Add countries only (US, UK, CA, AU, NZ)
5. At ad level:
   - Turn **OFF** Advantage+ Creative
   - Turn **OFF** all creative enhancements (except maybe music)
   - Turn **OFF** site links
   - Use **"Learn More"** CTA for low-ticket

### Why Single Ads Beat Multi-Variant

Multi-variant: Can't know which creative got the sale.
Single ads: Know exactly what works, can duplicate and tweak.

> "When I find a winner, the first thing I'll do is duplicate this and change the headline."

## Part 3: Testing Protocol

### Testing Order (Miles)

1. **Offer first** — Can have the best avatar and angle, but if nobody wants the offer, nothing else matters
2. **Angles second** — Test different headlines and hooks
3. **Avatars third** — Which audience segment responds best
4. **Creative last** — Images, videos, copy variations

### Validation Thresholds (Cat)

| Sales | Status | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 5-10 | "Onto something" | Keep running, don't optimize |
| 30 | Minimum to trust data | Can start A/B testing |
| 100 | Truly validated | Numbers stabilize, scale confidently |

> "The first six sales that come in, I'm like, oh, this page is the winner for sure... But when I leave it 30, 40 sales, sometimes it's the total opposite."

### Budget & Creative Ratio

| Daily Budget | Number of Ads | Notes |
|--------------|---------------|-------|
| $25-30/day | 3-4 ads | Minimum viable — need enough spend per ad |
| $100/day | 8-10 ads | Sweet spot for testing |
| $500+/day | 15-20+ ads | Scale by adding creative |

**The math:** At 2% conversion and $2 CPC, you need 50 clicks = $100 to expect one sale.

## Part 4: The Awareness Framework

Based on Eugene Schwartz's "Breakthrough Advertising" — the key to scaling.

| Level | Definition | Creative Approach |
|-------|------------|-------------------|
| **Aware** | Knows you, knows your product | Direct response: "$27 — Start Now" |
| **Solution Aware** | Knows solutions exist, shopping | "Why this beats [alternative]" |
| **Problem Aware** | Knows they have a problem | Pain points, "Are you..." |
| **Unaware** | Doesn't know they have a problem | Education, story, belief shifts |

### Why Ads Die After Week 1

Facebook shows your ads to warm audience first (aware/solution aware). Once that's exhausted, it shifts to cold (problem aware/unaware).

**If you only have "aware" creative, your ads tank when Facebook hits cold audience.**

### When You Hit Each Level

- **$100-500/day:** Mostly aware audience, good CPAs
- **$500-1000/day:** CPAs start rising, hitting problem aware
- **$1000+/day:** Definitely hitting unaware, need strategy changes

### Ad Naming Convention

Name ads by awareness level + format + destination:

```
Aware - Image - Order Page
Solution Aware - Video - Sales Page
Problem Aware - Carousel - Sales Page
Unaware - Text - Sales Page
```

## Part 5: Scaling

### Frequency Rules

| Frequency | Status | Action |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| < 2 | Healthy | Can scale |
| 2 | Warning | Monitor closely |
| 3+ | Fatigued | Do NOT scale — add new creative first |

> "If you try to scale a campaign with fatigued ads, you're gonna blow that up."

### Scaling Method

1. Add more creative (scale by feeding the algorithm)
2. Duplicate ad set with new creative when hitting ad limits
3. Raise budget as you add creative

> "Every time you raise the budget, you should be adding more ads into your ad set."

### CPA Reality Check

> "A $100 CPA is quite normal. If your funnel converts at 2% and you are getting $2 link clicks then that's a $100 CPA."

**The fix:** Increase conversion rate OR decrease CPC. If you have a healthy AOV, $100 CPA is fine.

### Optimize for ROAS, Not Lowest CPA

> "Some of my greatest campaigns with return on ad spend and AOV were my most expensive cost per purchase."

Don't chase lowest CPA. Optimize for:
1. ROAS (are you profitable?)
2. AOV (are customers spending?)

### Scaling by Targeting Unaware

> "The best way to reduce your CPA at scale is to hyper-segment your ad copy and sales page."

When CPA rises at scale:
1. It's usually because Facebook depleted the aware segment
2. Build creative that speaks to problem aware/unaware
3. Consider separate landing pages for cold traffic

## Part 6: Pixel & Tracking

### Basic Setup

1. **Base pixel** → Head code of entire funnel
2. **Standard purchase event** → Page AFTER order form (upsell page, not confirmation)

### CAPI (Conversion API)

Standard pixel = browser-based (affected by cookie blocking)
CAPI = server-side (accurate, tracks AOV)

> "If you're tracking average order value, Facebook will start to optimize for purchase conversion value."

### CAPI Setup

- Use **Stape.io** ($10/month) for non-techie setup
- Once CAPI works, remove standard purchase event (prevents double reporting)

> "CAPI tracking will give you solid reporting. That's all you need. You don't need Hyros."

## Part 7: Troubleshooting

### The Diagnostic Checklist

| Check | Target | If Bad |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Enough spend at ad level? | $100 per ad minimum | Wait for data |
| Enough landing page views? | 50 views minimum | Wait for data |
| CPC acceptable? | Under $7 | New hooks/creative |
| CTR acceptable? | Over 1% | Hook not stopping scroll |
| Ad "keeps the scent"? | Match promise to page | Fix congruence |
| Messaging/positioning? | Clicks but no sales | Sales page is losing the job |
| Pricing? | $17-$67 cold | Test price points |

### Video vs Static

> "Video ads, I've tried them so often, and they just never convert. But when you get a video ad to convert, it has a lot more sustainability."

**Current reality:** Static ads crushing. Text-on-black works. Video is harder to crack but has more longevity when it works.

## Part 8: AOV Levers

### 1. Order Bumps (Highest Impact)

More order bumps nearly **DOUBLED** conversion rates in Cat's split test:

| Metric | Control (fewer bumps) | Variation (3 bumps) |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Sales rate | 4.94% | **9.44%** (+91%) |
| Earnings/page view | $3.39 | **$6.10** (+80%) |

### 2. Front-End Price Point

> "When I did a $17 promo, my AOV went right down. More sales, more volume — but they were not buying as many bumps and upsells than when selling at $47 or $67."

### 3. Two-Step Order Forms

> "We usually roll with two-step order forms. First thing: name and email. Then credit card. That way you can do remarketing or abandoned cart sequences."

Always capture email before payment info.

## Part 9: Engagement Campaigns

Separate from conversion campaigns. Purpose: Build omnipresence cheaply.

**Structure:**
- $3-25/day budget
- 2 posts per ad set
- Separate campaigns for IG and Facebook
- Rotate content weekly (check organic engagement first)
- Target: Cold + Warm (engaged last 30 days)

**Why it works:**
- Cheap (video views/engagement = cheapest objectives)
- Builds omnipresence
- Social proofs your content
- Auction performance: engagement helps you compete

## Part 10: Email Follow-Up Structure (Miles)

Every offer is an argument. To win, establish CIRCUMSTANCE first.

### Sequence Structure

1. **Circumstance** — Why is this relevant RIGHT NOW?
2. **Problem amplification** — Problems caused by the circumstance
3. **Solution** — Why your mechanism resolves these problems
4. **Objection removal** — Handle limitations and concerns
5. **Value stack** — What they get
6. **Scarcity/Urgency** — Why now

## Key Benchmarks

| Metric | Target | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Landing page conversion | 2-5% cold, 10-15% warm | Below 2% = messaging problem |
| CPC | Under $7 | Higher OK for niche B2B |
| CTR | Over 1% | Below = hook problem |
| Cost per sale | $50-100 | At 2% CVR, $2 CPC |
| AOV | $100+ | Need bumps/upsells |
| ROAS | 2.0+ | Cat's range: 2.0-2.35 |

# Client Ready Accelerator — Delivery Framework

**The 8-Week Accelerator ($5,000) delivery system.**

This document defines how the Accelerator is delivered — a done-with-you intensive that takes clients from validated offer to profitable funnel.

---

## The Core Principle

> "This isn't coaching. This is building together."

The Accelerator is different from the Community or DFY Offer Build:
- **Community:** You do the work, guided by curriculum and group calls
- **DFY Offer Build:** I build your offer document, you implement
- **Accelerator:** We build together, I do heavy lifting, you make decisions

At any moment, an Accelerator client should know:
1. **What we're building this phase**
2. **What you need to provide** (decisions, content, feedback)
3. **What I'm handling** (implementation, strategy, optimization)

---

## Who This Is For

Accelerator clients have already:
- Validated their offer (bought $47, gone through Community curriculum, or have existing traction)
- Decided they want speed + expert guidance, not DIY
- Committed to invest in getting it right the first time

**Not for:** People who haven't validated, want to "figure it out themselves," or aren't ready to make decisions quickly.

## The 8-Week Structure

### Three Phases, Three Calls

| Phase | Weeks | Focus | Call |
|-------|-------|-------|------|
| **Foundation** | 1-2 | Offer refinement, funnel architecture, positioning | Call 1: Kickoff |
| **Build** | 3-5 | Landing page, emails, checkout, assets | Call 2: Midpoint |
| **Launch** | 6-8 | Traffic, optimization, scaling plan | Call 3: Launch + Optimization |

**The rhythm:** Build async between calls. Calls are for alignment, decisions, and course-correction.

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2)

### Objective
Lock in the offer, map the funnel, establish positioning.

### Week 1: Kickoff + Extraction

**Day 1-2: Onboarding**
- Client completes deep-dive onboarding form (see below)
- Michael reviews and prepares for kickoff call
- WhatsApp chat created, intro message sent

**Day 3-4: Kickoff Call (60 min)**

Call structure:
| Segment | Time | Focus |
|---------|------|-------|
| Context review | 10 min | Review onboarding form, clarify gaps |
| Offer refinement | 20 min | Zone of genius, ideal client, pricing, positioning |
| Funnel architecture | 15 min | Map the complete system (front-end → backend) |
| Deliverables | 10 min | What I'll build, what you'll provide, timeline |
| Next steps | 5 min | First async deliverables, questions |

**Day 5-7: Post-Kickoff Build**

Michael delivers:
- Funnel architecture document (visual map of entire system)
- Offer positioning document (refined messaging, pricing rationale)
- Initial landing page outline

Client provides:
- Story elements (background, transformation, why you do this)
- Proof elements (testimonials, results, credentials)
- Voice samples (existing content, how they naturally communicate)

### Week 2: Positioning Lock

**Async work:**
- Michael refines positioning based on client feedback
- Messaging map created (headlines, hooks, core copy angles)
- Landing page copy draft started

**Deliverables by end of Week 2:**
- [ ] Funnel architecture locked
- [ ] Offer positioning locked
- [ ] Messaging map complete
- [ ] Landing page outline approved

**Client check-in:** Loom review of positioning + messaging. Client approves or requests changes.

---

## Phase 2: Build (Weeks 3-5)

### Objective
Build all funnel assets — landing page, emails, checkout, bumps.

### Week 3: Landing Page + Core Copy

**Async work:**
- Landing page copy complete (full draft)
- Headline variations for testing
- Design direction / wireframe

**Client provides:**
- Feedback on copy draft (voice check, accuracy)
- Any additional proof elements
- Design preferences (examples they like)

**Midpoint Call Prep:**
- Michael sends Loom walkthrough of landing page copy
- Client reviews before Call 2

### Week 4: Midpoint Call + Email System

**Midpoint Call (60 min)**

Call structure:
| Segment | Time | Focus |
|---------|------|-------|
| Landing page review | 20 min | Walk through copy, get approval or changes |
| Email system mapping | 15 min | 10-day welcome, daily rhythm, ascension |
| Checkout + bumps | 10 min | Order bump strategy, pricing |
| Traffic preview | 10 min | Where traffic will come from, initial targeting |
| Next steps | 5 min | Final build phase deliverables |

**Post-call build:**
- Landing page finalized
- Email sequences drafted:
  - 10-day welcome sequence
  - Daily email templates (first 7 days of broadcast)
  - Ascension emails (Community → DFY Offer Build → Accelerator)
- Checkout page copy
- Order bump copy

### Week 5: Assembly + Tech

**Async work:**
- All copy delivered in master document
- Tech setup guidance (or done-for-you if included)
- Checkout configuration
- Email automation setup

**Deliverables by end of Week 5:**
- [ ] Landing page live (or ready to go live)
- [ ] Checkout configured with bumps
- [ ] Email sequences loaded and tested
- [ ] Thank you / access flow working

**Client check-in:** Full funnel walkthrough via Loom. Test purchase to verify everything works.

---

## Phase 3: Launch (Weeks 6-8)

### Objective
Turn on traffic, optimize based on data, establish scaling plan.

### Week 6: Traffic Setup

**Async work:**
- Ad creative direction (hooks, angles, image concepts)
- Targeting strategy document
- Campaign structure recommendation
- First ad copy drafts (3-5 variations)

**Client provides:**
- Any existing ad account access (or sets up new)
- Approval on creative direction
- Budget confirmation

**If client runs ads themselves:**
- Michael provides complete playbook + Loom walkthrough
- Client launches, reports metrics

**If Michael assists with setup:**
- Campaign structure built
- First ads launched
- Daily check-in on initial performance

### Week 7: Optimization

**Focus:** Read the data, make adjustments.

**Async work:**
- Review first week of traffic data
- Identify what's working (hooks, audiences, pages)
- Recommend optimizations (kill/scale decisions)
- Landing page tweaks if needed
- Email sequence adjustments based on open/click rates

**Client check-in:** Loom with data review + recommendations. Client approves changes.

### Week 8: Launch Call + Scaling Plan

**Launch Call (60 min)**

Call structure:
| Segment | Time | Focus |
|---------|------|-------|
| Results review | 15 min | What happened, what the data shows |
| Optimization review | 15 min | What we changed, what's working now |
| Scaling plan | 15 min | How to scale from here (budget, new creatives, audiences) |
| 90-day roadmap | 10 min | What to do for the next 3 months |
| Q&A + close | 5 min | Final questions, next steps |

**Deliverables by end of Week 8:**
- [ ] Traffic flowing and optimized
- [ ] First buyers (or clear path to first buyers)
- [ ] Scaling playbook (what to do next)
- [ ] 90-day roadmap document
- [ ] All assets handed over (copy docs, templates, access)

## Onboarding (Day 0-2)

### Immediate (Automated)

**Upon purchase:**
- Thank you page with welcome video
- Receipt email with what happens next
- Deep-dive onboarding form link

**Welcome Video Script (90 seconds):**
> "You're in the Accelerator. Here's what happens now: You'll get an onboarding form — takes about 15 minutes. Be thorough. The more you give me, the better I can build for you. Once you submit, I'll review it and we'll schedule your kickoff call within 48 hours. By the end of 8 weeks, you'll have a complete, profitable funnel. Not theory — the actual thing, built and running. Let's go."

### Deep-Dive Onboarding Form

**Section 1: Your Business**
- What do you do? (full description, not elevator pitch)
- Who do you help? (ideal client in detail)
- What transformation do you provide?
- What's your current offer structure? (prices, formats)
- What have you already validated? (sales, conversations, proof)

**Section 2: Your Funnel Goals**
- What's your front-end offer? (or what should it be?)
- What's your backend? (high-ticket, if any)
- What's your target AOV?
- What's your traffic budget (monthly)?
- What traffic sources have you tried?

**Section 3: Your Assets**
- Do you have existing copy? (landing pages, emails, ads)
- Do you have testimonials/case studies?
- Do you have a brand guide or voice examples?
- What tech are you using? (page builder, email, checkout)

**Section 4: Your Constraints**
- What's your availability for calls? (time zones, preferences)
- What decisions do you need help with vs. already know?
- What's worked before? What hasn't?
- What would make this investment worth it?

### Within 48 Hours

- Michael reviews form
- WhatsApp channel created with intro message
- Kickoff call scheduled
- Any clarifying questions sent async

## Async Support System

### WhatsApp Chat (Private)

**What it's for:**
- Quick questions
- Decision check-ins
- Sharing work for feedback
- Progress updates

**Response time:** Within 24 hours (business days), often faster

**Not for:** Long strategy sessions (save for calls), tech troubleshooting (that's tutorials/docs)

### Loom Reviews

**From Michael:**
- Deliverable walkthroughs (here's what I built, here's why)
- Strategy explanations (here's my thinking)
- Feedback on client-provided materials

**From client:**
- Questions that need visual context
- "Here's what I'm seeing" troubleshooting
- Approval/change requests on drafts

**Cadence:** As needed, typically 2-4 Looms per week during Build phase

### Weekly Pulse Check

**Every Monday (automated or manual):**

> "Week [X] check-in:
> 1. What I'm working on this week: [list]
> 2. What I need from you: [list]
> 3. Any blockers or questions?
>
> Reply here or drop a Loom if easier."

## What Success Looks Like

### For the Client (by Week 8)

- **Complete funnel live:** Landing page, checkout, bumps, emails, thank you
- **Traffic flowing:** Ads running or clear organic strategy executing
- **First buyers:** Revenue from the funnel (even if small)
- **Scaling clarity:** Knows exactly what to do for the next 90 days
- **Assets owned:** All copy, templates, strategies in their possession

### For You

- **Case study:** Documented transformation with metrics
- **Testimonial:** "Built my entire funnel in 8 weeks" proof
- **Referral potential:** Accelerator clients refer other Accelerator clients
- **Repeat business:** Some come back for new offers or ongoing support

## The Guarantee in Action

> "Profitable funnel or we keep working until it is."

### What "Profitable" Means

- Funnel is live and accepting payments
- Traffic is flowing (paid or organic)
- At least one sale has been made OR clear path to first sale with data showing what to fix

### What "Keep Working" Looks Like

**If by Week 8 they're not profitable:**

1. **Diagnose:** What's the actual blocker? (traffic, conversion, offer, targeting)
2. **Extend:** Additional 2-4 weeks of async support focused on the blocker
3. **Iterate:** Specific changes to test, with check-ins on results

**The conversation:**
> "We're at Week 8 and [X] isn't where we want it. Here's what the data shows: [diagnosis]. I'm committed to getting you profitable. Here's the plan: [specific next steps]. Let's extend support for [X weeks] and focus specifically on [the blocker]."

### When the Guarantee Doesn't Apply

- Client didn't provide requested materials (no story, no proof, no feedback)
- Client disappeared for weeks without communication
- Client made major changes without discussion (pivoted offer mid-build)
- External factors outside the funnel (product quality, fulfillment issues)

## Capacity Planning

### Accelerator Client Load

**Recommended:** 2-4 active Accelerator clients at a time
**Why:** High-touch requires real attention. More than 4 = quality drops.

### Michael's Time Commitment

| Activity | Time | Frequency |
|----------|------|-----------|
| Strategy calls | 60 min each | 3 per client (Weeks 1, 4, 8) |
| Async building (copy, docs) | 4-6 hrs/week | Weeks 1-5 |
| Async support (WhatsApp, Loom) | 3-4 hrs/week | All 8 weeks |
| Client review + prep | 1-2 hrs/week | All 8 weeks |
| **Total per client** | ~8-12 hrs/week | 8 weeks |

**Total hours per Accelerator client:** 64-96 hours over 8 weeks

At $5,000, that's ~$52-78/hour effective rate. Scale by raising price or reducing hours through better systems.

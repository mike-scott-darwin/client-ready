# Client Ready Sprint — Delivery Framework

**The 4-Week Sprint ($297) delivery system.**

This document defines how the Sprint is delivered — not what's taught (see `classroom/modules.md`), but how clients experience it.

---

## The Core Principle

> "Clients don't need more content. They need clarity."

At any moment, a Sprint client should know:
1. **Where they are** in the journey
2. **What comes next**
3. **What they can safely ignore right now**

---

## The 4 Milestones

The Sprint maps to the Client Ready Method:

| Week | Milestone | Outcome | Call Focus |
|------|-----------|---------|------------|
| 1 | **Extract** | Offer document complete | Zone of genius extraction, ideal client, pain points |
| 2 | **Validate** | Offer tested with warm audience | DM outreach, first conversations, feedback loops |
| 3 | **Build** | Funnel live and accepting payments | Landing page, checkout, bumps, email setup |
| 4 | **Launch** | Traffic flowing, first buyers | Ads or organic traffic, optimization, next steps |

**Each milestone has ONE clear deliverable.** Don't move on until it's done.

## Onboarding Flow (Day 0-7)

### Day 0: Purchase

**Immediate (automated):**
- Thank you page with welcome video (60-90 seconds)
- Receipt email with Skool access link
- Onboarding form link (must complete before first call)

**Welcome Video Script:**
> "You're in. Here's what happens next: You'll get an email with access to the Skool community. Fill out the onboarding form — takes 5 minutes — and I'll review it before our first call. The goal of the Sprint is simple: by Day 30, you'll have a validated offer, a live funnel, and traffic flowing. Let's go."

### Day 1: Onboarding Form

**What the form captures:**
- What do you do? (brief description of expertise)
- Who do you help? (current ideal client, if any)
- What have you already tried? (courses, funnels, offers)
- What's your biggest stuck point right now?
- What would make this Sprint a success for you?

**Why this matters:**
- Michael reviews before Call 1 — shows up prepared
- Client articulates their situation — creates ownership
- Surfaces the real blockers early

### Day 2: Welcome Message (Personal)

**From Michael in Skool DM:**
> "Hey [Name] — saw your onboarding form. [One specific observation or validation]. Looking forward to Call 1 on [date]. Your first action: post your intro in the community using the prompt in the Welcome thread. See you there."

**Why this matters:**
- Personal touch before first call
- They feel seen, not processed
- Intro post creates community connection

### Day 3: Intro Post Prompt

**Community prompt (pinned in Welcome):**
> "Introduce yourself to the Sprint crew. Tell us:
> 1. What's your expertise? (1 sentence)
> 2. Who do you want to help?
> 3. What's one thing you're hoping to figure out in the next 4 weeks?"

**Why this matters:**
- Public commitment
- Other Sprint members see each other
- Creates accountability

### Day 5: Pre-Call Check-In

**Automated DM or email:**
> "Call 1 is in [X days]. Before we meet, I want you to do ONE thing: Review the AI Offer Extraction lesson in Phase 1 and try Prompt 1 (Zone of Genius Extraction). Don't worry if it's messy — that's what the call is for. Come with your output ready."

**Why this matters:**
- Arrives with something to discuss
- Not starting from zero on the call
- Primes engagement with curriculum

### Day 7: First Call

Sprint officially begins. (See Call Structure below.)

## Weekly Call Structure

**Format:** Group call (all Sprint clients), 60-90 minutes
**Cadence:** Weekly, same day/time
**Platform:** Zoom

### Call Flow

| Segment | Time | What Happens |
|---------|------|--------------|
| **Anchor** | 5 min | "Here's where we are in the roadmap. This week is about [milestone]." |
| **Wins** | 10 min | Quick round: "One win from this week, even if small." |
| **Teaching** | 15 min | One concept tied to this week's milestone |
| **Hot Seats** | 30-40 min | 2-4 clients get live feedback on their work |
| **Next Actions** | 5 min | "By next call, your ONE deliverable is..." |
| **Q&A** | 10 min | Open questions |

### Week-by-Week Focus

**Call 1 (Week 1 Kickoff):**
- Anchor: "This week is EXTRACT. By Call 2, you have a complete offer document."
- Teaching: The 5 AI prompts, zone of genius extraction
- Hot Seats: Review AI prompt outputs, sharpen positioning
- Next Action: Complete offer document using all 5 prompts

**Call 2 (Week 2 Kickoff):**
- Anchor: "This week is VALIDATE. By Call 3, you've had real sales conversations."
- Teaching: DM outreach, the Reconnection script, handling responses
- Hot Seats: Review offer documents, refine ideal client language
- Next Action: Start 5 DM conversations using the scripts

**Call 3 (Week 3 Kickoff):**
- Anchor: "This week is BUILD. By Call 4, your funnel is live."
- Teaching: Landing page structure, checkout setup, order bumps
- Hot Seats: Review DM conversations, adjust offer based on feedback
- Next Action: Funnel live and accepting payments (even if imperfect)

**Call 4 (Week 4 Kickoff + Wrap):**
- Anchor: "This week is LAUNCH. Traffic on, first buyers coming."
- Teaching: Traffic options (warm audience, ads, organic), what to watch
- Hot Seats: Review live funnels, troubleshoot issues
- Next Action: Traffic flowing, first buyers in — or clear plan for next 7 days
- Wrap: "Here's your path forward: [Blueprint → Accelerator]"

## Between-Call Support

### Chat Access

**Where:** Skool community (Sprint members can DM Michael)
**Expectation:** Response within 24 hours (business days)
**Best for:** Quick questions, stuck points, wins to share

**Not for:** Long strategy sessions (save for calls), tech support (that's for tutorials)

### Accountability Check-Ins

**Midweek prompt (Day 3-4 between calls):**

Automated post in Sprint thread:
> "Midweek check-in. Reply with:
> 1. Progress on your ONE deliverable this week
> 2. What's blocking you (if anything)?
> 3. What help do you need before the next call?"

**Why this matters:**
- Surfaces stuck clients before the call
- Creates peer accountability
- Gives Michael signal on who needs attention

## Engagement Tracking

### Status Tiers

| Status | Definition | Action |
|--------|------------|--------|
| **Green** | Active in last 7 days (call attended, posted, or DM'd) | None needed |
| **Orange** | No engagement for 7-13 days | Nudge DM: "Hey [Name], noticed you've been quiet. Everything okay? What's your biggest stuck point right now?" |
| **Red** | No engagement for 14+ days | Direct outreach: "Hey [Name], checking in. You've got [X weeks] left in the Sprint. What would make it worth your time to re-engage?" |

## The "What They Can Ignore" Principle

At each milestone, tell clients what NOT to focus on:

**Week 1 (Extract):**
> "Don't worry about your landing page, your ads, or your email sequence. This week is ONLY about the offer. Get the offer document right — everything else follows."

**Week 2 (Validate):**
> "Don't build your funnel yet. Your job is to have real conversations. The funnel can wait — you need feedback from real people first."

**Week 3 (Build):**
> "Don't obsess over traffic yet. Get the funnel live. It doesn't need to be perfect — it needs to exist. You can optimize once you have data."

**Week 4 (Launch):**
> "Don't scale yet. Your job is to get first buyers and learn. Scaling is for after you've proven the offer converts."

## Deliverables by Milestone

### Week 1: Offer Document

**What "done" looks like:**
- Completed AI prompt outputs (all 5)
- Clear articulation of: zone of genius, ideal client, pain points, story, offer
- Price set (even if it changes later)
- Ready to present to a warm prospect

**Proof of completion:** Post offer document (or summary) in Sprint thread for feedback

### Week 2: Validated Conversations

**What "done" looks like:**
- At least 5 DM conversations started using scripts
- At least 2 real responses with feedback
- Offer adjusted based on what you learned
- (Bonus: First sale or clear buying signal)

**Proof of completion:** Screenshot of DM conversation or summary of feedback received

### Week 3: Live Funnel

**What "done" looks like:**
- Landing page live (even if basic)
- Checkout working and accepting payments
- Order bumps added (or intentionally skipped)
- Thank you / access page set up
- Welcome email sending

**Proof of completion:** Share funnel link in Sprint thread

### Week 4: Traffic Flowing

**What "done" looks like:**
- Traffic source activated (warm audience, ads, or organic)
- At least 50 visits to landing page (or equivalent outreach)
- First buyers OR clear data on what's not converting
- 30-day plan for what's next

**Proof of completion:** Share traffic stats or first buyer screenshot

## Capacity Planning

### Sprint Cohort Size

**Recommended:** 8-12 clients per Sprint
**Why:** Small enough for meaningful hot seats, large enough for peer energy

**Overfull (12+):** Hot seats feel rushed, some clients invisible
**Underfull (<5):** Less peer accountability, feels hollow

### Michael's Time Commitment

| Activity | Time | Frequency |
|----------|------|-----------|
| Weekly call | 90 min | Weekly |
| Call prep (review forms, notes) | 30 min | Weekly |
| DM responses | 30 min/day | Daily |
| Engagement tracking + nudges | 30 min | Weekly |
| **Total per Sprint** | ~8-10 hrs/week | 4 weeks |

## The Guarantee in Action

> "Launch your validated offer in 30 days — or Michael keeps working with you until you do."

**How this works:**
- If a client completes the work but doesn't hit milestones, they get extended support
- "Completes the work" = attended calls, posted deliverables, asked for help
- Doesn't apply to: clients who disappear, didn't engage, or didn't follow the process

**The conversation:**
> "Hey [Name], you put in the work but [X] didn't happen. I'm committed to getting you there. Here's what I suggest: [specific next step]. Let's book a 1:1 call to troubleshoot."

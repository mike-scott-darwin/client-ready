## The Self-Liquidating Funnel: The Goal Before You Scale

**The #1 lesson from Cat Howell:** Your bumps should cover your ad spend. OTOs are profit, not survival.

> "That was the point when I was able to break even on ads with just bumps, no OTOs." — Miles Stutz

**Why This Matters:**

Most coaches treat OTOs as part of their break-even calculation. This is dangerous because:
- OTO conversion rates vary wildly (10-30%)
- You're gambling on upsells to cover ad costs
- One bad week can wipe out your budget
- You can't scale confidently

**The Self-Liquidating Structure:**

| Revenue Source | Role | Target |
|----------------|------|--------|
| Front-end ($47) | Covers ~25% of ad spend | CPA around $100 |
| Bumps ($37 + $67 + $97 = $201) | Covers remaining 75% | 50%+ combined take rate |
| OTOs ($97-$397) | **Pure profit** | 10-20% conversion |
| Backend ($5K) | Scale profit | Email ascension |

**The Math:**

If your CPA is $100 and your front-end + bumps generate $100+ per customer:
- You break even **before** OTOs
- Every OTO sale is profit
- Every backend sale is profit
- You can scale without fear

**The Benchmark:**

- **Combined bump rate:** 50%+ (half of buyers take at least one bump)
- **Target AOV from checkout:** $100+ (front-end + bumps only)
- **Don't scale ads until this is consistent**

**What To Test First (In Order):**

1. **Bump sequence** — Test $97 → $67 → $37 (anchor effect)
2. **Bump copy** — Specific numbers, price anchoring, exclusivity
3. **Bump pricing** — Small adjustments can 2x take rates
4. **Pre-checked vs unchecked** — Test both

**The $300 Rule:**

> "If you spend $300 without a sale, change the snack. Don't optimize — change the offer."

Testing priority when stuck:
1. Offer (biggest impact)
2. Headline
3. Price
4. Design

## The Four-Phase Launch System

### Phase 1: Install Conversion Tracking (15-20 Minutes)

**Why This Matters:** Without proper tracking, Facebook's algorithm is flying blind. It can't optimize your campaigns, build quality audiences, or tell you what's actually working. This is the foundation everything else depends on.

**What You're Installing:**

**Facebook Conversion API (CAPI):**

- Tracks conversions even with iOS privacy updates
- Gives Facebook accurate data to optimize your campaigns
- Enables better custom audience creation
- Future-proofs your tracking against privacy changes

**How To Set It Up:**

1. Access your Facebook Events Manager
2. Connect Conversion API through your funnel platform (GoHighLevel, ClickFunnels, etc.)
3. Install Facebook Pixel on all funnel pages (landing page, checkout, thank you page)
4. Configure standard events: ViewContent, AddToCart, Purchase
5. Test with Facebook Pixel Helper Chrome extension
6. Verify data quality score shows "Good" or "Great"

**Success Check:**

- [ ] Pixel firing on all pages
- [ ] Test purchase tracked correctly
- [ ] Events showing in Events Manager
- [ ] Data quality score is green

**Pro Tip:** Let your tracking run for 24-48 hours and collect some initial data before creating custom audiences. Quality data from the start means better optimization later.

---

### Phase 2: Build Custom Audiences (10-15 Minutes)

**Why Custom Audiences Matter:** Facebook's power isn't just in showing your ads to people — it's in finding people similar to your best customers. Custom audiences are the foundation for scaling profitably.

**The Three Priority Audiences:**

**Audience 1: Website Visitors (If Available)**

- People who visited your site in last 180 days
- Shows buying intent and brand awareness
- Minimum 100+ people needed for lookalikes
- Use for: Building 1% lookalike audiences

**Audience 2: Email Subscribers (If Available)**

- Your existing email list or past customers
- Already engaged with your content
- Highest quality audience for lookalikes
- Use for: Creating your best-performing audiences

**Audience 3: Video/Content Engagers**

- People who engaged with your social content
- Shows interest in your messaging
- Good for initial retargeting
- Use for: Warming up cold traffic

**Creating Lookalike Audiences:**

1. Go to Facebook Ads Manager → Audiences
2. Select your best custom audience (email list or purchasers work best)
3. Create 1% lookalike audience in your primary country
4. Name clearly: "LAL - Email Subscribers - 1% - US"
5. Let populate for 6-24 hours before using

**What If You Don't Have Data?** Don't worry — start with interest targeting (covered in Phase 4). Build your pixel data as ads run, then create lookalikes after you have 100+ website visitors or purchases.

### Phase 3: Create Scroll-Stopping Ad Copy (30-60 Minutes)

**The Two Proven Ad Frameworks:**

**Framework 1: Minimum Viable Ad (Best for Quick Testing)**

Perfect for immediate results with simple, direct messaging.

**Structure:**

- **Element 1:** Bold statement with specific result
- **Element 2:** What they're getting + price point
- **Element 3:** Clear call-to-action

**Example:**

> "MY VIRTUAL ASSISTANT TURNS MY FACEBOOK & INSTAGRAM INBOX INTO 4-6 CLIENTS EVERY SINGLE DAY…
>
> Swipe My DM Script for $7
>
> Learn more"

**Why It Works:**

- Instant clarity on the offer
- Can be created in 5 minutes
- Easy to test multiple variations
- Mobile-optimized short format
- Direct response with clear pricing

**Framework 2: Triple Hook Method (Best for Story-Driven Ads)**

Based on $30,000+ monthly ad spend testing, this structure stops scrolling and compels complete readthrough.

**The Triple Hook Structure:**

**Hook 1 - Premise:** Bold statement with specific numbers
*"This might seem crazy, but my business is making over $7,000 a day..."*

**Hook 2 - Stakes:** Why this matters, what's at risk
*"This isn't normal in the coaching industry"*

**Hook 3 - Twist:** Pattern interrupt challenging conventional wisdom
*"...and virtually nobody knows who I am"*

**Complete Ad Structure:**

1. **Opening:** Triple Hook (first 3-5 seconds stops scrolling)
2. **Body:** Story + Problem + Solution (builds connection and desire)
3. **Close:** Clear CTA with price point and next step

**When To Use Each Framework:**

- **Minimum Viable:** Quick testing, simple offers, warm audiences, immediate results needed
- **Triple Hook:** Compelling story to tell, need education, building brand, complex offers

**Ad Copy Best Practices:**

- Include specific numbers and results
- Add time constraints ("15 minutes," "48 hours")
- Show clear mechanism ("DM Script," "Digital Snack")
- Address objections ("Even if you've never...")
- Keep mobile-friendly (short paragraphs, 2-3 lines max)
- Front-load important info in first 2 lines

### Phase 4: Launch Your Campaign Structure (20-30 Minutes)

**The Proven 1-Campaign, 2-3 Ad Set Structure:**

Based on extensive testing, this structure maximizes performance while keeping setup simple.

**Campaign Setup:**

- **Objective:** Sales/Conversions (Purchase event)
- **Campaign Name:** "[Product Name] - [Date]"
- **Budget Type:** Ad set budgets (not campaign budget optimization initially)

**Ad Set 1: Worldwide Broad Targeting**

- **Budget:** $33/day (if testing $100 total split 3 ways)
- **Audience:** Worldwide, Age 18+
- **Exclusions:** All custom audiences (ensures cold traffic)
- **Advantage+ Audience:** ON (lets Facebook optimize)

**Why Worldwide Works:**

- Larger pool = better optimization
- Lower costs in international markets
- Facebook finds your buyers globally
- Tests offer appeal across markets

**Ad Set 2: Interest Targeting (Tier 1 Countries)**

- **Budget:** $33/day
- **Location:** US, Canada, UK, Australia, New Zealand
- **Interests:** 10-15 relevant to your niche (business coaching, online marketing, lead generation, etc.)
- **Same exclusions** as Ad Set 1

**Ad Set 3: Lookalike Audiences (Only If You Have Data)**

- **Budget:** $34/day
- **Location:** Your primary market (usually US)
- **Audience:** 1% lookalike from best custom audience
- **No interests** (let lookalike do the work)

**The 10-Ad Creative Strategy:**

Each ad set contains 10 ads using this multiplication formula:

- **1 ad copy** (from your chosen framework)
- **2 headlines** (variations on your core promise)
- **5 images** (from Canva templates or simple graphics)
- **= 10 total ads** (5 images × 2 headlines)

**Critical Duplication Strategy (Prevents Account Issues):**

1. Create and publish first ad
2. Wait for approval (15-30 minutes)
3. Duplicate 4 times, changing only the image
4. Wait for approval again
5. Duplicate all 5 ads, changing only the headline
6. Result: 10 ads safely published

**Why This Order Matters:** Publishing too many ads at once can trigger Facebook's review systems and get your account flagged.

## Launch Day Checklist

**Before Turning On Ads:**

- [ ] Conversion API installed and tracking correctly
- [ ] Test purchase completed successfully
- [ ] Custom audiences created (if available)
- [ ] Landing page loads fast on mobile
- [ ] Payment processing working smoothly
- [ ] All ads approved and delivering
- [ ] Budget set conservatively ($5-10/day minimum per ad set)

**First 24-48 Hours — What To Monitor:**

- **Ad Approval Status:** Ensure all ads are delivering
- **Cost Per Click:** Should be $0.50-$2.00 depending on niche
- **Click-Through Rate:** 1%+ is good, 2%+ is great
- **Purchase Events:** Verify tracking is working correctly
- **Cost Per Purchase:** Compare to your profit margins

**What NOT To Do:**

- Don't make changes during first 3-5 days (learning phase)
- Don't panic if first day shows no sales
- Don't increase budgets more than 10-20% at a time
- Don't add too many interests at once
- Don't judge performance before spending 2-3x your product price

## Scaling Protocol: From $100/Day to $500/Day+

**Week 1: Testing Phase**

- Let all ad sets run without changes
- Identify winning ads (lowest cost per purchase)
- Kill ads spending 3x product price with no sales
- Keep winners running

**Week 2: Initial Scaling**

- Increase budget 10-20% daily on winning ad sets
- Create 2-3 new ad variations based on winners
- Test new interest combinations
- Monitor cost per purchase closely

**Week 3+: Aggressive Scaling**

- Scale winning campaigns to $100-200/day per ad set
- Launch additional campaigns with new creatives
- Build lookalike audiences from purchasers
- Implement retargeting for abandoned carts

**Success Metrics:**

- **Good:** 1.5x ROAS (return on ad spend)
- **Great:** 2.5x-3x ROAS
- **Excellent:** 4x+ ROAS

## Common Launch Mistakes That Kill Profitability

**Mistake #1: No Tracking Installed**
Running ads without Conversion API means Facebook can't optimize. You're burning money with no data to improve.

**Mistake #2: Too High Initial Budget**
Starting at $100+/day before validating your offer overwhelms the algorithm and wastes money. Start at $10-20/day per ad set.

**Mistake #3: Making Changes Too Soon**
Adjusting campaigns during the 3-5 day learning phase resets optimization and prevents Facebook from finding your buyers.

**Mistake #4: Only Testing One Ad**
With one ad, you can't identify what's working. Always test multiple variations simultaneously.

**Mistake #5: Ignoring Mobile Optimization**
80% of traffic is mobile. If your ad or landing page doesn't work perfectly on mobile, you're losing most of your sales.

**Mistake #6: Relying on OTOs to Break Even**
If you need OTOs to cover your ad spend, you can't scale safely. OTO conversion rates vary wildly (10-30%). One bad week tanks your budget. **Your bumps should cover your CPA.** OTOs are profit, not survival. Don't scale until checkout AOV (front-end + bumps) is consistently $100+.

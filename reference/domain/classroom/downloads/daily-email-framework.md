# The Daily Email Framework

**One email. Every day. To buyers only.**

---

## The Structure

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                         │
│   STORY (60%)                           │
│   Personal experience, client story,    │
│   lesson learned, something that        │
│   happened today                        │
│                                         │
├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                         │
│   OFFER (30%)                           │
│   One product from your ladder          │
│   Rotate through the week               │
│                                         │
├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                         │
│   CTA (10%)                             │
│   Single, clear action                  │
│   One link. One instruction.            │
│                                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

## Story Ideas (When You're Stuck)

| Source | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Client win | "Sarah DM'd me yesterday..." |
| Your mistake | "I screwed this up last week..." |
| Skool question | "Someone asked me in the community..." |
| Personal life | "My kid said something that made me think..." |
| Industry observation | "I saw a coach post this and cringed..." |
| Behind the scenes | "Here's what my morning looks like..." |

---

## What NOT to Write

- Listicles ("5 tips for...")
- Generic advice with no story
- Multiple CTAs ("Click here OR reply OR...")
- 2000-word essays (aim for 200-400 words)
- Emails without an offer

---

## The Math

| Frequency | Touchpoints/Year | Result |
|-----------|------------------|--------|
| Monthly | 12 | Forgotten |
| Weekly | 52 | Baseline |
| **Daily** | **365** | **Top of mind** |

---

## Time Budget: 15-20 Minutes

1. **Notice** (morning) — What happened? What did you learn?
2. **Write** (15 min) — Story → Offer → CTA
3. **Send** (same time daily) — Build reader habit

---

## The Rule

**Story first. Offer second. Always.**

People buy from people they know. Daily stories = they know you.

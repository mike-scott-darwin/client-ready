# No-Phone Offer Checklist

**Evaluate any offer against the 4 principles**

---

## The 4 Principles

A No-Phone Offer sells without a sales call because it answers every question before someone buys.

Use this checklist to evaluate your offer.

---

## Principle 1: Clear Outcome + Timeframe

**The question:** "What exactly will I have when this is done, and when will I have it?"

| Check | Item |
|-------|------|
| [ ] | Outcome is specific, not vague ("validated funnel" not "business growth") |
| [ ] | Timeframe is concrete ("8 weeks" not "ongoing support") |
| [ ] | Result is measurable or tangible (they'll know when they have it) |
| [ ] | Promise matches what you can actually deliver |

**Good:** "Validated, profitable funnel generating customers in 8 weeks"
**Bad:** "Transform your business and achieve your goals"

## Principle 2: Clear Process

**The question:** "What exactly happens after I pay?"

| Check | Item |
|-------|------|
| [ ] | Week-by-week or phase-by-phase breakdown exists |
| [ ] | They can visualize the journey from start to finish |
| [ ] | No mystery about what work happens when |
| [ ] | Process matches the timeframe (8 weeks = 8 steps) |

**Good:** "Week 1: Extraction. Week 2: Validation. Week 3: Funnel. Week 4: Traffic..."
**Bad:** "We'll work together to figure out what you need"

## Principle 3: Clear Delivery

**The question:** "What exactly do I get access to?"

| Check | Item |
|-------|------|
| [ ] | Number of calls is specific (3 calls, not "calls as needed") |
| [ ] | Access channels are named (Slack, Loom, email) |
| [ ] | Deliverables are listed (templates, docs, recordings) |
| [ ] | Response times are set (24-hour Slack response) |
| [ ] | Duration is bounded (8 weeks, not "lifetime access") |

**Good:** "3x 60-minute calls, unlimited Loom reviews, Slack access for 8 weeks, all templates included"
**Bad:** "Unlimited support and access to everything"

## Principle 4: Easy to Start

**The question:** "What happens immediately after I pay?"

| Check | Item |
|-------|------|
| [ ] | First step is automatic (confirmation email, form, calendar link) |
| [ ] | Time to first interaction is short (48 hours, not "we'll reach out") |
| [ ] | No ambiguity about what they do next |
| [ ] | Low friction first action (fill a form, not "prepare materials") |

**Good:** "Pay → Onboarding form → First call scheduled within 48 hours"
**Bad:** "Pay → We'll be in touch soon to discuss next steps"

## Scoring

Count your checkmarks:

| Score | Assessment |
|-------|------------|
| 16/16 | Ready for No-Phone |
| 12-15 | Almost there — fix the gaps |
| 8-11 | Needs work — consider adding a call option |
| <8 | Not ready — use call-based selling for now |

---

## Red Flags (Needs a Call)

Even if you score well, you may still need calls if:

- [ ] Offer requires significant customization per client
- [ ] Price is above $5K (trust gap is higher)
- [ ] Traffic is cold (no prior relationship)
- [ ] Industry has high skepticism/past bad experiences
- [ ] Offer is new/unproven (you need feedback)

---

## The Test

**Can someone read your offer page and buy without asking questions?**

If yes → No-Phone works
If no → Find out what questions they have and answer them on the page

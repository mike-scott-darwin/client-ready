# Codification Audience

**AI-literate business owners and operators who've tried AI, seen it underdeliver, and suspect the problem is context — not capability.**

---

## Primary Audience: Business Owners ($100K–$500K+ Revenue)

### Who They Are

- Running established businesses (not idea-stage)
- Already using AI tools (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini) — but not getting the ROI they expected
- Have real expertise and institutional knowledge worth codifying
- Non-developers. They don't want to build tools. They want a system.
- Revenue: $100K–$500K+/year (enough to justify $147–$497/month)
- Team size: 1–15 people

### What They've Tried

- ChatGPT custom instructions (too shallow, doesn't compound)
- Custom GPTs (platform-locked, no real context)
- AI writing tools (Jasper, Copy.ai) — generic outputs, voice mismatch
- Prompt packs and courses (used once, abandoned)
- Hired AI consultants ($5K–$20K, got a report, nothing changed)
- Free-tier AI (works for basic tasks, fails for anything requiring business context)

### What They Believe

- AI is powerful but it doesn't "get" their business
- There must be a better way than re-explaining everything every session
- Their business knowledge is valuable and shouldn't be locked in their head
- They're falling behind competitors who've figured out AI
- "Context" matters more than "prompts" — they sense this even if they can't articulate it

### What They Fear

- Being left behind as competitors adopt AI more effectively
- Key employees leaving and taking institutional knowledge with them
- Spending more money on AI tools that don't deliver
- Their expertise becoming commoditized by generic AI outputs
- Building dependency on a platform that could change or disappear

### What Keeps Them Stuck

- **No methodology.** They know AI can help, but they don't know how to bridge "I have expertise" to "AI uses my expertise."
- **Tool overwhelm.** New AI tools every week. Each promises to solve everything. None do.
- **Generic outputs.** Every AI output sounds like every other business. Their unique voice, expertise, and perspective are lost.
- **No compounding.** Each session starts from zero. Nothing they do with AI today makes tomorrow's session better.
- **The prompt trap.** They've been told better prompts = better outputs. They've spent hours on prompts. The outputs are still generic.

## Secondary Audience: Marketing Agencies

### Who They Are

- Digital marketing or creative agencies (5–50 people)
- Managing 10–50+ client accounts
- Already using AI for content generation, ad copy, strategy
- Pain: client context dies after the onboarding document

### Why They Buy

- **The brief problem:** Every client has a brand brief. It's a static document from month 1. By month 3, it's outdated. By month 6, nobody reads it. Every new team member asks the same questions.
- **The quality problem:** AI outputs don't sound like the client. They sound like generic marketing. The agency spends hours editing AI outputs to match client voice.
- **The scaling problem:** Adding clients means adding account managers. Revenue scales, but so does headcount. Codification lets them scale quality without scaling people.
- **The retention problem:** When an account manager leaves, client context leaves with them. Codified knowledge stays.

### What They Want

- Every client's identity codified in structured reference files
- AI outputs that sound like the client from the first draft
- New team members who can generate client-quality work on day 1
- A system that gets smarter the longer they work with a client

## Tertiary Audiences

### Fractional CMOs & Consultants

- Juggle 3–7 clients simultaneously
- Need context switching without quality loss
- Each client's reference stack lets them drop in and out seamlessly
- Codification is a sellable deliverable: "I'll codify your brand before I leave"

### Course Creators & Knowledge Entrepreneurs

- Their IP is being commoditized by AI generating similar content
- Need to codify what makes their methodology unique
- Reference files turn their expertise into defensible, AI-powered outputs
- The system generates course content, emails, social — all in their voice

### Professional Services (Law, Accounting, Consulting)

- Institutional knowledge problem: senior partners retire, expertise disappears
- Client knowledge fragmented across emails, documents, conversations
- Regulated industries need consistent, accurate outputs (reference files provide guardrails)
- 6% of small law firms have implemented AI (massive opportunity)

### Venture-Backed Startups (Seed to Series B)

- Scaling fast, losing institutional knowledge faster
- New hires can't absorb company knowledge quickly enough
- Need AI to understand their product, market, and positioning deeply
- Codification becomes part of the onboarding infrastructure

## The Buyer Journey

### Awareness → Lead Magnet

**Trigger:** LinkedIn post, newsletter, or ad about "context > prompts" or "95% of AI pilots fail"

**They think:** "That's exactly what's happening to me. AI gives me generic garbage. Maybe it's not the AI — maybe it's how I'm using it."

**Action:** Download Reference Stack Starter Kit (4 template files)

### Lead Magnet → Workshop

**Trigger:** They try the templates. They see a difference. But filling them out properly is hard. They want guidance.

**They think:** "These files actually changed the output quality. But I need help extracting what matters from my business. I can't just fill in blanks."

**Action:** Buy the workshop ($197)

### Workshop → Sprint

**Trigger:** Workshop gives them 4 core files. They see the potential. But building the full system — research layer, decision framework, content strategy, automation — is beyond DIY.

**They think:** "I see it now. But I need someone to build this with me. I don't want to spend 3 months figuring out the architecture."

**Action:** Enroll in sprint ($2,500 individual / $5,000 agency)

### Sprint → License

**Trigger:** Sprint builds their complete reference stack. They're generating outputs. The system works. But the engine keeps improving — new skills, new templates, community patterns.

**They think:** "I can't go back to doing this manually. The engine updates, the community insights, the new templates — I need to stay connected."

**Action:** Subscribe to license ($147/mo solo / $497/mo agency)

## The Language They Use

### What they say to themselves:
- "I've been using ChatGPT for a year and it still doesn't know my business"
- "Every output sounds the same — like everyone else's AI content"
- "I keep copy-pasting the same context into every conversation"
- "My team uses AI differently and inconsistently"
- "I spent $10K on an AI consultant and got a PowerPoint"

### What they search for:
- "how to make AI understand my business"
- "AI for business owners not developers"
- "why AI outputs sound generic"
- "best AI setup for small business"
- "knowledge management AI"
- "how to train AI on my business" (misnomer — they mean codify, not fine-tune)

### What they respond to:
- Before/after comparisons (same prompt, with and without reference files)
- "Stop prompting, start codifying"
- "Your AI doesn't know your business"
- "95% of AI pilots fail — here's why"
- "Context > Prompts"
- System screenshots (they want to SEE the architecture, not just hear about it)

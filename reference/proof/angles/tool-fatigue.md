# The Tool-Fatigue Angle

**Status:** Secondary test. Targets the `audience.md` buyer directly — the AI-literate coach with 200 saved ChatGPT chats, a graveyard of half-finished courses, and nothing shipped.

**Guardrail:** This is a *pain/tool-fatigue* angle, NOT the shelved customer-facing "codify your brain" pitch. Frame the pain (endless buying, never shipping); resolve to the $47 offer that ships one thing. Do not reintroduce codify-as-selling-point language (per 2026-07-01 status update).

---

## The Core Move

Name the graveyard. "A funnel course you never finished. A launch formula you watched once. A folder called 'Start This Monday' dated last year." Then the relief: **you don't have a knowledge problem — you have a never-shipped-anything problem. And the fix isn't a 13th course. It's one offer that actually exists.**

## Why It Works

- **Bullseye for `audience.md`.** Tool tourism, prompt hoarding, "50 best prompts that don't work," "built 3-5 custom GPTs, none connected." This buyer is described almost verbatim in the audience file.
- **The block is building, not knowing.** The insight that lands: the knowledge was never the bottleneck — shipping was. AI removes the "staring at a blank page" excuse.
- **Anti-consumption is contrarian.** In a market that sells the next course, "you don't need another course" earns trust.

## The Reframe (false-cause → true-cause)

| The story they tell themselves | The true cause |
|---|---|
| "I need to learn more before I launch." | You have more than enough. You've never had a knowledge problem. |
| "The next course/prompt pack will unlock it." | Every purchase is procrastination with a receipt. The block is building, not knowing. |
| "I'm too overwhelmed to start." | Overwhelm has nothing to grab onto once the AI does the building. Answer 5 questions → the offer exists. |

## Hooks

### Short (ads, static image headline)
- "You don't need a 13th prompt pack. You need one offer that exists."
- "Your hard drive is a graveyard of courses you never finished."
- "I've bought every prompt pack. I still couldn't explain what I sell. Here's what fixed it."
- "The block was never knowing. It was building."

### Medium (caption, email)
- "12 courses. 200 saved ChatGPT chats. Three custom GPTs that don't talk to each other. And still — no offer you can actually sell. You don't have a knowledge problem. You have a never-shipped problem. Five prompts, one afternoon, one offer that exists. That's the whole fix."

### Long (VSL / story)
- "I told the truth for once: I'd bought everything and shipped nothing. Every time I sat down to build my offer, I'd open twelve tabs and close the laptop. The block was never the knowing — I knew plenty. It was the building. So I stopped buying and answered five questions honestly. The AI did the arranging. An hour later I had one clear offer — not a 13th course, not another folder of prompts. One thing I could put in front of a real person that afternoon."

## Awareness Level

**Solution aware, fatigued.** They've bought solutions before and been burned. Lead with the honest naming of the graveyard, not a new promise — they're allergic to promises.

## Proof (honest, no income claims)

- Real product screenshots: the 5 prompts running, the finished offer doc — proof that this one ships something, unlike the graveyard.
- Michael's own "6 months building a course nobody bought" story (`voice.md` Pattern 7) — a shipped-the-wrong-thing confession that earns the anti-consumption stance.

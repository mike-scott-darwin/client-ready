## Style Patterns

### Tone
- Casual, conversational, "bro" energy
- Strategic profanity (f*ck, sh*t) for pattern interrupt
- Anti-guru positioning - calls out industry BS
- Personal stories + vulnerability
- Direct, no fluff

### Structure
- **Short paragraphs** (1-3 sentences max)
- **Heavy use of bullet points** and emojis
- **PS always used** - often restates offer or adds urgency
- **One clear CTA** - usually "comment on this post" or "reply to email"
- Length: 300-600 words typical

### Subject Lines
| Type | Example |
|------|---------|

| Urgency | "3 spots left (get 10k+ in bonuses TODAY)..." |
| Personal | "bro, I just need something that f*cking works..." |
| Question | "would you sign this?" |

## Persuasion Framework

### 1. Enemy/Problem Identification
- **The enemy:** 8-12 week bloated programs, "nurturing", big competitors
- **Pain amplification:** suburban strip mall, day job, broke coaches
- Positions against: "common advice", gurus, "trust the process"

### 2. Contrarian Hook
Every email opens with a contrarian take:
- "When did 8-12 weeks become standard practice?"
- "Your coaching skillset does NOT entitle you to clients"
- "Nurturing your market comes at insane cost"
- "Reciprocity is dead"

### 3. Solution Positioning
- **Simple** - 45 days vs 3-4 months
- **Lean** - no team, no ads, no funnels
- **Profitable** - 80-90% margins, cash collected
- **Fast** - results in weeks not months

### 4. Social Proof Patterns
- Specific numbers: "$60k in 30 days", "40 people already"
- Name drops: Traffic & Funnels, Cole Gordon
- Client results with names: "Ege went from $14k to $40k"
- Scarcity: "3 of 9 spots taken"

### 5. Risk Reversal
- 14-day unconditional guarantee
- "We won't ask for homework"
- "If we don't help DOUBLE your net income - YOU DON'T PAY"
- Respects buyer as adult, not student

## What to Mirror for Your Emails

### Do This
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- Contrarian opener that challenges common advice
- Specific numbers and timeframes
- Name your enemy clearly
- PS that restates the offer
- Single, clear CTA
- Casual tone without being unprofessional

### Avoid
- Long walls of text
- Multiple CTAs
- Generic "valuable content"
- Soft, non-committal language
- Over-polished corporate tone

## Template Structure

```
[Contrarian hook - challenge common belief]

[Short story or example that proves the point]

[Bullet points showing the problem with "common way"]

[Introduce your solution as the alternative]

[Specific benefits with numbers]

[Social proof - names, results, scarcity]

[Single CTA - engage, don't just click]

[Sign off with personality]

PS. [Restate offer + urgency]
```
